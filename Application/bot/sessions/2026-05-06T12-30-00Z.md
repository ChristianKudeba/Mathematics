## Result

**Quantitative leading constant.**
$$c_1 = H(1) \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} = 0.8681354129 \pm 2 \cdot 10^{-7}.$$
Where $H$ is the explicit Euler product
$H(s) = (1 - 4^{-s}) \prod_{p\equiv 1(4)}(1 - 3p^{-2s} + 2p^{-3s})
\prod_{p\equiv 3(4)}(1 - p^{-2s})$, analytic on $\Re s > 1/2$, with
$G(s) = \sum_{d sf}\rho(d) d^{-s} = \zeta_K(s) H(s)$ ($K = \mathbb Q(i)$).

**Rigor status.** The small-$e$ Tauberian piece gives the rigorous lower bound
$T(N) \ge H(1)\pi/4 \cdot N \log N + O(N)$. The upper bound $\le H(1)\pi/2
\cdot N\log N$ (the doubling) is heuristic-supported by:

1. The radical identity $\tau^*(d) = \tau(\mathrm{rad}(d))$, Hooley's
   identity applies to $\mathrm{rad}(n^2+1)$.
2. Empirical: $T(N)/(N\log N)$ descends monotonically from $1.034$ (N=500)
   to $0.954$ (N=$10^5$); residual $(T - c_1 N L)/N \approx 0.99 \pm 0.03$
   stable across 8 N.

A 0.01 shift in $c_1$ would induce a $\Delta \cdot \log N \approx 0.115$
drift in the residual across $N \in [500, 10^5]$, easily visible against the
$\pm 0.03$ scatter — so empirically $c_1 = 0.868 \pm 0.001$.

**Application to $B_3^{\rm bdy}$.** By the exact decomposition
$B_3^{\rm bdy} = T + P - NK$, leading term matches. Empirical
$B_3^{\rm bdy}/(N\log N)$ peaks $\approx 0.9485$ around
$N \in [10^4, 3 \cdot 10^4]$, descends to $0.9451$ at $N=10^5$. Asymptote:
$c_1 = 0.8681$, NOT $3/\pi = 0.955$.

**Falsifiable forecast.** $B_3^{\rm bdy}/(N\log N)$ at $N = 10^6$: predicted
$\in [0.93, 0.94]$. At $N = 10^8$: $\in [0.91, 0.92]$. Asymptote $0.8681$.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The algebraic identity $G = \zeta_K H$ and the numerical $H(1) = 0.5527$ are
solid. The empirical case for $c_1 = 0.8681$ (and against $3/\pi$) is
convincing across 8 N. **Documented soft spots:**

(a) The "factor of 2 doubling" in the Selberg–Delange derivation is not
    fully rigorous for $\tau^*$ — the radical-identity reduction was sketched
    but the boundary argument analog of Hooley 1957 §3 was not written out.
(b) Secondary constant $c_0^T$ is empirical $\approx 0.99$, not derived in
    closed form (would need $H'(1)$ and the next Laurent coefficient of $\zeta_K$).
(c) $P(N)/N \to ?$ and the conjectured $P_\infty = K_\infty$ is suggested
    by $K_N - P/N \to 0$ but not proven.
(d) "Turning-point" reading of $B_3^{\rm bdy}/(N\log N)$ at $N=10^5$ is one
    data point; the stronger evidence is the $T(N)/(N\log N)$ descent.
