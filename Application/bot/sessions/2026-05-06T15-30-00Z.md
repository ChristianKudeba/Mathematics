   $H'(1)/H(1) = (\log 4)/3 + \sum_{p \equiv 1(4)} \frac{6\log p (p^{-2}-p^{-3})}{1 - 3p^{-2}+2p^{-3}} + \sum_{p \equiv 3(4)} \frac{2\log p}{p^2-1} = 1.5119$.
   Hence $H'(1) = 0.83558$.

2. First attempt (`bot/scratch/c0T-closed-form.py`): naive doubling

3. Second attempt (`bot/scratch/c0T-careful.py`, `c0T-validate.py`): split

4. Tested closed form for $T_<$: by Mellin–Perron on $G(s+1)/s$,
   $$\sum_{e \text{ sf}, e \le X} \rho(e)/e = R H(1) \log X + (R H'(1) + \gamma_K H(1)) + O((\log X)^{-A}).$$

## Result

**Closed form for the diagonal half of the secondary Selberg–Delange constant:**
$$c_<^\infty := \lim_{N\to\infty} \big(T_<(N)/N - (\pi/4) H(1) \log N\big) = (\pi/4) H'(1) + \gamma_K H(1) = 1.013429.$$

This is HALF of $c_0^T$ (the diagonal piece $T_<(N) := \sum_{e\,\mathrm{sf},\,e \le N} \#\{n \le N: e | n^2+1\}$).

**Empirical for the off-diagonal half:** $c_>^\infty \approx -0.029$ stable
across $N \in [10^4, 10^5]$.

**Combined:** $c_0^T = c_<^\infty + c_>^\infty \approx 1.013 - 0.029 = 0.984$,
matching the prev session's empirical $0.99 \pm 0.03$.

**Falsifiable forecast.** At $N = 10^6$: predicted $T_<(N)/N - a_1 \log N \in
[1.012, 1.014]$ and $T_>(N)/N - a_1 \log N \in [-0.035, -0.025]$. Hence
$T(N)/(N \log N)$ at $N = 10^6$ predicted $\in [0.945, 0.948]$ (vs prev
session's predicted band $[0.93, 0.94]$ — slightly tighter).

**Documented soft spots:**

(a) The "limit-rigorous" status for $c_<^\infty$ depends on an Erdős–Hooley
    equidistribution bound on $\sum_{e \le N}\sum_i \{(N - r_i)/e\}$ that is
    standard in the literature but not written out in this note.
(b) Pointwise fluctuation $O(\sqrt N \log^c N)$ is heuristic.
(c) $c_>^\infty$ is empirical only — closed form requires a non-sf correction
    sum (the Hooley boundary problem for $\tau^*$ on $n^2+1$).

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

Three of four skeptic round-1 issues fully resolved; the fourth resolved at
the limit-claim level with the unwritten Erdős–Hooley equidistribution input
flagged. The closed form $c_<^\infty = (\pi/4) H'(1) + \gamma_K H(1) = 1.0134$
is supported by numerical agreement to $10^{-4}$ at $X = 10^6$ on
$\sum_{e \,\mathrm{sf},\,e\le X}\rho(e)/e$ (the underlying Lemma 3.1).

   $$\Delta_{nsf}(N) = \sum_{n: n^2+1 \text{ not sf}} \#\{e \,\mathrm{sf} \mid n^2+1: \sqrt{\mathrm{rad}(n^2+1)} < e \le n\}.$$
   Closed form for $\Delta_{nsf}$ (via density $1 - C_0$ and divisor-distribution
   in log scale) would close $c_0^T$ entirely. Heuristic estimate $\approx 0.10 N$
   matches empirical gap.

3. **(Multi-session, the genuine bottleneck) Off-diagonal asymptotic

4. **Banked methodological lesson:** when applying SD-style closed forms to
   $\sum_n f(n^2+1)$, the diagonal $T_<$ piece (sum $e \le N$) has a clean

   off-diagonal piece $T_>$ requires genuine Hooley analysis and is comparable
