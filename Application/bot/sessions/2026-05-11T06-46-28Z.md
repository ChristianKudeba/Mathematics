   **Theorem 4 (Unique-prime refinement).** For every $(D3)$-tuple $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4$ satisfying $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$ and every prime $p$ with $|S_p((e_i))| = 1$ (say $S_p = \{i_0\}$): $p \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_{i_0} \equiv -N \pmod p$. *Proof.* Reduce $(\star\star) := \sum_i s_i L_i (N + a_i) \equiv 0 \pmod M$ modulo $p$. Since $e_i$ are squarefree, the $p$-part of $M$ is $p$, so $p \mid L_j$ for $j \notin S_p$ (terms vanish), and $L_{i_0}$ is a unit mod $p$ (term survives). Forces $a_{i_0} \equiv -N \pmod p$. Squaring with $a_{i_0}^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{e_{i_0}}$ gives $p \mid N^2 + 1$.

   **Corollary 7 (Structural decomposition).** Every $(D3)$-tuple decomposes uniquely as $e_i = e_i^{(1)} \cdot e_i^{(\ge 2)}$, where $e_i^{(1)} := \prod_{p \in \mathrm{supp}(e_i):\,|S_p|=1} p$. The $(e_i^{(1)})$ form a pairwise-coprime 4-tuple of divisors of $N^2+1$ (pairwise coprimality from $|S_p|=1$ definition); $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$. The $(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ are described by a shared-prime structure $\sigma$: a finite collection of disjoint sets $(\mathcal{P}_S)_{|S| \ge 2}$ of sf-good primes. The shared $a_i^{(\ge 2)}$ satisfy a joint linear constraint $(\diamond_p)$ for each shared prime $p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}$.

   **Theorem 5 (Per-shared-prime fiber bound).** For $|S_p| = k \ge 2$ and $p$ odd, $(\diamond_p)$ — viewed as an affine-linear equation in $(\eta_{p,i})_{i \in S_p} \in \{\pm 1\}^k$ — has at most $2^{k-1}$ solutions. *Proof.* Fix any $i_* \in S_p$; for each choice of the remaining $k-1$ signs, the equation determines $\eta_{p, i_*} \in \mathbb{F}_p$ uniquely; at most one of $\{+1, -1\}$ matches (since $+1 \not\equiv -1 \pmod p$ for odd $p$). $2^{k-1}$ total.

   **Corollary 8 (Counting formula).** Given fixed $\sigma$ and fixed $(e_i^{(1)})$, the number of $(D3)$-tuples with this data is $\le \prod_{p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}} 2^{|S_p| - 1}$ (independence across distinct shared primes via CRT on $e_i^{(\ge 2)}$).
