## Result

**Two empirical findings:**

1. **F1, F2 forecasts confirmed.** $|E(10^7)|/\sqrt N = 0.206$ (in $[0.05, 0.5]$);
   $|E(10^7)|/N = 6.5\!\cdot\!10^{-5}$ (well below $10^{-3}$).

2. **Direct empirical evidence for the analytic input the proof needs.** The
   bound $|T_h(N)| \le c \sqrt N$ holds with $c \le 1.222$ uniformly across
   43 sampled $h$ values up to $h = 7560$ at $N = 10^7$. The empirical scaling
   matches the natural iid CLT prediction (mean $\approx 0.66 \sqrt N$, max
   $\approx 1.30 \sqrt N$ heuristically). This is **far stronger than what the
   proof requires** — the proof needs only $T_h(N) = o(N)$ uniform in $h \le H(N)$
   for *some* $H(N) \to \infty$.

**Implication for the Fourier reduction.** §5 of the new note rigorously
combines main-term and remainder bounds in the Vaaler decomposition, showing:
*if* $|T_h(N)| \le c\sqrt N$ uniformly in $h \le H(N)$ with $\log H = o(\sqrt N)$,
*then* $\Psi(N) = o(N)$ and hence Lemma 3.4 holds. The data of §3-§4 supports
the hypothesis with substantial headroom (we have $\sqrt N$, the proof needs
$o(N)$).

**Rigor accounting unchanged.** Lemma 3.4 still depends on the un-rigorized
uniform bound. This session adds *empirical evidence at one $N$ over many $h$*
and *empirical evidence at many $N$ over a few $h$*. The cross-quantification
*uniform in $h$ as $N \to \infty$* is not directly tested.

   data does not rule out arithmetic resonances we haven't probed.
3. **Iid CLT match (§7) is "consistent with", not "predicted by"** — distinct
   $|T_h|$ are not iid since they share the underlying $(e, r_i)$ set.
