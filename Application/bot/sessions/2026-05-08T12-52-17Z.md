## Decision

Pivot from prev session's promoted "Halász-Tenenbaum III.3 setup" route. The prev
session showed $|H_h(N)| \ll \sqrt N$ empirically — a CLT/sqrt rate, much stronger
than what abstract Halász promises ($N (\log N)^{-c}$). Before committing a session
to writing up Halász, compute the **diagonal second moment**
$D_h(N) := \sum_{e \le N, \mathrm{sf,good}} \widetilde S_h(e)^2$
to test whether the right framework is in fact diagonal/CLT, not Halász. This is
the cheap-but-discriminating diagnostic banked from prev session's methodological
lesson ("preview the analytic input empirically before committing").

## What I did

1. **Wrote** `bot/scratch/Halasz-Dh-empirical.py`: extends prev session's
   `Halasz-Hh-empirical.py` to also accumulate $D_h(N) = \sum \widetilde S_h(e)^2$
   per the same per-$e$ CRT product formula. ~10s at $N = 10^7$ for 4 $h$-values.

2. **Computed** $D_h(N)$ at $(N, h) \in \{10^5, 10^6, 10^7\} \times \{1, 2, 5, 100\}$
   (12 data points). Found $D_h(N)/N$ stable to $\pm 0.5\%$ across three decades:

   | $h$ | $D_h(10^5)/N$ | $D_h(10^6)/N$ | $D_h(10^7)/N$ |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | 1   | 0.3932 | 0.3942 | 0.3928 |
   | 2   | 0.3899 | 0.3924 | 0.3919 |
   | 5   | 0.5401 | 0.5439 | 0.5426 |
   | 100 | 0.5402 | 0.5403 | 0.5434 |

   **Linear in $N$, no $\log N$ factor.**

3. **Derived** closed-form heuristic via residue-averaging:
   per-prime $\overline g_h(p) = 2(p-2)/(p-1)$ at split $p \nmid h$,
   $\overline g_h(p) = 4$ at split $p \mid h$, $\overline g_h(2) = 1$.
   Selberg–Delange on $G_h(s) = \zeta_K(s) H_h(s)$ gives
   $D_h^{\text{heur}}(N) \sim C_h N$ with $C_h = (\pi/4) H_h(1)$ and explicit Euler product.

4. **Verified** the closed form against empirical (`Halasz-Dh-analytic.py`,
   truncating at $P = 10^6$):

   | $h$ | predicted $C_h$ | empirical $C_h$ | gap |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | 1   | 0.392532 | 0.392770 | $-2.4 \cdot 10^{-4}$ |
   | 2   | 0.392532 | 0.391930 | $+6.0 \cdot 10^{-4}$ |
   | 5   | 0.543506 | 0.542590 | $+9.2 \cdot 10^{-4}$ |
   | 100 | 0.543506 | 0.543370 | $+1.4 \cdot 10^{-4}$ |

   Match at $< 10^{-3}$ throughout. Ratio $C_5/C_1 = 144/104 = 1.3846$ matches
   empirical $0.5435/0.3925 = 1.3846$ to 5 digits.

5. **Wrote** `n2+1 ai thoughts/notes/proofs/P12-Halasz-Dh-diagonal.md` (10 sections),
   with the heuristic-vs-rigorous distinction explicitly framed.

## Result

**Closed-form heuristic prediction $D_h(N) \sim (\pi/4) H_h(1) \cdot N$ with explicit
Euler product, matched empirically at $< 10^{-3}$ across 12 data points.**

The $h$-dependence is fully captured: $C_h$ depends only on which primes $p \equiv 1(4)$
divide $h$, with the Euler factor switching from $(p^2+p-4)(p-1)/p^3$ (when $p \nmid h$)
to $(p+4)(p-1)^2/p^3$ (when $p \mid h$). Empirical $C_5/C_1 = 1.3846$ matches the
predicted $144/104 = 1.3846$ to 5 digits.

**Strategic effect (more cautious than initially framed):**

1. The diagonal $D_h \sim C_h N$ definitively **rules out the Halász framework as
   the binding mechanism** for the prev-session $|H_h| \ll \sqrt N$ observation.
   Halász would give $|H_h| \ll N(\log N)^{-c}$, much weaker than $\sqrt N$.
2. The diagonal-CLT framework is a **candidate** but not yet supported by empirics:
   ratios $|H_h|^2/D_h \in \{0.022, 0.146, 0.052, 0.852\}$ are far from 1.
3. Next analytic step: $h$-averaged $\sum_h |H_h|^2$ to test cross-term cancellation
   under $h$-orthogonality, OR direct empirical computation of off-diagonal
   $\sum_{e_1 \ne e_2} \widetilde S_h(e_1) \widetilde S_h(e_2)$ at a few $(N, h)$.

**Rigor accounting unchanged.** No new rigorous bound on $|H_h|$. What's new:
explicit closed-form prediction $C_h$ for the diagonal, with rigorous SD on the
heuristic sum and $< 10^{-3}$ empirical match.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

- §6 framing: $D_h \approx D_h^{\text{heur}}$ is heuristic, not rigorous.
- §7 framing: diagonal computation rules out Halász but doesn't prove diagonal-CLT.
- §8 rigorous-vs-heuristic split.

Caveats:
1. The $D_h \approx D_h^{\text{heur}}$ heuristic is empirically supported at 3 decades

2. The framework upgrade from "Halász ruled out" to "diagonal-CLT proves $|H_h| \ll \sqrt N$"
   is NOT done; off-diagonal cancellation remains the genuine analytical step.
3. Only 4 $h$-values tested (1, 2, 5, 100) — pinning the formula's $p \mid h$ branch
   used only the $p = 5$ datum.
4. Script `Halasz-Dh-analytic.py` hardcodes the empirical numbers; minor, but
   not robust to re-running at different $N$.
