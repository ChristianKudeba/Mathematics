## What I did

1. **Algebraic Laurent expansion.** Derived three Laurent coefficients of $G(s) = \zeta_K(s)^3 H(s)$ at $s=1$:
$$A_3 = R^3 H_0, \quad A_2 = 3R^2 \gamma_K H_0 + R^3 H_1, \quad A_1 = 3R(R\beta_K + \gamma_K^2) H_0 + 3R^2 \gamma_K H_1 + R^3 H_2,$$
where $R = \pi/4$, $\gamma_K, \beta_K$ are the first two non-singular Laurent coefficients of $\zeta_K(s)$ at $s=1$ (Euler-Kronecker-type constants for $\mathbb{Q}(i)$), and $H_j = H^{(j)}(1)/j!$.

2. **Translation to $S(N)$ asymptotic.** Via Perron + partial summation + the formal substitution $\log N^2 = 2\log N$:
$$S(N) \sim c_3 N \log^3 N + c_2 N \log^2 N + c_1 N \log N + c_0 N,$$
with $c_3 = 4A_3/3 = \pi^3 H(1)/48$ (matches last session) and the new closed forms
$$c_2 = 2A_2 = 6 R^2 \gamma_K H(1) + 2 R^3 H'(1), \qquad c_1 = 2 A_1.$$

3. **Closed-form for $\gamma_K, \beta_K$.** Using $\zeta_K = \zeta \cdot L(\cdot, \chi_4)$:
$$\gamma_K = L'(1, \chi_4) + \gamma R, \qquad \beta_K = \tfrac12 L''(1, \chi_4) + \gamma L'(1, \chi_4) - \gamma_1 R$$
($\gamma_1 \approx -0.0728$ first Stieltjes constant).

4. **Numerical evaluation.** $L'(1, \chi_4) \approx 0.19290$ (mpmath; agrees with closed form $(\pi/4)(\gamma + 2\log 2 + 3\log \pi - 4\log \Gamma(1/4))$). $\gamma_K \approx 0.6462$. $\beta_K \approx 0.0915$. $H(1) \approx 0.12324$ via Euler product over primes $\le 10^7$. $H'(1) \approx 0.5934$ via $H(1)\sum_p H_p'/H_p$, with explicit per-prime-class formulas (e.g. $(22p-6)\log p/[(p-1)(p^2+4p-1)]$ for split primes); cross-checked by central finite difference, agree to 6 digits. $H''(1)/2 \approx 0.4534$ via finite difference (3-4 digits trustworthy).

5. **Predictions:** $c_3 \approx 0.0796$ (unchanged), $c_2 \approx 0.8698$, $c_1 \approx 2.143$.

6. **Empirical comparison.** With existing $S(N)$ data at $N \in \{10^3, 10^4, 10^5, 3\!\cdot\!10^5, 10^6\}$: 3-term prediction (no fitting, all from closed forms) gives $S(N)/\text{predicted} \in [1.024, 1.046]$, monotonically improving with $N$. Constrained least-squares fit with $c_3$ pinned at predicted: empirical $c_2 = 0.886$ (within 2% of predicted $0.870$), empirical $c_1 = 2.65$ (vs predicted $2.14$, with the 18% gap absorbed into the unmodeled $c_0$ basis function).
