## Result

**Empirical observations (descriptive, on the tested 42-point grid):**

1. $|H_h(N)|/\sqrt N \le 1.36$ across all $h \in \{1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 10, 13,
   17, 20, 50, 100, 500, 1000, 2000, 5000, 10000\}$, $N \in \{10^6,
   3 \cdot 10^6, 10^7, 2 \cdot 10^7, 3 \cdot 10^7\}$.
2. $\rho_h(N) = H_h(N)^2 / D_h(N) \le 3.39$ across the same grid; max
   attained at $(h, N) = (1000, 10^7)$.
3. $\rho_{100}(N)$ trajectory across $N$:
   $\{2.11, 0.62, 0.85, 0.026, 0.064\}$ — non-monotone, no descent.
4. Horizontal scan at $N = 10^7$: empirical mean $\bar\rho \approx 0.55$,
   median $\approx 0.06$. Both well below the chi-sq(1) iid prediction
   ($1$ and $\approx 0.455$ respectively).

**Three readings:**

- **Pickup-hint question RESOLVED in stable/random direction.** $\rho_h \to 0$
  is refuted by the $h=100$ trajectory. $\rho_h \asymp 1$ (random walk) is the
  consistent reading.
- **"$\rho_h = O(1)$ uniformly" remains consistent** with data, equivalent
  to the strategically-relevant $|H_h(N)| \ll \sqrt N$ uniformly. **NOT
  established** — only fails to be refuted.
- **Empirical mean $\bar\rho < 1$ points to negative off-diagonal correlation.**
  Same direction as `P12-Halasz-h-averaged.md` $R(N, H) < 1$ finding;
  this session adds the fixed-$N$ horizontal-scan version.

**Strategic implications (refined):**

- The right next analytic question is: why is the cross-pair sum
  $H_h(N)^2 - D_h(N) = \sum_{e_1 \ne e_2} \widetilde S_h(e_1) \widetilde S_h(e_2)$
  systematically negative on average? **Same question as
  `P12-Halasz-h-averaged.md`**; this session re-confirms in a different
  averaging direction (over $h$ at fixed $N$, vs cumulative $\sum_{h \le H}$).
- Spectral-no-peak (`P12-Halasz-Phi-d-spectrum.md`) + $h$-averaged
  off-diagonal-negative + this-session fixed-$N$ off-diagonal-negative form
  three independent empirical confirmations of the same picture.

**Rigor accounting:** no new rigorous content. All findings descriptive of
the tested $(N, h)$ grid.
