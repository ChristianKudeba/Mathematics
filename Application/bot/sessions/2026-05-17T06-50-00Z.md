## Skeptic dialogue

**Round 1.** General-purpose subagent. Brief: critique III.2.b on six axes — (a) small regime, (b) large regime, (c) transition regime, (d) phase/magnitude separation, (e) smoothed-test-function admissibility, (f) citation accuracy.

**Round 1 raised 7 CORE + 4 COSMETIC:**

- **CORE 1 (small regime uniformity).** Bessel-power-series leading-term asymptotic $J_{2it}(2z)\sim z^{2it}/\Gamma(1+2it)$ is valid for *fixed* $\nu = 2it$ as $w = 2z\to 0$, not uniform in $t$ for $|t|\le T$. $|\Gamma(1+2it)|\asymp|t|^{1/2}e^{-\pi|t|}$ grows like $e^{\pi|t|}$, blowing up the "leading term" approximation in regions where it must be small.
- **CORE 2 (small regime sign-choice).** Contour shift direction $t\mapsto t-iA$ vs $t+iA$ was selected to land on the desired $r^{2A}$ saving rather than derived. The dimensional accounting "$r^{2+2A}\cdot T^2\ll T^3(\rho/T)^{2A}$" has a hidden $T^{2A}$ factor that doesn't actually absorb.
- **CORE 3 (large regime kernel asymptotic).** Hankel asymptotic at $\nu = 2it$ with $\Re\nu = 0$ produces $\cos(w-it\pi-\pi/4) = \cos(w-\pi/4)\cosh(t\pi) + i\sin(w-\pi/4)\sinh(t\pi)$, growing exponentially in $|t|$. The chunk derives $\sinh(2\pi t)\sinh(4r\sin\theta)/r$ leading term, which **does not cancel** $\sinh(2\pi t)$ in the denominator at $\sin\theta\neq 1/2$.
- **CORE 4 (IBP saving is logarithmic).** The $t$-derivative of the kernel is $O(\log\rho)$ from $z^{\pm 2it}$ factors, not $O(\rho/T)$. Stationary-phase IBP needs a **phase derivative** of size $\rho/T$, not a logarithmic derivative; the claimed $(T/\rho)^N$ saving is unjustified by IBP. The true large-regime decay comes from Mellin–Barnes $\Gamma$-quotient Stirling analysis, not IBP.
- **CORE 5 (transition saddle inconsistency).** Author successively produces $T^{7/2}$, $T^{1/2}$, $T^{3/2}$, selects $T^{3/2}$ by reverse-engineering. The "$T^{-2}$ Stein-Plancherel factor" appears with no derivation. The $\Phi''(t^*)\asymp 1/T$ Hessian claim assumes $J_{2it}(2z)\sim e^{it\Psi(z)}$ with smooth $\Psi$ — not established.

The bound (III.2.b.4) as stated does not hold pointwise; it holds **only after** Mellin–Barnes $\Gamma$-cancellation in BM 2003 §11, which is a different mechanism than the in-chunk $\sinh$-cancellation heuristic.
