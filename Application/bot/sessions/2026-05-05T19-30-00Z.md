## What I did

   \tau(m)^2$ in general, the labeling is wrong. The displayed sum is a partial of
   $S_3 := \sum \tau((n^2+1)^2)$. Note: this is a labeling error, NOT a computational
   error in the prior session's $P^{(2)} = N(\Sigma_*(\sup) - \Sigma_*(\inf))$, which
   is its own valid formal-SD prediction.

2. Derived the analytic structure for $S_3$. By the dual identity
   $\tau(m^2) = \sum_{d|m} 2^{\omega(d)}$, $S_3(N) = \sum_d 2^{\omega(d)} N_d(N)$.
   The relevant generating function is
   $$T_3(s) = \sum_d 2^{\omega(d)} \rho(d)/d^s = (1 + 2^{1-s}) \prod_{p \equiv 1(4)} \frac{1+3 p^{-s}}{1-p^{-s}}.$$
   Showed $T_3(s) = \zeta_K(s)^2 H_3(s)$ where $H_3$ has explicit Euler product
   convergent absolutely on $\Re s > 1/2$:
   - At $p = 2$: $(1+2^{1-s})(1-2^{-s})^2$, value $1/2$ at $s=1$.
   - At $p \equiv 1(4)$: $(1+3p^{-s})(1-p^{-s})^3 = 1 - 6p^{-2s} + 8p^{-3s} - 3p^{-4s}$.
   - At $p \equiv 3(4)$: $(1-p^{-2s})^2$.
   Numerical: $H_3(1) \approx 0.27775$, $H_3'(1) \approx 0.84241$.

3. Two-term Laurent: $\Sigma_3(X) := \sum_{d \le X} 2^{\omega(d)}\rho(d)/d \sim
   b'_2 (\log X)^2/2 + b'_1 \log X$ with $b'_2 = (\pi/4)^2 H_3(1) \approx 0.17133$ and
   $b'_1 = (\pi/4)^2 H_3'(1) + 2(\pi/4) \gamma_K H_3(1) \approx 0.80157$, where
   $\gamma_K \approx 0.6462$ is the next Laurent coefficient of $\zeta_K$ at $s=1$.
   Hence $S_3(N) \sim 2 b'_2 NL^2 + 2 b'_1 NL$.

4. Empirical validation: at $N \in \{3 \cdot 10^4, 10^5\}$, the 2-term Laurent matches
   empirical $S_3$ to within 2-3%. Corroborates the $\zeta_K^2 H_3$ structure.

5. Applied to the partial sum: $W_{K-1} = $ partial $S_3$ on $n \in (n_-, N]$ with
   $n_- = \lfloor\sqrt{\inf - 1}\rfloor$, $\inf = N \cdot 2^{K-1}$. Formal prediction
