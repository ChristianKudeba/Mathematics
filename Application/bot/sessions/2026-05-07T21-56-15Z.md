2. **Derived an exact integer identity for $B(N)$:**
   $$B(N) = U(N) - T(N)/2$$
   where $U(N) := \sum_{n \le N} \#\{e \,\mathrm{sf}\mid n^2+1 : e \le n\}$
   and $T(N) := \sum_n 2^{\omega(n^2+1)}$. Cross-checked at four $N$ values
   $\{10^3, 3 \cdot 10^3, 10^4, 3 \cdot 10^4\}$: $U - T/2 - B = 0$ exactly
   in every case. Reproduces the AB-decomposition note's $B(10^4) = 880$.

3. **Established rigorous SD asymptotic for $U(N)$:**
   $$U(N) = a_1 N \log N + (c_<^\infty - a_1) N + o(N).$$

     $U(N) = N \Sigma_*(N) - \tfrac{1}{2} A(N) - F(N)$ with $A(N)$ over
     $e \ge 2$ and $F(N)$ the same fractional-part sum as Cor 3.2 of
     `P12-c0T-secondary-constant.md`.

## Result

**Existence of $B^\infty$ is now reduced (modulo no new analytic content) to
existence of $c_0^T$**, the secondary asymptotic of $\sum_{n \le N} 2^{\omega(n^2+1)}$.

Specifically:
1. **Pointwise identity** $b(n) = \#\{e \,\mathrm{sf}\mid n^2+1 : e \le n\} - 2^{\omega-1}$
   for $n \ge 1$ (rigorous, computationally verified).
2. **Exact integer identity** $B(N) = U(N) - T(N)/2$.
3. **Rigorous SD asymptotic** $U(N) = a_1 N \log N + (c_<^\infty - a_1) N + o(N)$
   (via SD on $G(s+1)$ for $\Sigma_*$, SD on $G(s)$ for $A$, and the SAME
   Erdős–Hooley fractional-part bound that Cor 3.2 already invokes).
4. **Equivalence theorem:** $\lim B(N)/N$ exists $\iff$ $\lim T(N)/N - c_1 \log N$ exists,
   with $B^\infty = (c_<^\infty - a_1) - c_0^T/2$.

Empirical confirmation: $U(N)/N - a_1 \log N$ approaches predicted
$c_<^\infty - a_1 = 0.579362$, with gap $-1.0 \cdot 10^{-3}$ at $N = 3 \cdot 10^4$.

**The conditional structure** $c_0^T = 1.158725 - 2 B^\infty$ from the
AB-decomposition note now reads: $c_0^T = 1.158725 - 2 B^\infty$ where
$B^\infty$ exists iff $c_0^T$ exists. The closed form is genuinely
"$c_0^T = 1.158725 - 2 B^\infty$ where the constant $B^\infty$ is the value
of one definite limit", with no prior independent existence requirement.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The equivalence is rigorous *modulo* Lemma 3.4 (Erdős–Hooley discrepancy
on $F(N)$). This caveat is identical to the one underlying the predecessor
Cor 3.2 — no new unrigorized step introduced. The caveat is NOT
"$B^\infty$ might not exist"; it is "the standard Halász–Tenenbaum
machinery applied to Hooley-1957 §3-style discrepancy estimates is invoked
without explicit constants tracked".

Specific caveats:
1. Lemma 3.4 itself is not rigorized in this note; cited as standard
   (Hooley §3 + Erdős–Hooley delta-function for $\tau^*$).
2. The equivalence is *of existence as limits*, not of proof routes.
   A direct rigorization of $c_0^T$ via, e.g., $\zeta_K^\kappa H_\kappa$
   Selberg–Delange on the Dirichlet series of $2^{\omega(n^2+1)}$ at $s = 1$
   (treating $T$ as a level-$\kappa$ sieve sum) would not necessarily
   rigorize Lemma 3.4.
