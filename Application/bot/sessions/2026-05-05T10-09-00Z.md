## Result

**Rigorous identity.** $S(N) = N\Sigma_*(N^2+1) + B(N)$ where $B(N) = \sum_{d \le N^2+1} \tau(d^2)\delta_d(N)$, $\delta_d(N) = N_d(N) - \rho(d) N/d$. Both sums truncate at $d \le N^2+1$ exactly.

**Empirical localization (six datapoints, $N \in [10^3, 3 \cdot 10^5]$).** Splitting $B = B_< + B_>$ at $d = N$:
- $|B_<(N)/N| \le 0.04$ across the data range. So the small-divisor "boundary error" is at most $O(N)$ at our scales — *not* the source of the observed $\approx 0.85 NL$ residual.
- $B_>(N)/N \approx 0.833 L - 2.876$, $R^2 = 0.982$. Matches the previous-session empirical fit $0.85L - 2.2$ on the 3-term-Laurent residual (equivalently $0.85L - 3.08$ on the 4-term-Laurent residual; arithmetic verified).

**Reformulation as a discrepancy sum.** Using the pairing $r \leftrightarrow d - r$ on roots of $x^2 \equiv -1 \pmod d$,
$$ B_>(N) = \sum_{d > N} \tau(d^2) \!\!\sum_{r \in (0, d/2),\ r^2 \equiv -1 \!\!\pmod d}\! \big(\mathbb{1}[r \le N] + \mathbb{1}[d - r \le N] - \tfrac{2N}{d}\big), $$
which for $d > 2N$ simplifies to $\sum_d \tau(d^2) \sum_r [\mathbb{1}[r \le N] - 2N/d]$ (since $d - r > d/2 > N$). This is a discrepancy sum measuring deviation from uniformity of small roots $r \in (0, d/2)$, weighted by $\tau(d^2)$, and is the precise analytic target for the next-step rigorization.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The localization claim — that the empirical $\approx 0.85 NL$ residual on $S(N)$ lives in $B_>(N)$ at our six datapoints, and the small-divisor piece $B_<(N)$ is at most $O(N)$ over the same range — is verified by both skeptic rounds. The documented soft spot is the Tauberian-budget extrapolation from $X \le 10^7$ to $X = 9 \cdot 10^{10}$, which is asserted via analyticity rather than constant-tracked; the conclusion is robust (the budget is $\le 0.5$ per N either way, much smaller than observed $B_>/N$). The "$B_<$ asymptotically $O(N)$" claim is explicitly empirical-only and flagged as such.

## Verdict: PROGRESS

A clean structural localization that converts "the Hooley boundary" from heuristic phrasing into a precise sub-sum $B_>(N)$ with an explicit reformulation as a $\tau(d^2)$-weighted discrepancy sum on small roots of $x^2 \equiv -1 \pmod d$. The empirical target $B_>(N) \approx 0.85 NL - 2.9 N$ is now sharp: any rigorization of the second-moment asymptotic must compute or bound this specific sum. The complementary $B_<(N) = O(N)$ is established empirically over the data range, freeing future analytic work to focus on $B_>$.
