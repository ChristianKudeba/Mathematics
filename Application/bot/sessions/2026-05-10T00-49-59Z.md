## Result

**Three findings:**

1. **Across all three $(N, h)$ legs, the cumulative profile $C_h(D)/T_h$
   exhibits a structural mid-to-upper-$k$ pattern**: negative-dominated
   bulk → overshoot of $\mathrm{cum}/T_h$ above $1$ → positive correction
   wave → small negative tail to settle at $1.000$. The §3.2 single-instance
   observation of the predecessor session is now confirmed at three points.

2. **Monotone all-negative dyadic profile is refuted on the dominant blocks**
   (clean refutation; positive correction blocks present in every leg with
   contributions $-0.128 T_h$ to $-0.278 T_h$ each).

3. **A single Fourier mode (single $\xi^*$) is in tension with the data**;
   the trajectory of the peak block from $k=17$ ($N=3 \cdot 10^6$) to $k=21$
   ($N=10^7$) implies $c \approx 2.3$ for $\xi^* \sim N^{-c}$, with honest
   integer-block uncertainty $c \in [1.7, 2.9]$. Suggestive — not refutation —
   on a 2-point integer measurement.

**What remains plausible:**

- Single complex-pole damped oscillation in $\Psi_h(d)$ on a $d$-scale that
  grows with $N$ faster than $N^1$ (the cancellation pattern of §4.2 is
  consistent with this).
- Multi-mode spectral structure with mass distributed over multiple low frequencies.

**Strategic implication:** the natural next step is direct
$|\widehat{\widetilde S_h}(\xi)|^2$ on a fine $\xi$-grid at two distinct
$N$ — same data set, different transform — to discriminate between the
remaining pictures.

**Rigor accounting:** no new rigorous content this session. Identity $(\star)$
is verified numerically (was rigorous before). Findings 1–3 are descriptive
of the tested grid.
