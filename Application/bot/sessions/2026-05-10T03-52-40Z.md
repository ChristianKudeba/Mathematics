## Result

**Two findings (one positive, one negative).**

1. **(Positive)** Direct power spectrum $|\widehat{\widetilde S_h}(\xi)|^2$
   on the linear $\xi$-grid at $N \in \{3 \cdot 10^6, 10^7\}$, $h = 1$,
   has $\bar P_j \le D_h$ for *every* dyadic $\xi$-block in $(0, 1/2]$
   (resolution $\Delta\xi \in [3 \cdot 10^{-8}, 1 \cdot 10^{-7}]$). The
   spectrum is monotone non-decreasing in $\xi$ on the tested grid until
   saturation at $D_h$; no interior peak.

2. **(Negative — closing routes)** Three previously-plausible pictures
   (single Fourier mode, single narrow complex-pole damped oscillation,
   multi-mode with finite peaks) are **refuted in the narrow-linewidth
   regime**. Broad-linewidth Lorentzians ($\lambda \gtrsim 1/N$) are
   NOT refuted by this data, but the negative integral $\sum_d \Psi_h(d)
   = H_h^2 - D_h \approx -D_h$ already documented in prior sessions makes
   negative-amplitude Lorentzians (= dips) the natural fit, not positive
   peaks.

**The previous session's "$\xi^* \sim N^{-c}$, $c \approx 2.3$" inference
(P12-Halasz-Phi-d-dyadic.md §6 (iv)) is now superseded.** The peak
observed in the *cumulative* function $C_h(D)/T_h$ does not correspond
to a peak frequency in the spectrum.

**Strategic implication.** Spectral / Halász-mean-value approaches that
aim to bound $|H_h|$ via a localized $\xi^*$ are ruled out. The right
scalar target is the DC suppression factor $\rho_h(N) := |H_h|^2 / D_h$,
empirically $\le 0.022$ at the tested points, $\ll 1$ uniformly. A
quantitative bound on $\rho_h$ uniform in $N$ (and ideally in $h$)
would give the $|H_h| \ll \sqrt{N}$ rate that the P12 program needs.

**Rigor accounting:** no new rigorous content. Findings 1, 2 are
descriptive of the two tested $(N, h)$ points. The §6 "$T_h \approx -D_h/2$"
observation is a definitional restatement — the substantive content
$|H_h|^2 \ll D_h$ comes from earlier sessions.
