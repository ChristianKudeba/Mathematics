## Result

**(I) Diagonal/off-diagonal decomposition.** Across $N \in [500, 10^4]$,
$B_3^{\rm bdy}/(N \log N)$ rises monotonically from 0.911 to 0.948, in
the ballpark of $3/\pi \approx 0.955$ but with no proven limit.
$B_3^{\rm off}$ has matching opposite sign (forced by $B_3 = O(N)$
plus the bdy-decomposition definition), so the conjectural $B_3 = O(N)$
manifests as the residual of an $\sim cN\log N$ cancellation between
the diagonal sum (a clean SD-tractable object) and the off-diagonal
sum (the original analytic difficulty).

**(II) Per-$d$ sign-bucketing at $N=1000$.** Pos sum 13,399 vs $|{\rm neg}|$
13,063, net 336 (97.5% cancellation). Top 10 single contributors all
$d = n^2+1$ for $n \in [203, 993]$ with $\omega(n^2+1) \in \{4, 5\}$,
giving $+307$ ($91\%$ of net).

**Strategic upshot.** The diagonal piece $B_3^{\rm bdy}$ is a clean
Selberg–Delange object (1-2 sessions of bookkeeping for a rigorous
asymptotic with secondary $\Theta(N)$ term). The off-diagonal piece
$B_3^{\rm off}$ is the genuine remaining analytic problem and is
*not* simplified by this decomposition — the prior session's
"comparable to entire Hooley 1957" framing applies primarily to it.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The empirical decomposition table is correct (cross-checked against

sanity-checked by skeptic). The structural reframing is sound.
Documented soft spots: (a) per-$d$ sieve only at $N=1000$, not
$N=3000$ (timed out); (b) $B_3^{\rm bdy}/(N\log N) \to ?$ trend is
6 data points, limit not pinned; (c) the constant $C$ for
$\sum 2^{\omega(n^2+1)} \sim CN\log N$ is *related to* but not literally
Hooley's $3/\pi$ — its closed form requires its own SD computation
not done here; (d) the off-diagonal cancellation is the substantive
content and is not addressed.

## Verdict: PROGRESS

Converts the prior session's "$B_3$ has consistent positive bias"
empirical observation into a structural decomposition: the bias is
the residual of a $cN\log N$ cancellation between a tractable diagonal
piece and the off-diagonal. The diagonal piece is now a defined,
computable target (per-$n$ enumeration, $O(N)$); the off-diagonal
inherits the original analytic difficulty.

   pipe-buffer / timeout interaction. This pins the dyadic-window
   structure across $N$.

3. **(Multi-session, the real bottleneck) Off-diagonal asymptotic.**
   Establish $B_3^{\rm off}(N) = -c_1 N \log N + c_0' N + o(N)$ with
   the SAME leading constant $c_1$ as the diagonal (forced if
   $B_3 = O(N)$), and an independent secondary constant $c_0'$.
   This is essentially the original Hooley-style analytic problem.

4. **Banked methodological lesson:** when an empirical residual is
   composed of two pieces, each individually large but cancelling, the
   cleanest first move is to compute each piece directly (when feasible)
   to understand the magnitude of the cancellation. A "small bias" can
   hide a precise cancellation between two large pieces — the
   "$0.5 N$" headline of $B_3$ hides $\sim N \log N - N \log N$.
