## Decision

The previous session's pickup hint flagged path 1 (smoothed-window estimate of the cross-$d$ off-diagonal $\mathcal{O}(N)/N^2$) as the cleanest next step — but the existing data shows $\mathcal{O}/N^2$ wildly fluctuating ($-0.70, +0.25, -0.44, -0.33, -0.32$ across 5 windows), which is not "small noise on a clean limit" but the dominant signal.  I pivoted to a higher-EV experiment: compute the **cumulative second moment** $V(N) = \sum_{M \le N} T(M)^2$ directly from the $\tau(n^2+1)$ sieve up to $N = 10^7$, and check whether $V(N)/N^2$ converges (and to what).  This addresses the same conjecture (does $T$ have $\sqrt N$-RMS cancellation in the cumulative sense?) with a much less noisy estimator than single-window $\sum T^2$.

## What I did

1. **Wrote** `bot/scratch/V-cumulative.py`: full sieve of $\tau(n^2+1) \chi_4(n+1)$ to $N = 10^7$ in 13 s using even-only $k$-indexing (since $\chi_4$ kills odd $n$), then computes $V(N)$ for all $N$ in 0.6 s.

2. **Ran** the dense scan in `bot/scratch/V-fluctuation.py`. Found:
   * $V(10^7)/10^{14} = 0.1937$.
   * Across $N \in [10^5, 10^7]$, $V(N)/N^2 \in [0.15, 0.34]$.
   * Dense scan over $N \in [5\times 10^6, 10^7]$ at 100 sample points: mean $0.280$, std $0.057$, **rel std/mean $0.205$**.

3. **Made an INCORRECT claim** in the first draft: that this $0.205$ is "$\sim 5.6\times$ smaller" than the random-walk null prediction $2/\sqrt 3 \approx 1.155$, and concluded the data shows "mean-reverting structure" beyond a Gaussian RW.

4. **Skeptic round 1 destroyed the claim** (correctly): the empirical $0.205$ is the within-path std of $V(N)/N^2$ across $N$ on a single deterministic sequence; the RW prediction $1.155$ is the *ensemble* std at a fixed $N$ across realizations.  These are different quantities and conflating them is the error.

5. **Wrote** `bot/scratch/V-rw-simulation.py`: Monte Carlo of 50 Gaussian RW paths to $K = 10^6$ and 10 paths to $K = 5 \times 10^6$ with $\sigma^2 = 0.4$ (chosen to match empirical $V/N^2 \approx 0.2$; the *relative* std/mean is scale-invariant in $\sigma^2$).  Result: within-path rel std/mean is $0.222 \pm 0.13$ at $K = 10^6$ and $0.213 \pm 0.10$ at $K = 5 \times 10^6$.  **The empirical $0.205$ is statistically indistinguishable from the corrected RW null** ($z \approx -0.08$).

6. **Rewrote** `n2+1 ai thoughts/notes/proofs/P12-V-cumulative-second-moment.md` to retract the "mean-reverting structure" sub-claim, document the error and the corrected Monte Carlo comparison, and reduce the headline to descriptive statistics + a methodological lesson.

7. **Pickled** `bot/scratch/Tcum-V.pkl` (5001 sparse $(M, T(M), V(M))$ samples) for downstream sessions.

## Result

**Main claim (after retraction):** $V(N)/N^2 \in [0.15, 0.34]$ over $N \in [10^5, 10^7]$ with mean $\approx 0.28$ in the dense upper-half scan.  The within-$N$ fluctuation is statistically consistent with a Gaussian random-walk null on the same statistic; we have **no positive evidence** for mean-reverting structure of $T$ from this estimator.

**Methodological positive:** the previous session's hope that "smoothed-window estimates can pin a constant to 2 digits in 1 session" is **refuted** — even cumulative $V/N^2$ shows $\sim 20\%$ within-$N$ fluctuation at $N = 10^7$, and the fluctuation is RW-like, so more smoothing won't help materially.  Pinning $C^\dagger$ requires either $N \ge 10^9$ (memory-prohibitive) or theoretical input.

**Recovered descriptive numbers:** RMS$(T(M)/\sqrt M) \in [0.55, 0.78]$ over $N \in [10^5, 10^7]$, consistent with the prior session's sparse-sample estimate $0.638$.

**Methodological lesson (banked):** when comparing empirical within-path statistics against a theoretical null, ALWAYS use a Monte Carlo of the null applied to the same statistic — not the ensemble formula.
