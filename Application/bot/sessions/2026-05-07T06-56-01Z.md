## Result

**Empirical $B(N)/N$ at $N = 10^7$**: $0.085679$, vs predicted $0.085704$ —
gap $2.5 \cdot 10^{-5}$, well within sampling noise SE $\sim 10^{-4}$.

**Both sub-pieces match independently within $1\sigma$:**
- $\omega(q) = 1$: empirical $0.07792$, predicted $0.07794$.
- $\omega(q) \ge 2$: empirical $0.00776$, predicted $0.00776$.

**Conclusion**: the closed-form heuristic
$$B^\infty \approx c_1 \sum_{p \equiv 1(4)} \frac{\log p}{(p+2)(p-1)} = 0.085704$$
is empirically consistent with the data to within a $\sim 10^{-4}$ noise floor.
The prev session's "14% miss" alarm at $N = 10^5$ resolves as finite-$N$
artifact — at $N = 10^7$ the gap closes by $\sim 100\times$ relative to its
$N = 10^5$ value. The cross-correlation/double-counting hypothesis is
no longer supported by the data, though it cannot be logically refuted by
empirical agreement alone.

**Combined heuristic $c_0^T$:**
$$c_0^T \approx 2 R H'(1) + 2\gamma_K H(1) - 2 R H(1) - \pi H(1) \sum_{p \equiv 1(4)}\frac{\log p}{(p+2)(p-1)} \approx 0.98732,$$
