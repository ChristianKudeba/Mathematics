## Result

**Three findings, by rigor level:**

1. **Empirically refuted on grid (1 of 2 hypotheses):** the
   "fixed absolute $d$-scale" hypothesis $D^*_{50} = O(1)$ as $N \to
   \infty$.  $D^*_{50}$ grows from $\sim 10^3$ to $\sim 4 \cdot 10^4$
   over the tested range.

2. **Empirically NOT refuted:** the "uniformly spread" hypothesis
   $D^*_{50}/N \to \mathrm{const}$.  Data could be approaching a
   positive constant; 3 $N$-points cannot rule it out.

3. **Suggestive (single instance):** at the largest $(N, h)$ a
   "negative wave + positive correction wave" pattern appears
   ($24\%$ overshoot at $D = 3 \cdot 10^5$ in $(3 \cdot 10^6, 1)$).
   Whether this generalizes is open.

**Auxiliary observation:** $|H_h|^2/D_h \le 0.236$ on the 6-point
grid with 4 of 6 $\le 0.013$ — order-of-magnitude suppression vs the
diagonal-CLT one-step prediction of $\approx 1$.  Trend in $N$
non-monotone in either $h$.

**Strategic implication:** the simplest "spectral peak at fixed
absolute $\xi$" picture is refuted; remaining plausible pictures are
(i) low-frequency dominance with $\xi \sim N^{-c}$ for some $c \in
(0, 1]$, or (ii) signed dyadic-block structure with sign alternation
at separated $d$-scales.  Direct $|\widehat{\widetilde S_h}(\xi)|^2$
data (prev session pickup hint #2) discriminates between (i) and (ii).

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

Caveats:
1. Three $N$ values, two $h$ values — limited grid.
2. Overshoot in $C_h(D)/T_h$ is a single strong instance.  Stability
   under wider $(N, h)$ untested.
3. The "uniform-spread vs sublinear" $D^*$ scaling is *consistent
   with both* on this grid.
4. §7.1 heuristic about Fourier support is unproven; needs direct
   $|\widehat{\widetilde S_h}(\xi)|^2$ data to pin.

Skeptic rounds verified: (a) definition consistency with predecessor
