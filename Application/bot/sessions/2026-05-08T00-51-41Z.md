## Result

**Two verifiable items achieved:**

1. **Rigorous identification.** Lemma 3.4 ($E(N) = o(N)$) is equivalent —
   modulo $O(N^\epsilon)$ jumps — to the saw-tooth statement
   $$\Psi(N) := \sum_{\substack{e \,\mathrm{sf}\\ 2 \le e \le N}} \sum_{i=1}^{\rho(e)} \bar B_1\!\left(\frac{N - r_i^{(e)}}{e}\right) = o(N).$$
   Via Vaaler/Fourier, $\Psi(N) = o(N)$ in turn follows from
   $\sup_{h \le H(N)} |T_h(N)|/N \to 0$ for some $H(N) \to \infty$, where
   $T_h(N) = \sum_{e \le N, \mathrm{sf}, e \ge 2} e^{2\pi i h N/e} S_h(e)$.
   The reduction in §2-§3 is rigorous; §4 establishes the multiplicative
   structure of $S_h(e)$ but only the trivial bound $|S_h(e)| \le \rho(e)$ is
   used.

2. **Empirical validation** at 7 values of $N$ up to $10^6$, with $|E|/\sqrt N$
   bounded across 6/7 scales. The qualitative $o(N)$ bound (Lemma 3.4) is
   *strongly* supported empirically; the heuristic pointwise $O(\sqrt N \log^c N)$
   rate is also supported.

**What is NOT achieved.** A sub-linear bound on $T_h(N)$. The trivial bound
$|S_h(e)| \le \rho(e)$ gives only $|T_h(N)| = O(N)$. Routes (i)/(ii)/(iii)
of §5 (Salié bounds; Vinogradov; Hooley 1957 §3) are each multi-session.

**Honest assessment.** This note converts the unrigorized step from
"$E(N) = o(N)$" to "$T_h(N) = o(N)$ uniformly in $h$". The latter is in
*cleaner Fourier form*, easier to look up in the literature and verify a
specific citation against. But the analytic difficulty is unchanged: it's
a re-statement, not a difficulty reduction. The session's value is
(a) the empirical validation and (b) the cleaner formulation for future
literature work.
