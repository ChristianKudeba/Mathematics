## Result

**Empirical envelope.** For sf-good $e \le N$ with $e \ge 2$ and the F.2 definition
of $C_{\mathrm{num}}(N)$ (off-diagonal pair sum of $1/|\sin(\pi\beta)|$):
$$
C_{\mathrm{num}}(N) \le N^{2 + o(1)} \quad \text{(empirical, } 7 \text{ points,} \, N \le 2000\text{)},
$$
equivalently $C(N) = 2 C_{\mathrm{num}}/A \le N^{1 + o(1)}$. Both
$C_{\mathrm{num}} \sim 0.19 N^2 \log N$ and $C_{\mathrm{num}} \sim N^{2.22}$ fit.

**Theorem F.2 with empirical envelope.** $|R(N, H) - 1| \le C(N)/H \le N^{1 + o(1)} / H$.
Effective at $H \gg N \log N$ (or $H \gg N^{1.22}$ depending on model).

**Strategic implications at $H \gg N \log N$**:
- (Cauchy/Jensen) $L^2$-mean $|H_h| \le \sqrt{A(N)} \le 0.66 \sqrt N$.
- (Markov density) 99% of $h \in [1, H]$ satisfy $|H_h(N)|^2 \le 100 \cdot A(N) \le 43 N$.

**Strategic gap (unchanged from prev session).** Upstream Lemma 3.4 needs
*uniform-in-$h$* $|H_h(N)| \ll \sqrt N (\log N)^{O(1)}$ for $h \le H(N)$ slowly
growing. F.2 gives only the Cesaro mean over $h$; this is necessary but not
sufficient. F.2 with measured $C(N)$ is a Markov/density tool, not a uniform tool.
