## What I did

1. **Empirical second-moment of $T(M)$** (`bot/scratch/second-moment-empirical.py`). Loaded the existing `Tcum.pkl` (50 000 sparse samples up to $N_{\max} = 5 \times 10^7$), computed dyadic-window sums $\sum_{M \in (N, 2N]} T(M)^2$ via stride-1000 approximation. Result: $\sum T^2 / N^2 \in [0.29, 1.24]$ across windows; full-range $\sum_{M \le N_{\max}} T^2 / N_{\max}^2 \approx 0.195$. Window fluctuations large but consistent with $\Theta(N^2)$ overall.
2. **Direct AP-decomposition diagonal** (`bot/scratch/diag-vs-second-moment.py`). For $N \in \{1000, 2000, 5000, 10000\}$:
   - Built the full set of $(d, n_0)$ pairs with $d \in L_{\mathrm{odd}}, d \le 2N, n_0^2 \equiv -1 \pmod d$ via Hensel-lift BFS over primes $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$.
   - Walked $S_{M,d}(n_0)$ as $M$ increased $1 \to 2N$.
   - Computed $\mathcal{D}(N) = 4\sum_d \sum_M c_d(M)^2$ (full AP diagonal) and $\mathcal{D}_0(N) = 4 \sum_d \sum_{n_0} \sum_M S_{M,d}(n_0)^2$ ("true diagonal", $n_0=n_0'$ only).
3. **Findings.**
   - $\mathcal{D}(N)/N^2$ stayed in $[0.976, 1.011]$ across the four $N$'s — only 1.5% drift over a decade.
   - $\mathcal{D}_0(N)/N^2$ stayed in $[0.745, 0.753]$ — even tighter.
   - Cross-$n_0$-within-$d$ piece $\mathcal{D} - \mathcal{D}_0$ is $\approx 0.24 N^2$ steady.
   - Cross-$d$ off-diagonal $\sum_M T^2 - \mathcal{D}$ is *negative on average*, ranging $-0.025 N^2$ to $-0.70 N^2$ across windows. Large negative cross-$d$ at $N = 10^4$ (where direct = 0.29 vs. diag = 0.99).
4. **Analytical sketch.** $S_{M,d}(n_0)^2$ averages to $\tfrac{1}{2}$ over a $4d$-period (steady-state). Yields interior lower bound $\mathcal{D}_0(N) \gtrsim 2c N^2 \approx 0.64 N^2$ where $c \approx 0.319$ from P12 Thm C step 6 numerics. Trivial upper bound $\mathcal{D}_0(N) \le 6c N^2 \approx 1.92 N^2$. The empirical $0.747$ sits between, but the boundary contribution from $d \in (N, 2N]$ is not derived.
5. **Wrote up in** `n2+1 ai thoughts/notes/proofs/P12-second-moment-diagonal.md`.

## Verdict: PROGRESS

Concrete deliverables: a verified empirical statement that the AP-decomposition diagonal scales as $N^2$ with constant near 1; a partial analytical derivation locking in $\mathcal{D}_0(N) \gtrsim 0.64 N^2$ rigorously; an explicit clarification that the strategy hint's "$N \cdot \sum \rho(d)^2/d \sim N^2$" line was misleading — the second-moment route does **not** strictly avoid the polylog problem, because Cauchy from $T = 2\sum_d c_d$ overcounts by $|L_{\mathrm{odd}}(N)| \sim N/\sqrt{\log N}$. Net: the second-moment route's "diagonal works cleanly" half is empirically verified and partially proved; its "off-diagonal is small" half remains a research direction, not a 1-2-session sub-task. STRATEGY.md updated to reflect this honest scope.
