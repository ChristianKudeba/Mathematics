## Result

**Empirical localization.** $B_>(N)$ is concentrated in deep-tail dyadic windows: at our four data points $N \in [10^4, 3 \cdot 10^5]$, the fraction of $|B_>(N)|$ in windows with $d > N^{1.85}$ is $70\%, 86\%, 96\%, 91\%$ respectively. Cumulative residuals through $d \le N^{1.7}$ are bounded by $0.44 N$ across all four checkpoints, consistent with the formal-Selberg-Delange prediction up to the documented Tauberian budget.

**Discriminator on previous-session sub-tasks.** The "moderate tail $d \in (N, 2N]$" sub-task is refuted: residual contribution $\le 0.075 N$ (≤ 1% of total). The "deep tail $d > 2N$" sub-task is sharpened to $d > N^{1.85}$.

**Negative result on a tempting reformulation.** The complementary-divisor map $d \leftrightarrow r = (n^2+1)/d$ does NOT yield an analytic reduction. The $r = 1$ piece is essentially a tail of $S(N)$ itself (asymptotically of the same order); $r = 2, 3, \ldots$ likewise relabel rather than simplify. The localization is a diagnostic, not a strategic reduction.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The empirical localization claim — that $B_>(N)$ is dominated by $d > N^{1.85}$ at our four data points — is the substantive result and is robust to the skeptic's CORE issues. The skeptic-flagged anomaly at $N = 10^5$ and the non-monotone concentration trend across four data points are documented; one more decade of data ($N = 10^6, 3 \cdot 10^6$) is needed to rule out a fixed-cutoff artifact in the concentration measure.
