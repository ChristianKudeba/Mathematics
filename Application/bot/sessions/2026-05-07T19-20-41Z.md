## Result

**Empirical $c_0^T(3 \cdot 10^7) = 0.987283$**, gap to high-precision
predicted $0.9873174$ is $-3.47 \cdot 10^{-5}$, a factor-3.29 shrinkage
from the $-1.14 \cdot 10^{-4}$ gap at $N = 10^7$.

**Per-component gaps** (emp − pred) at $N = 3 \cdot 10^7$:
- $\Delta_{c_<} = -2.58 \cdot 10^{-5}$
- $\Delta_A = -4.08 \cdot 10^{-6}$
- $\Delta_B = -4.37 \cdot 10^{-6}$
sum to $-3.47 \cdot 10^{-5}$ exactly via the AB identity.

**The heuristic $B^\infty_{\rm pred} = 0.085704$ has its strongest
single-scale agreement to date** at $N = 3 \cdot 10^7$ (gap
$-4.4 \cdot 10^{-6}$). At $N \in \{10^6, 10^7\}$ the gap is $-2.5 \cdot 10^{-5}$,
so the cumulative empirical interval is $B^\infty \in [0.085680, 0.085704]$.

**Decay rate is NOT cleanly $1/(\log N)^A$**. The local slope $k \approx 18$
at $[10^7, 3 \cdot 10^7]$ is implausibly large; we are in a pre-asymptotic
regime dominated by sub-leading SD constants, not the principal Tauberian
remainder. Pickup hint #4's $1/\log N$ projection ($\Delta \approx 4 \cdot 10^{-5}$
at $N = 10^9$) was conservative.

**No new rigorous content.** The match remains conditional on (a) existence
of $B^\infty$ (paralleling Estermann 1931 density of sf $n^2+1$, unproven
here); (b) the heuristic per-prime closed form for $B^\infty$.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

Empirical agreement at the new scale tightens prev-session's bounds.
Caveats:
1. $B^\infty$ existence and value remain conditional.
2. Pre-asymptotic regime: rate cannot be identified from 2 well-resolved
   high-$N$ points; could reverse / oscillate at $N \gg 3 \cdot 10^7$.
3. The factor-3.29 shrinkage is anchor-sensitive ($\sim 20\%$).
4. $c_<^{\rm app}$ vs. asymptote sign behavior depends on which precision
   level of predicted $c_<^\infty$ is used (lower-precision $1.013401$
   gives sign-flip; high-precision $1.013430$ does not — both gaps
   negative).
