## Result

**Empirical $c_0^T(10^7) = +0.987203$**, matching the closed-form
prediction $c_0^T_\infty = 0.987256$ (with stated 6-digit inputs) at

(c) prev-session's confirmation of $B^\infty = 0.085704$, (d) prev

and the cumulative-trend stability data.

Predicted value re-derivation (with 6-digit constants $H(1) = 0.552674$,
$H'(1) = 0.83558$, $\gamma_K = 0.6462$):
$c_0^T_\infty = 2(R H'(1) + \gamma_K H(1) - R H(1)) - 2 B^\infty
= 2 \cdot 0.579332 - 2 \cdot 0.085704 = 1.158664 - 0.171408 = 0.987256$.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The numerical match is strong (gap below input-precision uncertainty)
and the empirical $B(N)/N$ stability across $N = 10^6$ to $10^7$
(at the $10^{-6}$ level) is genuinely strong evidence for convergence.
However:

1. **Heuristic-ness inherited.** The $B^\infty = 0.085704$ value comes
   from a heuristic per-prime closed form (uniform-in-log assumption,
   Hensel-CRT factorization) — see prev session's caveats. So the
   "closed-form prediction" of $c_0^T$ is heuristic-conditional.
2. **Circularity.** The AB integer identity makes $c_0^T(N)$
   algebraically equal to $2(c_<^{\rm app}(N) - A(N)/N - B(N)/N)$;
   the match is forced by prev sessions' rigorous and empirical
   ingredients, not independent.
3. **Existence of $B^\infty$ unproven.** Plausible (cf. Estermann 1931
   density of sf $n^2+1$) but no rigorous proof.
4. **Input-precision uncertainty.** The headline closed-form value
   $0.987256$ vs $0.987322$ (prev note) differ by the stated 6-digit
   precision of $H(1), H'(1), \gamma_K$.
