$$B^{(\omega=2),\infty}_{\rm full} \approx c_1 C_0 G \cdot [S_\beta^\sharp S_\alpha^\sharp - S_{\rm diag}^\sharp] \approx 0.0075381,$$

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The FULL closed-form prediction $0.0075381$ matches empirical $0.0075123$
at $N = 10^7$ within sampling noise ($0.5\sigma$, SE $= 5.1 \cdot 10^{-5}$),
and the LEAD approximation is empirically distinguishable at $\approx 4\sigma$.

Caveats: (i) Hooley uniform-in-log heuristic (potentially differential bias
across $\omega$, not resolved); (ii) Hensel-CRT factorization treated as
literal Euler product; (iii) finite-$N$ drift of $6 \cdot 10^{-5}$ per decade
between $N = 10^6$ and $10^7$ comparable to the FULL-empirical gap, so
"convergence" not strictly established (would need $N \approx 10^9$);
(iv) block-bootstrap SE captures only AP-level correlation crudely.
