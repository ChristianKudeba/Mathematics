## Decision

STRATEGY.md's promoted Path 1 (high-EV) was: derive $\sigma^2$ analytically via Selberg–Delange on $\sum_{n \le N}\tau(n^2+1)^2$, the diagonal of the second-moment expansion of the $\sigma$-spin sum $T$. I executed it: identify the relevant Dirichlet series, factor it through $\zeta_K(s) = \zeta_{\mathbb{Q}(i)}(s)$, locate the pole structure, and predict the leading constant in closed form. Numerical verification at $N \le 10^6$ to test the prediction.

## What I did

1. **Algebraic identification.** Proved $\tau(n^2+1) = \tau_K(n+i)$ for all $n \ge 1$ (Gaussian-integer ideal divisor function), with both $n$-even and $n$-odd cases verified explicitly. Used Vinogradov's $\tau(m)^2 = \sum_{d|m}\tau(d^2)$.

2. **Dirichlet series.** Set
$$G(s) := \sum_{d \ge 1}\tau(d^2)\rho(d) d^{-s}, \qquad \rho(d) = \#\{n \pmod d : d \mid n^2+1\}.$$
Derived local factors:
   - $p \equiv 3 \pmod 4$: factor $1$ (since $\rho(p^k) = 0$).
   - $p = 2$: factor $1 + 3\cdot 2^{-s}$.
   - $p \equiv 1 \pmod 4$: factor $L_p(s) = (1 + 4 p^{-s} - p^{-2s})/(1-p^{-s})^2$.

3. **Comparison with $\zeta_K(s)^3$.** Showed $G(s) = \zeta_K(s)^3 H(s)$ where $H(s)$ is an absolutely convergent Euler product on $\Re s > 1/2$ (since each split-prime correction is $1 + 0\cdot p^{-s} - 11 p^{-2s} + O(p^{-3s})$). Evaluated:
$$H(1) = \tfrac{5}{16}\!\!\prod_{p\equiv 3(4)}\!\!\left(1 - p^{-2}\right)^3\!\!\prod_{p\equiv 1(4)}\!\!\left(1 - p^{-1}\right)^4\!\left(1 + 4 p^{-1} - p^{-2}\right) \approx 0.12324.$$

4. **Selberg–Delange asymptotic (formal).** With triple pole and $\zeta_K$ residue $\pi/4$, the Tauberian extraction gives
$$\sum_{n \le N}\tau(n^2+1)^2 \overset{?}{\sim} C \cdot N(\log N)^3, \qquad C = \frac{\pi^3 H(1)}{48} \approx 0.0796.$$

5. **Caveat (CORE).** The elementary derivation has a boundary error from $d > N$ that, naively bounded, *dominates* the main term. This is the analog of the entire Hooley 1957 hyperbola argument. The leading constant is the formal SD prediction; rigorous proof of the asymptotic remains for next session.

6. **Numerical check.** Wrote `bot/scratch/tau-sq-second-moment.py`. Computed $S(N)$ exactly for $N$ up to $10^6$ via sieved factorization. Also computed $\sum \tau(n^2+1)$ as a control against Hooley's rigorously known $(3/\pi)N\log N$. Results: at $N = 10^6$, the rigorous Hooley ratio is $1.070$ (7% off asymptote); our $\tau^2$ ratio is $1.978$ (98% off, but consistent with the higher-pole secondary-correction structure). Pairwise 1-term fits $y = c_3 + a/x$ in $x = \log N$ approach $c_3 \approx 0.076$ at the largest pair, vs prediction $0.0796$. Convergence pattern consistent with a positive $b/x^2$ correction.

7. **Wrote up** `n2+1 ai thoughts/notes/proofs/P12-tau-squared-second-moment.md` with the rigorous Dirichlet-series content, the conjectural asymptotic, and the empirical evidence.

## Result

**Rigorous content (proven this session):**
* $\tau(n^2+1) = \tau_K(n+i)$ as functions of $n$ (Lemma 1).
* $\tau(m)^2 = \sum_{d|m}\tau(d^2)$ (Lemma 2).
* The Dirichlet series $G(s) := \sum_d \tau(d^2)\rho(d) d^{-s}$ factors as $G(s) = \zeta_K(s)^3 H(s)$ with $H$ given by an explicit Euler product converging absolutely on $\Re s > 1/2$. In particular $H(1) \approx 0.12324$ is finite, nonzero, and computable in closed form to arbitrary precision by truncating the Euler product.

**Conjectural content (formal SD prediction, supported numerically):**
$$\sum_{n \le N}\tau(n^2+1)^2 \sim \frac{\pi^3 H(1)}{48}\, N(\log N)^3, \quad C \approx 0.0796.$$
Empirical 1-term fits at $N \in [3\cdot 10^5, 10^6]$ give $c_3 \approx 0.076$, within 5% of $C$.

**Implication for $\sigma$-spin.** The trivial Cauchy bound is $T(N)^2 \le N \cdot S(N) \asymp N^2(\log N)^3$, recovering the trivial $\tau$-summed bound. Empirically $T(N) = O(\sqrt N)$, so off-diagonal $\chi_4$-twist cancellation must beat the diagonal by a factor $(\log N)^3$. Computing the off-diagonal pair correlation $\sum \tau(n^2+1)\tau((n+h)^2+1)\chi_4(n+1)\chi_4(n+h+1)$ in closed form is the next sub-target.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

Author and skeptic agree:
* Dirichlet-series identity $G(s) = \zeta_K(s)^3 H(s)$ is rigorously established with $H$ explicit.
* The asymptotic $S(N) \sim C N \log^3 N$ is the formal SD prediction, supported by numerical evidence but not yet rigorized; the Hooley-type boundary argument is the substantive remaining analytic step.
* Numerical evidence is *consistent with* but does not *empirically pin* the constant.
* $H(1) = 0.12324$ and $C = 0.0796$ are computable in closed form to arbitrary precision.

**Caveat:** The asymptotic itself is conjectural. The natural follow-up (estimated 1 session) is to port Hooley's hyperbola/$\mathbb{Z}[i]$-parametrization argument from $\sum \tau(n^2+1)$ to $\sum \tau(n^2+1)^2$ and rigorize the leading asymptotic.

Specifically: split divisors of $n^2+1$ at $\sqrt{n^2+1}$, parametrize via primitive Gaussian integers $\alpha = n+i$ and their ideal divisors, apply lattice-point counting in $\mathbb{Z}[i]$ on a smooth weight. The 1957 Hooley paper for $\sum \tau$ is the template; the move to $\tau^2$ requires extending the parametrization to pairs of divisors. Estimated 1 session.

2. **(Heavier, longer.)** Compute the off-diagonal pair correlation
$$C(N; h) = \sum_{n \le N}\tau(n^2+1)\tau((n+h)^2+1)\chi_4(n+1)\chi_4(n+h+1)$$
in closed form. This would directly give $\sigma^2$ via the second-moment expansion. Selberg–Delange on $\zeta_K$ with shifted twists; non-trivial because the twist $\chi_4(n+1)\chi_4(n+h+1)$ is non-multiplicative in $n$. Estimated 2–3 sessions; heaviest analytic step in the project so far.
