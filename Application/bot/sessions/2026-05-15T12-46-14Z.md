3. **(I) Archimedean factor $\phi_\infty$.**

    Coordinates: $E_\infty := E \otimes_\mathbb{Q} \mathbb{R} \cong \mathbb{C} \times \mathbb{C}$ via the two embeddings $\sigma_1, \sigma_2 \colon E \hookrightarrow \mathbb{C}$ extending the unique archimedean embedding $F = \mathbb{Q}(i) \hookrightarrow \mathbb{C}$. As $F_\infty$-algebra, $E_\infty = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{C}$ (split). The $F$-Galois involution $\bar{\cdot} \in \mathrm{Gal}(E/F)$ extends to $E_\infty$ as the **swap** $(z_1, z_2) \mapsto (z_2, z_1)$.

    **Notation pitfall recorded.** The naïve formula "$\phi_\infty(x) = e^{-2\pi x\bar x}$" with $\bar{\cdot}$ = $F$-Galois is **wrong**: gives $e^{-2\pi z_1 z_2}$, which is complex-valued and not Schwartz. The correct positive-definite Gaussian uses **place-wise complex conjugation**:
    $$\phi_\infty(x) := \exp(-2\pi q_\infty(x)), \quad q_\infty(x) := |\sigma_1 x|^2_\mathbb{C} + |\sigma_2 x|^2_\mathbb{C}.$$
    This is the positive-definite $\mathbb{R}$-quadratic form on $E_\infty \cong \mathbb{R}^4$ given by the sum of the squared Euclidean lengths of the two complex embeddings. Throughout the literature (YZZ, Friedberg–Hoffstein), "$e^{-2\pi x\bar x}$" is used as place-wise complex-conjugation absolute-value-squared shorthand, not as Galois norm $\mathrm{Nm}_{E/F}$. For an imaginary-quadratic $E/\mathbb{Q}$ the two coincide; for our Bianchi setting with $F = \mathbb{Q}(i)$ totally imaginary they do **not**. Subsequent chunks must not silently substitute one for the other.

    Verified properties (Ia)–(Ie):
    - **(Ia) Schwartz.** $\phi_\infty \in \mathcal{S}(E_\infty)$.
    - **(Ib) Positive.** $\phi_\infty > 0$; $\phi_\infty(0) = 1$.
    - **(Ic) Fourier self-duality.** $\widehat{\phi_\infty} = \phi_\infty$ under self-dual Haar measure on $E_\infty$ (per C3) and Fourier transform $\widehat{\phi_\infty}(y) = \int_{E_\infty} \phi_\infty(x) \psi_\infty(\mathrm{tr}_{E_\infty/F_\infty}(x\bar y)) dx$. Verified by explicit completing-the-square on each $\mathbb{C}$-factor: with $\mathrm{tr}(x\bar y) = z_1 w_2 + z_2 w_1$ (Galois swap on $\bar y$) and $\psi_\infty(z) = e^{2\pi i \cdot 2 \mathrm{Re} z}$, each $\mathbb{C}$-integral $2 \int_\mathbb{R} e^{-2\pi u^2 + 4\pi iua} du \cdot \int_\mathbb{R} e^{-2\pi v^2 - 4\pi ivb} dv = 2 \cdot e^{-2\pi a^2}/\sqrt 2 \cdot e^{-2\pi b^2}/\sqrt 2 = e^{-2\pi |\tau|^2}$. Combining the two factors yields $\widehat{\phi_\infty}(y) = e^{-2\pi(|w_1|^2 + |w_2|^2)} = \phi_\infty(y)$. ✓
