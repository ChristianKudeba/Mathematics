## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The empirical computation is correct (cross-validated at small $N$);
the script is correct (identity, sieve, factorization argument all sound);
the data interpretation is now skeptic-confirmed. Documented soft spots:
(i) only 1.8 decades of data, doesn't distinguish $\Theta(N)$ from
$N \log\log N$ slow growth; (ii) the constant $C \approx 0.49$ is a
working hypothesis from the data, not a derived value; (iii) the cross-
check with the band difference is consistency, not triangulation; (iv)
the underlying $B_3 = O(N)$ conjecture is still unproven analytically.

## Verdict: PROGRESS

Converts the prior session's "qualitative reduction to Hooley-boundary
cancellation" from a black-box conjectural component into a quantitatively
characterized empirical object. We now have direct empirical evidence on
$B_3(N)$ at many $N$, with a working "$\sim 0.49 N$" reading that any
analytic attack should aim to either confirm or refute. The cross-check
$0.49(1-1/\sqrt 2) \approx 0.14 N$ vs the prior empirical band $[0.04N, 0.21N]$

## Next-session pickup hint

1. **Extend $B_3$ data to $N = 10^5$.** Sieve enumeration at $X = 10^{10}$
   would be ~10× slower (~30 min). Borderline-tractable in one session.
   Two more decades of data would discriminate $\Theta(N)$ from $N \log\log N$.

2. **Sign-bias diagnostic.** Compute the per-$d$-bracket signed contribution
   to $B_3$: are most $d$ contributing positive $\delta_d$, or do a few large
   contributions dominate? This would inform the "rounding-bias vs missed
   constant Laurent" question (Discussion §4 of the proof note).

3. **Analytic attack on the constant $C$.** If $B_3 \sim C N$, what is $C$?
   The sum $\sum_d 2^{\omega(d)} (\{Nx_0(d)/d\} - \{N x_1(d)/d\}/2 - 1/2 + ...)$
   for $x_i$ the $\rho(d)$ roots of $x^2 \equiv -1 \pmod d$ has structure
   amenable to analytic treatment (Erdős-Hooley / cotangent-Fourier).
   Multi-session.

4. **Methodological lesson banked.** When a rigorous reduction lands on an
   empirical bound (here, $B_3 = O(N)$), the cheapest first move is to
   directly compute the unknown via the exact identity (when available) at
   multiple $N$ — this immediately tells you whether you're chasing a
   $\Theta(N)$ constant or a $o(N)$ bound, which determines whether to
   pursue an "exact constant" derivation or a "cancellation" argument.
