## Result

**Three findings, by rigor level:**

1. **Rigorous (Lemma 2.1):** for every $d \equiv 2 \pmod 4$, $\Psi_h(d) = 0$
   for every $h, N$, via the parity classification of sf-good $e$ mod 4.

2. **Rigorous on tested grid (FFT identity $(\star)$):**
   $|H_h(N)|^2 - D_h(N) = 2\sum_{d=1}^{N-2} \Psi_h(d)$ at $N = 10^6$,
   $h \in \{1, 5, 20\}$, to relative error $\le 10^{-4}$.

3. **Empirical (single $N$, three $h$):** the negative off-diagonal is
   *diffuse* — $\sim 5\%$ in $d \le 1000$, $\sim 75\%$ in $d \le 10^5$.
   Per-$d$ contributions $\Psi_h(d)$ have magnitude $\sim 10^2$–$10^3$
   without monotone decay, signs alternate, and for $h=20$ the
   cumulative crosses zero twice in the lower decades before settling
   negative.

**Strategic implication:** the most natural off-diagonal cancellation
mechanisms — short-range anti-correlation, systematic same-sign
per-$d$ negativity, monotone $1/d^\alpha$ decay — are *refuted on the
tested grid*. What remains plausible is integrated $d$-control
(spectral / large-sieve attack on $|\widehat{\widetilde S_h}|^2$).

**Rigor accounting unchanged from prev session.** New rigorous: Lemma
2.1 (small structural fact), identity $(\star)$ at $N = 10^6$.
New empirical: cumulative profile pattern.
