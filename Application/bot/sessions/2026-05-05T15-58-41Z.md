## Result

**Empirical.** At $N = 10^6$, $B_>(N)/N = 8.8621$, fraction in $d > N^{1.85}$ is
$103\%$, bulk-region cumulative residual is $-0.31 N$.

**Combined trend (5 data points).** $\text{cum}_{d \le N^{1.85}} / N$ goes
$1.45 \to 0.81 \to 0.26 \to 0.72 \to -0.31$ — net decrease, one local upward step.
Deep-tail fraction $\ge 90\%$ at three of the five $N$.

**Structural.** $W_{K-1}$ (the partial top window) at every $N$ in our range equals the
$r = 1$ contribution to $B_>(N)$, hence a partial sum of Hooley's second moment
$S_2(N) = \sum \tau(n^2+1)^2$. Renaming, not reduction.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The localization claim is *modestly strengthened* by the new data point — five-point
evidence is consistent with a true asymptotic concentration of $B_>(N)$ in $d > N^{1.85}$.
Documented soft spots: (i) one non-monotone step in the five-point bulk-residual
sequence is unexplained; (ii) the Tauberian-budget defense at $X = 10^{12}$ is
qualitative not effective. Both flagged in the writeup.
