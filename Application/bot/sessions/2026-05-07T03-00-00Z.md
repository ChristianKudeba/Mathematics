## Skeptic dialogue

**Round 1** (general-purpose subagent) confirmed §4 algebra is correct
($\sum_{k\ge 2}(k-1)/p^k = 1/(p-1)^2$ verified) but raised four CORE concerns:

1. **$\mathbb E[\tau^*(n^2+1) | v_p = k]$ pulled out of a divergent product**
   — morally OK but glossed; the global $c_1 \log N$ asymptotic and the
   single-prime factorization both assume the residue at $s=1$ of
   $\zeta_K \cdot H$ separates cleanly across Euler factors.
2. **Cross-correlation gap.** The $\nu_p^+$ decomposition is exact, but
   summing per-$p$ contributions evaluates each as if $v_p$ were independent
   of other primes. **Empirical check: $\omega(q) = 1$ matches predicted to
   4 decimals, but $\omega(q) \ge 2$ shows $0.0089$ vs $0.0078$ predicted —
   $\sim 14\%$ miss.** The total reconciles only because the $\nu_p^+$ sum
   double-counts joint events $\{v_p \ge 2, v_{p'} \ge 2\}$, and the
   over-count happens to cancel the $\omega \ge 2$ shortfall.
3. **Uniform-in-log is the dominant uncontrolled error.** Sf divisor logs
   are Gaussian-distributed (CLT), not uniform; the window $(\sqrt r, n]$
   sits in the upper half where Gaussian density exceeds uniform.
4. **4-decimal match likely coincidental.** $B(N)/N$ drifts $0.0880 \to 0.0857$
   over 2 decades; with $O(1/\log N) \sim 0.005$ corrections, the apparent
   $0.0857 = 0.0857$ at $N = 10^6$ is *within* finite-$N$ noise, not strong
   evidence for the closed form being exact.

## Result

**Closed-form heuristic** (NOT rigorous):
$$B^\infty \approx c_1 \sum_{p \equiv 1 \pmod 4} \frac{\log p}{(p+2)(p-1)} \approx 0.08570,$$
where $c_1 = \pi H(1)/2 = 0.86813$.

**Empirical agreement** (mixed signal, honestly):
| level | predicted | empirical | gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| $\omega(q) = 1$ contribution | $0.07794$ | $0.07795$ ($N = 10^5$) | $< 0.0001$ |
| $\omega(q) \ge 2$ contribution | $0.0078$ | $0.0089$ ($N = 10^5$) | $0.0011$ ($14\%$) |
| **total $B^\infty$** | $0.08570$ | $0.0857$ ($N = 10^6$) | $< 0.0001$ |

**Combined $c_0^T$ closed form** (heuristic):
$$c_0^T \approx 2 R H'(1) + 2 \gamma_K H(1) - 2 R H(1) - \pi H(1) \sum_{p \equiv 1(4)}\frac{\log p}{(p+2)(p-1)} \approx 0.98733.$$

Empirical $c_0^T$ at $N = 10^6$: $\approx 0.988$ — match to 3 decimals.

**Rigorous co-products of this session:**
- Density formula $D_q = C_0 \prod_p 2(1-1/p)/(p^{v_p}(1-2/p^2))$ — rigorous,
  matches empirical to 4-5 decimals.
- Aggregate density $1 - C_0 = 0.10516$ for non-sf $n^2+1$ — matches
  empirical $0.10517$.
- Identity $\log Q = \sum_p \nu_p^+ \log p$ — exact.
- Algebraic identity $\sum_{k \ge 2}(k-1)/p^k = 1/(p-1)^2$.

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

The closed form is documented as a heuristic (not rigorous), with explicit
listing of three uncontrolled error sources (uniform-in-log, leading-order
$\tau^*$ asymptotic, single-prime factorization) and one concrete empirical
discrepancy (14% miss in $\omega(q) \ge 2$). The total apparent 4-decimal
match is acknowledged as partly coincidental. The density side ($D_q$ formula)
is rigorous and matches empirical to 4-5 decimals.
