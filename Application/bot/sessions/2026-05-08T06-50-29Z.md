## Result

**Pickup hint #1 from prev session is RETIRED.** Two combined arguments:

(i) **Analytic.** B-process on the unweighted $e$-sum gives only
$|U_h(N)| \ll \sqrt{hN} + N/\sqrt h$, which at fixed $h = O(1)$ is $\ll N$.
No improvement over trivial sf-count.

(ii) **Empirical.** $|U_1(N)|/\sqrt N$ grows like $\sqrt N$ across
$N \in \{10^5, 10^6, 10^7\}$ (values $6.13, 17.72, 52.30$), refuting any
$|U_1| \ll \sqrt N \log^c N$ over the range.

(iii) **Cauchy–Schwarz transfer.** Even granting the counterfactual ideal
$U_h \ll 1$ rigorously, transferring to $T_h$ via the multiplicative weight
$\widetilde S_h(e)$ pays second-moment $\sum |\widetilde S_h|^2 \asymp N (\log N)^c$,
yielding only $|T_h| \ll \sqrt N \log^{c/2} N$ — *not* the empirically-needed
uniform $\sqrt N$.

**Implication for the proof of Lemma 3.4.** The $\sqrt N$ rate of $T_h(N)$
at small $h$ comes from cancellation in the *multiplicative root-weight*
$\widetilde S_h(e) = \prod_{p|e} 2\cos(2\pi h r_p/p)$, not from cancellation
in the $e$-direction phase. The binding analytic content is a Halász-type
bound on the multiplicative function $e \mapsto \widetilde S_h(e)$ twisted
by the phase $e^{2\pi i h N/e}$.

**Rigor accounting unchanged.** No new rigorous content. What changes is
the proof roadmap: a previously-promising route is closed, and the
remaining route is identified more sharply (Halász on $\widetilde S_h$,
not van der Corput on $U_h$).

## Consensus: WITH-CAVEAT

Skeptic accepts (after rewrites):

  ($|T_5(10^6)| = 267$ matches; sign convention etc. carried over).
- The B-process derivation in §2 is correct as a "technique-ceiling" statement.
- The Cauchy–Schwarz obstruction in §5 is correct.
- The conclusion "B-process route is too weak" is correctly drawn.

Caveats:
1. The asymptotic growth rate of $U_h(N)$ for fixed $h$ is *not* established.
   Empirically $|U_1|/N \in [0.016, 0.020]$ across $N \in [10^5, 10^7]$,
   compatible with $\Theta(N)$, $\Theta(N/\log N)$, or slow polylog decay.
   What is established empirically is $|U_1| \gg N^{1-o(1)}$ over the range,
   ruling out the specific $\sqrt N \log^c N$ rate.
2. The Cauchy–Schwarz transfer is the *natural* but not *unique* way to
   move bounds from $U_h$ to $T_h$. A clever multiplicative argument that
   bypasses CS is in principle possible.
3. Notation: $\widetilde S_h$ here vs $S_h^*$ in prev session — same object.
