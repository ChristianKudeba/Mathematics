## Decision

STRATEGY.md's promoted #1 was Hooley-style rigorization of $\sum \tau(n^2+1)^2 \sim c_3 N (\log N)^3$. Three previous sessions developed the formal Selberg–Delange identification of $G(s) = \zeta_K(s)^3 H(s)$ and computed closed-form Laurent coefficients $c_3, c_2, c_1$, but the leading **asymptotic itself remains conjectural** because the boundary-error analog of Hooley 1957 is not yet rigorized. I judged the full Hooley argument to be a multi-session task. I instead targeted the **rigorous order-of-magnitude upper bound** $S(N) \ll N (\log N)^3$ via a black-box appeal to Nair's theorem (1992 / Nair–Tenenbaum 1998 / Henriot 2012). This converts the order from "conjectural" to "rigorous" in one session and immediately gives a rigorous Cauchy–Schwarz upper bound $|T(N)| \ll N(\log N)^{3/2}$ on the σ-spin sum.

## What I did

1. **Set up Nair's bound.** Quoted the Nair-style theorem
$$\sum_{X<n\le X+y} f(F(n)) \ll y \cdot \prod_{p \le X^g}\big(1 - \rho_F(p)/p\big) \cdot \sum_{m \le X^g} \frac{f(m)\rho_F(m)}{m}$$
(form most-common in the modern literature; precise paper-by-paper bookkeeping varies, see citation caveat below).

2. **Verified hypotheses for $f = \tau^2$, $F = x^2+1$.**  $\tau^2$ multiplicative non-negative; $\tau^2(p^l) = (l+1)^2 \le 4^l$ (induction, $A_1 = 4$); $\tau^2(n) \le n^\epsilon$. $F$ irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}$, no fixed prime divisor ($F(0)=1$, $F(1)=2$). $\rho_F$: $\rho(2)=1$, $\rho(2^k)=0$ for $k\ge 2$, $\rho(p^k) = 0$ for $p \equiv 3(4)$, $\rho(p^k) = 2$ for $p \equiv 1(4)$, all $k \ge 1$.

3. **Identified the Dirichlet series.** Set $D(s) := \sum_m \tau^2(m) \rho_F(m)/m^s$. At $p \equiv 1(4)$: $D_p(s) = 2(1+p^{-s})/(1-p^{-s})^3 - 1$. Defined $H_0(s) := D(s)/\zeta_K(s)^4$. Verified — both algebraically and numerically via `bot/scratch/nair-upper-bound-verify.py` — that the local factor $H_{0,p}(s) = D_p(s) \cdot (1-p^{-s})^8$ at split primes has the universal expansion $1 + 0 \cdot p^{-s} - 18 p^{-2s} + 56 p^{-3s} - 80 p^{-4s} + \ldots$ (the $p^{-s}$ coefficient cancels: $8-8 = 0$). Hence $H_0(s)$ converges absolutely on $\Re s > 1/2$ and is analytic and nonzero at $s = 1$; so $D$ has pole order 4 at $s=1$.

4. **Bound the two factors.** $\mathcal{P}(N^2) := \prod_{p \le N^2}(1 - \rho_F(p)/p) \ll 1/\log N$ via Mertens for arithmetic progressions ($\sum_{p \equiv 1(4)} 1/p \sim \tfrac12 \log\log Y$). $\mathcal{M}(N^2) := \sum_{m \le N^2} \tau^2(m)\rho_F(m)/m \ll (\log N)^4$ via Selberg–Delange applied (after rewriting $\zeta_K^4 = \zeta^4 L(\chi_{-4})^4$) to $D = \zeta^4 \cdot G_0$ with $G_0 = L(\chi_{-4})^4 H_0$ regular and nonzero at $s=1$, giving $\sum_{m \le Y} \tau^2(m)\rho_F(m) \sim c_4 Y (\log Y)^3$ and then partial summation.

5. **Combined.** $S(N) \ll N \cdot (1/\log N) \cdot (\log N)^4 = N (\log N)^3$.

6. **Numerical sanity check.** Empirical $S(N)/(N(\log N)^3) \in [0.157, 0.262]$ for $N \in [10^3, 10^6]$ from previous-session data — bounded throughout, decreasing toward the conjectured leading constant $c_3 \approx 0.0796$.

7. **Wrote up:** `n2+1 ai thoughts/notes/proofs/P12-tau-squared-upper-bound-Nair.md`. Scratch: `bot/scratch/nair-upper-bound-verify.py` (verifies the universal $-18 p^{-2s}$ coefficient at all split primes).
