**This file proves:**
1. **Theorem 4 (Unique-prime refinement).** For every $(D3)$-tuple $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{T}(N)^4$ satisfying $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$, and for every prime $p$ with $|S_p((e_i))| = 1$:
   - $p \mid N^2 + 1$, and
   - the unique $i$ with $p \mid e_i$ has $a_i \equiv -N \pmod p$.
   Equivalently, setting $e_i^{(1)} := \prod_{p \in \mathrm{supp}(e_i):\,|S_p|=1} p$ and $e_i^{(\ge 2)} := e_i / e_i^{(1)}$:
   $$e_i^{(1)} \mid N^2 + 1, \qquad a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}.$$

2. **Corollary 7 (Structural decomposition).** Every $(D3)$-tuple decomposes uniquely as
   $$(e_i, a_i) \;=\; \bigl(e_i^{(1)} \cdot e_i^{(\ge 2)},\ \mathrm{CRT}(a_i^{(1)}, a_i^{(\ge 2)})\bigr), \qquad i = 1, 2, 3, 4,$$
   with $(e_i^{(1)})$ a 4-tuple of pairwise-coprime divisors of $N^2 + 1$ in $[1, N]$ and $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$.

3. **Theorem 5 (Per-shared-prime fiber-size bound).** For every shared prime $p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}((e_i))$, the equation $(\star\star)$ mod $p$ admits at most $2^{|S_p|-1}$ solutions in the joint binary signs $(\eta_i)_{i \in S_p}$.

4. **Corollary 8 (Count formula given shared structure).** For fixed $(e_i^{(1)})$ and fixed shared-prime structure $\sigma = (S_p)_{p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}}$, the number of $(D3)$-tuples is at most
   $$\prod_{p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}} 2^{|S_p|-1}.$$

**This file does not prove** the full $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \le K^2 \log^B N$ bound. The remaining task — bounding the sum over shared-prime structures $\sigma$ and over $(e_i^{(1)})$ — is the open chunk P16.1.b.

## 1. Setup recap

We adopt the notation of `P16.1-HE4-D2-pairwise-coprime.md` §1–2. In particular:

- $\mathcal{T}(N) = \{(e, a) : 2 \le e \le N,\ e \text{ sf-good odd},\ a \in \mathcal{R}(e)\}$, where $\mathcal{R}(e) = \{a \in \{0, \dots, e-1\} : a^2 \equiv -1 \pmod e\}$.
- $\beta_{e,a} = (N + a)/e \pmod 1$.
- For a tuple $(e_i)_{i=1}^4$ of sf-good odd integers and a prime $p$, $S_p((e_i)) := \{i : p \mid e_i\}$ and $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}((e_i)) := \{p : |S_p| \ge 2\}$.

The constraint $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$ is, after multiplying by $M := \mathrm{lcm}(e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4)$ and writing $L_i := M/e_i$,
$$
L_1(N + a_1) + L_2(N + a_2) - L_3(N + a_3) - L_4(N + a_4) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod M. \tag{$\star\star$}
$$

We assign signs $s_i \in \{+1, -1\}$: $s_1 = s_2 = +1$, $s_3 = s_4 = -1$, so $(\star\star)$ reads $\sum_i s_i L_i (N + a_i) \equiv 0 \pmod M$.

**Prime-power decomposition of $M$.** Since every $e_i$ is squarefree, the $p$-part of $M$ is $p$ if $p \in \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(e_i)$ and $1$ otherwise. For each $p$ with $|S_p| \ge 1$:
- $i \in S_p \iff p \mid e_i \iff p \nmid L_i$ (i.e., $L_i$ is a unit mod $p$).
- $i \notin S_p \iff p \nmid e_i \iff p \mid L_i$.

## 2. Theorem 4: unique-prime refinement

**Theorem 4.** Let $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{T}(N)^4$ satisfy $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$. For every prime $p$ with $|S_p((e_i))| = 1$ — say $S_p = \{i_0\}$ — we have $p \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_{i_0} \equiv -N \pmod p$.

*Proof.* Reduce $(\star\star)$ modulo $p$. By the prime-power decomposition above, for $i \ne i_0$ we have $p \mid L_i$, so each such term contributes $0 \pmod p$. The remaining term is
$$
s_{i_0} L_{i_0} (N + a_{i_0}) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod p.
$$
Since $L_{i_0}$ is a unit mod $p$ (as $i_0 \in S_p$) and $s_{i_0} \in \{\pm 1\}$ is a unit mod $p$ (since $p$ is odd), this gives $N + a_{i_0} \equiv 0 \pmod p$, i.e., $a_{i_0} \equiv -N \pmod p$.

Since $a_{i_0}^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{e_{i_0}}$ and $p \mid e_{i_0}$: $a_{i_0}^2 \equiv -1 \pmod p$, hence $N^2 \equiv (-a_{i_0})^2 \equiv -1 \pmod p$, i.e., $p \mid N^2 + 1$. $\square$

**Remark.** This generalizes the pairwise-coprime case (Theorem 3 of `P16.1`): in the pairwise-coprime situation every prime in $\bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(e_i)$ has $|S_p| = 1$, so Theorem 4 forces every $e_i$ to divide $N^2 + 1$ and $a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i}$, recovering Theorem 3.

## 3. Corollary 7: structural decomposition of $(D3)$-tuples

**Notation.** For a fixed tuple $(e_i)_{i=1}^4$, partition the support $\mathcal{P} := \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(e_i)$ by $S$-class:
$$
\mathcal{P} = \bigsqcup_{\emptyset \ne S \subseteq \{1,2,3,4\}} \mathcal{P}_S, \qquad \mathcal{P}_S := \{p \in \mathcal{P} : S_p = S\}.
$$
Define
$$
e_i^{(1)} := \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_{\{i\}}} p, \qquad e_i^{(\ge 2)} := \prod_{S \ni i,\, |S| \ge 2} \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_S} p.
$$
Then $e_i = e_i^{(1)} \cdot e_i^{(\ge 2)}$ with $\gcd(e_i^{(1)}, e_i^{(\ge 2)}) = 1$.

**Corollary 7.** Every $(D3)$-tuple $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4$ decomposes uniquely (via CRT on each $e_i = e_i^{(1)} \cdot e_i^{(\ge 2)}$) as
$$
a_i = \mathrm{CRT}\bigl(a_i^{(1)},\ a_i^{(\ge 2)}\bigr), \qquad a_i^{(1)} \in \mathcal{R}(e_i^{(1)}),\ a_i^{(\ge 2)} \in \mathcal{R}(e_i^{(\ge 2)}),
$$
with the following structural constraints:
(a) The 4-tuple $(e_i^{(1)})_{i=1}^4$ is pairwise coprime, each $e_i^{(1)}$ divides $N^2 + 1$, and $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$.
(b) The shared-prime data $(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ is described by a shared-prime structure $\sigma$: a finite collection $(\mathcal{P}_S)_{|S| \ge 2}$ of disjoint sets of sf-good odd primes, indexed by subsets $S \subseteq \{1,2,3,4\}$ of size $\ge 2$. Conversely, $\sigma$ determines $(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ via $e_i^{(\ge 2)} = \prod_{S \ni i} \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_S} p$.
(c) The shared residues $(a_i^{(\ge 2)})$ satisfy the joint constraint
$$
\sum_{i \in S_p} s_i L_i (N + \eta_{p, i} r_p) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod p \qquad \text{for every shared prime } p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}, \tag{$\diamond_p$}
$$
where $\eta_{p, i} \in \{+1, -1\}$ is the unique sign with $a_i^{(\ge 2)} \equiv \eta_{p, i} r_p \pmod p$.

*Proof.*
**Pairwise coprimality of $(e_i^{(1)})$:** if $p \mid \gcd(e_i^{(1)}, e_j^{(1)})$ for $i \ne j$, then $p$ divides both $e_i$ and $e_j$, so $|S_p| \ge 2$, contradicting $p \in \mathcal{P}_{\{i\}} \cap \mathcal{P}_{\{j\}}$ which forces $|S_p| = 1$. Hence $\gcd(e_i^{(1)}, e_j^{(1)}) = 1$.

**$e_i^{(1)} \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$:** Theorem 4 applied prime-by-prime to each $p \mid e_i^{(1)}$ gives $p \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_i \equiv -N \pmod p$; CRT combines these into $e_i^{(1)} \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$. Restricting $a_i$ via CRT to its $e_i^{(1)}$-part gives $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$.

**Shared-prime constraint $(\diamond_p)$:** For each $p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}$, reduce $(\star\star)$ mod $p$. Terms for $i \notin S_p$ vanish (since $p \mid L_i$). The remaining sum is
$$
\sum_{i \in S_p} s_i L_i (N + a_i) \equiv 0 \pmod p.
$$
For $i \in S_p$, $a_i \equiv \pm r_p \pmod p$ (since $a_i^2 \equiv -1 \pmod p$ for $p \mid e_i$); write the sign as $\eta_{p,i} \in \{\pm 1\}$, so $a_i \equiv \eta_{p,i} r_p \pmod p$. Substituting gives $(\diamond_p)$.

The decomposition is unique because CRT bijects $\mathcal{R}(e_i) \cong \mathcal{R}(e_i^{(1)}) \times \mathcal{R}(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$, and the data $((e_i^{(1)}), (e_i^{(\ge 2)}), (a_i^{(1)}), (a_i^{(\ge 2)}))$ recovers $((e_i, a_i))$ via $e_i = e_i^{(1)} e_i^{(\ge 2)}$ and CRT on $a_i$. $\square$

**Remark.** Pairwise-coprimality applies only to $(e_i^{(1)})$, not to $(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ — indeed by construction $(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ shares primes pairwise.

## 4. Theorem 5: per-shared-prime fiber bound

We now bound the number of valid $(\eta_{p,i})_{i \in S_p}$ for each shared prime $p$.

**Theorem 5.** Let $p$ be an odd sf-good prime with $|S_p| = k \ge 2$. Then the equation $(\diamond_p)$ — viewed as an equation in the binary unknowns $(\eta_{p,i})_{i \in S_p} \in \{\pm 1\}^k$ with $r_p$ and the coefficients $\{s_i L_i \bmod p : i \in S_p\}$ fixed — has at most $2^{k-1}$ solutions.

*Proof.* Expand $(\diamond_p)$:
$$
\Bigl(\sum_{i \in S_p} s_i L_i\Bigr) N \;+\; r_p \sum_{i \in S_p} s_i L_i \eta_{p,i} \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod p.
$$
This is an affine-linear equation in $(\eta_{p,i})_{i \in S_p}$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$:
$$
\sum_{i \in S_p} c_i \eta_{p,i} \;\equiv\; t \pmod p, \tag{$\diamond'_p$}
$$
where $c_i := s_i L_i \in \mathbb{F}_p^\times$ (since $L_i$ is a unit mod $p$ and $s_i = \pm 1 \ne 0$ mod $p$ odd) and $t := -r_p^{-1} N \sum_i s_i L_i \in \mathbb{F}_p$. Fix any $i_* \in S_p$. For each choice of $(\eta_{p,i})_{i \in S_p \setminus \{i_*\}} \in \{\pm 1\}^{k-1}$, the equation $(\diamond'_p)$ becomes
$$
c_{i_*} \eta_{p, i_*} \;\equiv\; t - \sum_{i \ne i_*} c_i \eta_{p, i} \pmod p.
$$
The right side is a specific element of $\mathbb{F}_p$; since $c_{i_*} \in \mathbb{F}_p^\times$, this determines $\eta_{p, i_*}$ uniquely as an element of $\mathbb{F}_p$. There is then *at most one* $\eta_{p, i_*} \in \{\pm 1\}$ matching (since $+1 \not\equiv -1 \pmod p$ for odd $p$). Hence the total count of solutions is at most $2^{k-1}$. $\square$

**Sharpness check.** The bound is achieved, e.g., in the $k = 2$ case when the resulting linear equation in $(\eta_{p,1}, \eta_{p,2})$ has a non-trivial kernel of size 2 (as in Step 4 of Theorem 2). For larger $k$, $2^{k-1}$ is generally not achieved — most generic configurations give a strictly smaller solution count — but $2^{k-1}$ is the worst case we will use.

## 5. Corollary 8: counting formula given shared-prime structure

**Corollary 8.** Fix a shared-prime structure $\sigma = (\mathcal{P}_S)_{|S| \ge 2}$ and an ordered 4-tuple $(e_i^{(1)})_{i=1}^4$ of pairwise-coprime divisors of $N^2 + 1$ such that $e_i^{(1)} e_i^{(\ge 2)} \le N$ for each $i$, where $e_i^{(\ge 2)} := \prod_{S \ni i} \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_S} p$. Let $N_\sigma((e_i^{(1)}))$ denote the number of $(D3)$-tuples $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4$ with this $(e_i^{(1)})$ and shared structure $\sigma$. Then
$$
N_\sigma((e_i^{(1)})) \;\le\; \prod_{p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma)} 2^{|S_p|-1},
$$
where $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma) := \bigcup_{|S| \ge 2} \mathcal{P}_S$ (the multiset is in fact a set, by disjointness of the $\mathcal{P}_S$).

*Proof.* By Corollary 7, every such $(D3)$-tuple is determined by:
- $(a_i^{(1)})$: forced to $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$, so 1 choice each, 1 choice total.
- $(\eta_{p,i})_{p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma),\, i \in S_p}$: by Theorem 5, the number of valid joint $\eta$-tuples is at most $\prod_p 2^{|S_p|-1}$ (the constraints $(\diamond_p)$ for distinct $p$ are independent — they live in disjoint mod-$p$ slices of $a_i^{(\ge 2)}$, recovered via CRT on $e_i^{(\ge 2)}$).

Multiplying gives the claimed bound. $\square$

**Comparison with the free count.** Without the $(\diamond_p)$ constraints, each $a_i^{(\ge 2)} \in \mathcal{R}(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ has $2^{\omega(e_i^{(\ge 2)})}$ choices independently, for a total free count of $\prod_i 2^{\omega(e_i^{(\ge 2)})} = 2^{\sum_i \omega(e_i^{(\ge 2)})} = 2^{\sum_{|S| \ge 2} |\mathcal{P}_S| \cdot |S|}$. The constrained count is $\prod_p 2^{|S_p|-1} = 2^{\sum_{|S| \ge 2} |\mathcal{P}_S| \cdot (|S| - 1)}$. The ratio (free / constrained) is $2^{\sum_{|S| \ge 2} |\mathcal{P}_S|} = 2^{|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma)|}$ — each shared prime cuts the free count by exactly factor 2 on average (and the worst-case fiber bound matches this).

## 6. The $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1$ warm-up sub-case (chunk P16.1.c, formulated)

The minimally-shared case is when $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1$: a single shared prime $p_0$ with $|S_{p_0}| \in \{2, 3, 4\}$. By Corollary 8, the count for fixed $(e_i^{(1)})$ and fixed $p_0$ (and fixed $S = S_{p_0}$) is $\le 2^{|S|-1}$.

Summing over $(e_i^{(1)})$ (pairwise-coprime divisors of $N^2+1$, each $\le N$), over the choice of $S \subseteq \{1,2,3,4\}$ with $|S| \ge 2$ (the underlying shape of $S_{p_0}$), and over the choice of $p_0$ (sf-good odd prime $\le N$ — $p_0$ may or may not divide $N^2+1$; the structural constraint is only that the assignment puts $p_0$ into $\mathcal{P}_S$, not into any $\mathcal{P}_{\{i\}}$):
$$
|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|_{|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1}(N) \;\le\; \tau(N^2+1)^4 \cdot \sum_{\substack{S \subseteq \{1,\dots,4\} \\ |S| \ge 2}} 2^{|S|-1} \cdot \pi^{\mathrm{sf\text{-}good}}(N),
$$
where $\pi^{\mathrm{sf\text{-}good}}(N)$ is the count of primes $p_0 \equiv 1 \pmod 4$ with $p_0 \le N$. The combinatorial factor is
$$
\sum_{\substack{S \subseteq \{1,\dots,4\} \\ |S| \ge 2}} 2^{|S|-1} \;=\; \binom{4}{2} \cdot 2 + \binom{4}{3} \cdot 4 + \binom{4}{4} \cdot 8 \;=\; 12 + 16 + 8 \;=\; 36.
$$
Hence
$$
|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|_{|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1}(N) \;\le\; 36 \cdot \tau(N^2+1)^4 \cdot \pi(N) \;=\; N^{1+o(1)}.
$$
**This is well below $K^2 \approx N^{2+o(1)}$**, confirming that the $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1$ regime is sub-leading relative to the HE-4 target.

## 8. Open chunk P16.1.b — full $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}| \le K^2 \log^B N$ bound

**Statement to prove.** $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \le C \cdot K(N)^2 \log^B N$ for absolute constants $C, B$.

**Reduction (by Corollaries 7-8).** It suffices to prove
$$
\sum_{\sigma} \sum_{(e_i^{(1)})} \prod_{p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma)} 2^{|S_p|-1} \;\le\; C \cdot K(N)^2 \log^B N,
$$
where the inner sum is over ordered 4-tuples of pairwise-coprime divisors of $N^2+1$ with $e_i^{(1)} e_i^{(\ge 2)}(\sigma) \le N$, and the outer sum is over shared-prime structures $\sigma$ with $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma) \subseteq \{p : p \text{ sf-good prime} \le N\}$.

**Sub-cases by $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}(\sigma)|$:**
- $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 0$: empty (this is the pairwise-coprime case, $S_3^{\mathrm{pwc}} = \emptyset$ by Corollary 5).
- $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1$: bounded by $N^{1+o(1)}$ in §6 above. Sub-leading.
- $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| \ge 2$: the genuinely hard case; chunk P16.1.b proper.

**Candidate technique.** For each $\sigma$ with $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = k \ge 2$, the count $\prod_p 2^{|S_p|-1}$ is essentially the count of shared "$\eta$-data" cut by the linear constraints. The constraint structure couples shared primes via the global equation $(\star\star)$; in particular, sums over $\sigma$ of $\prod_p 2^{|S_p|-1}$ are related to divisor-sum identities for $\tau_3$ and $\tau_4$. Heath-Brown's $\tau_3$ identity (e.g., 1986) and Hooley-style elementary $\tau_k$-moment bounds are the natural tools. The bound $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}| \le K^2 \log^B N$ requires the divisor sum $\sum_e \tau(e)^c / e$ to be tight at $c \le O(1)$, which is exactly what these tools give.

**Detailed plan for chunk P16.1.b.**

1. **P16.1.b.1.** Reformulate the outer sum as $\sum_\sigma \prod_p 2^{|S_p|-1} \cdot \#\{(e_i^{(1)}) : e_i^{(1)} \mid N^2+1, \, \mathrm{pwc}, \, e_i^{(1)} e_i^{(\ge 2)}(\sigma) \le N\}$.

2. **P16.1.b.2.** For fixed $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}|$, decouple the $\sigma$-sum into: (i) a sum over the partition $(\mathcal{P}_S)_{|S| \ge 2}$ of structure, and (ii) a sum over the specific primes within each class. The latter sum factors into prime sums.

3. **P16.1.b.3.** Bound the $\sigma$-sum at fixed prime count by $\tau_k$-style identities; show overall sum is $\le K^2 \log^B N$.

4. **P16.1.b.4.** Verify the $e_i^{(1)} e_i^{(\ge 2)} \le N$ constraint can be relaxed at sub-leading cost.
