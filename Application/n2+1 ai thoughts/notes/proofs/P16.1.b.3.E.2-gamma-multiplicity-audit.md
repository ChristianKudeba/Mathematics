### 2.3 Generic non-coalescence

For generic $(s_1,s_2,s_3)$ on the contour $(c+i\mathbb R)^3$ — i.e., outside the codim-1 subvariety where $\mathrm{Im}(s_i)=\mathrm{Im}(s_j)$ for some $i\ne j$ — the three poles in $s_4$ are pairwise distinct (same real part $1-c$ but distinct imaginary parts). Coalescence is measure-zero on the outer contour; Cauchy's residue theorem applies pointwise off this measure-zero set and the formula extends to the full contour integration by integrability of the integrand.

### 2.4 Symmetry argument: equality, not over-counting

The integrand $I(\vec s)$ is $S_4$-symmetric in $(s_1,s_2,s_3,s_4)$. Restrict to $S_3$-symmetry on $(s_1,s_2,s_3)$ with $s_4$ played as the residue variable. Hence $R^{(4)}_k = R^{(4)}_l$ for all $k,l\in\{1,2,3\}$ AS NUMBERS.

The factor 3 in the SUM $\sum_{k=1}^3 R^{(4)}_k = 3\,R^{(4)}$ is the NUMBER OF DISTINCT POLES, not a symmetry-double-counting factor. **Not an over-count.**

The three $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_k+s_4)^2$ factors are independent in $s_4$: their pole locations $s_4=1-s_k$ depend on three independent parameters $s_1,s_2,s_3$. ## 6. Structural pre-derivation for $R^{(4)}_\delta$

$R^{(4)}_\delta$ is the residual 3-fold integral after extracting the $s_4=1-s_3$ residue:

$$
R^{(4)}_\delta(N) \;=\; \kappa\,N\cdot\frac{1}{(2\pi i)^3}\int_{(c+\eta)^2\times(\delta)} N^{s_1+s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(s_1)\widehat\Psi(s_2)\widehat\Psi(s_3)\widehat\Psi(1-s_3)\,G^{(3)}\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(3)}\,d^3 s.

To extract $\Lambda^2$-at-$N^2$, we need to perform further residue extractions producing $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^6$ (or equivalent triple-pole) structure at some $s_*=1/2$ slice.

**Sketched path.** Shift $s_1: (c+\eta)\to(\delta)$. The strip $[\delta, c+\eta]$ in $s_1$ contains the pole $s_1 = 1-s_2$ (at $\mathrm{Re}=1-c-\eta$). The diagonal pole $s_1 = s_3$ is at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1) = \delta$ (left boundary; handled by stratifying $s_3$ further to $(\delta-\eta')$, see b.3.A §4.1 for the analogous trick). The pole $s_1 + s_3 = 1$ is at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1) = 1-\delta > c+\eta$, outside.

Residue at $s_1 = 1-s_2$ extracts $\kappa$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2$ and substitutes:

- $\widehat\Psi(s_1) \to \widehat\Psi(1-s_2)$, $N^{s_1+s_2} \to N$.

- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_3)^2$ unchanged (pole at $s_2=s_3$).

Total $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ exponent in the integrand: $2+2+2+2 = 8$. So we have $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^8$ structure spread over two distinct codim-1 pole loci $\{s_2+s_3=1\}$ and $\{s_2=s_3\}$, each of order 4 ($\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4$).

**Two pole loci intersect at $s_2=s_3=1/2$.** This is the candidate $\Lambda^2$ source. ### 6.1 Cascade to $\Lambda^2$

A residue at $s_2 = 1-s_3$ (collapsing the pair-sum codim-1) would substitute $s_2 \to 1-s_3$ and produce $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4$-type residue with $\Lambda^1$ extraction (similar to b.3.C). Alternatively, a residue at $s_2 = s_3$ (collapsing the diagonal codim-1) first, then at $s_2 = s_3 = 1/2$ via $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2$ structure analogous to b.3.B Theorem 11.

Both paths produce $\Lambda^2$-at-$N^2$. The coefficient depends on detailed Laurent expansion; the rough expected magnitude is $\sim \kappa^6\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^4 H(\vec{1/2})\prod_p[\ldots]\cdot N^2\Lambda^2$ with sign determined by the leading $-\kappa^2$ on each double-pole-product.

### 6.2 Sign prediction

The leading sign on each $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4$ product is $-$ (from the conjugate-pair structure $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-w) \sim -\kappa^2/(w-1)^2$). Two such products contribute $(-1)\cdot(-1) = +$, so the bare residue integrand has $+\kappa^4$ leading at the codim-2 intersection. 

The contour-shift sign convention: leftward $s_1$-shift in $R^{(4)}_\delta$ picks up the residue at $s_1 = 1-s_2$ with sign $+$ (leftward strip ENCLOSES the pole counterclockwise — see b.3.A §5 sign tally, also note discussion in b.3.E.1 §5 for the opposite sign of rightward shifts).

Net expected sign: $+\kappa^6$ ... but the cancellation prediction needed $-\kappa^6$. **The sign expected from this rough sketch does NOT match the prediction.**

There are several possible reconciliations:

(i) The numerical $\kappa^2$-vs-$\kappa^4$ counting above is sketchy; one residue stage may halve a factor (e.g., $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2 s_*)^2$ residue is $\kappa/2$, not $\kappa$). After full bookkeeping the magnitude could change.

(ii) An additional rightward-shift step (analogous to b.3.E.1 §5's rightward $s_4=1/2$ extraction) may flip the sign.

(iii) The path enumeration above is incomplete; there may be MULTIPLE iterated-residue paths contributing to $\Lambda^2$ in $R^{(4)}_\delta$, and the SUM (not any single path's contribution) is what matters.

(iv) Most likely possibility: the sign sketch above is wrong by a factor of $-1$ from an unconsidered Jacobian or contour-orientation, and the correct answer is $-\kappa^6$ as needed.

A full rigorous derivation (b.3.E.3) is required to settle the sign. The sketch above suffices for this audit (γ) chunk: $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta]$ is non-trivial and is the structural location of the missing piece.
