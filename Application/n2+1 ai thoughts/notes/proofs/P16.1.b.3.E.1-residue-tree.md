# P16.1.b.3.E.1. Residue tree of $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}(N)$: enumeration of crossed hyperplanes

## Status

First sub-chunk of P16.1.b.3.E. Predecessor `P16.1.b.3.D-s2-shift.md` extracted the second $\log N$ at the $N^2(\log N)^2$ tier inside the residue term $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\mathrm{Res}}$, with strictly-positive coefficient $\frac{\kappa^6}{4}\widehat\Psi(1/2)^4 H(\vec{1/2})\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}[1+4p^{-1/2}]$. The b.3.D §11 sign-cancellation conjecture (route (a)) proposes that an opposite-sign $\Lambda^2$-piece arises from the residual integral $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}$ when the remaining three contours $s_1,s_2,s_3$ are shifted from $(c)$ to $(\delta)$, and that the two $\Lambda^2$-pieces sum to zero.

**Deliverable of this chunk.** Inventory of all hyperplanes crossed by the iterated shift $(c)^3\times(\delta)\to(\delta)^4$ in $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}$, identification of branches that can produce $(\log N)^2$ at the $N^2$-tier, and a structural prediction for the leading $\Lambda^2$-coefficient of those branches.

**This chunk does NOT yet evaluate residues.** It enumerates the tree, computes pole locations, and identifies which branches are at the $N^2$-tier. Residue evaluation is deferred to b.3.E.2.

We use $\Lambda:=\log N$ and $u:=$ (shift variable $-1/2$) throughout. Substrate facts: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2$ has a unique simple pole at $w=1$ with residue $\kappa>0$ (b.3.A Lemma 14); $H(\vec s)$ is $S_4$-symmetric and holomorphic in $\min_i\mathrm{Re}(s_i)>1/3$ (b.3.A §3); $\mathcal{D}_d$ is holomorphic.

## 1. Setup

From b.3.A Theorem 10:
$$
\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}(N) \;=\; \frac{1}{(2\pi i)^4}\int_{(c)^3\times(\delta)} I(\vec s)\,d^4 s,\quad I(\vec s)=N^{\sum_i s_i}\Bigl[\prod_i\widehat\Psi(s_i)\Bigr]\bigl(\mathcal{D}_F^{\mathcal{F}}(\vec s)-1\bigr)\mathcal{D}_d(\vec s),
$$
with $\mathcal{D}_F^{\mathcal{F}}(\vec s)=\bigl[\prod_{1\le i<j\le 4}\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_i+s_j)^2\bigr]H(\vec s)$ and contours $c=1/2+\epsilon_0\in(1/2,2/3)$, $\delta\in(1/3,1-c)\subset(1/3,1/2-\epsilon_0)$.

**Goal.** Shift sequentially $s_3:(c)\to(\delta)$, then $s_2:(c)\to(\delta)$, then $s_1:(c)\to(\delta)$. By $S_3$-symmetry of $I$ in $(s_1,s_2,s_3)$ (all three on the same contour $(c)$) we may permute the labels freely; the order $s_3$–$s_2$–$s_1$ is a convention.

**Convention.** "Original-type" pole: from a factor $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_i+s_j)^2$ where both $s_i,s_j$ are integration variables, located at $s_i+s_j=1$. "Diagonal-type" pole: emerges from a substitution $s_a\to 1-s_b$ that converts $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_a+s_c)^2$ into $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_c+1-s_b)^2$, with pole at $s_c=s_b$.

## 2. Round 1: shift $s_3:(c)\to(\delta)$

Hold $s_1,s_2$ on $(c)$ and $s_4$ on $(\delta)$. Strip $\mathrm{Re}(s_3)\in[\delta,c]$.

**Pole inventory of $I$ in $s_3$, inside the strip.** Each $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_i+s_j)^2$ factor of $\mathcal{D}_F^{\mathcal{F}}$ that involves $s_3$ has a candidate pole at $s_i+s_j=1$:

| factor | pole in $s_3$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_3)$ at pole | crossed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_3)^2$ | $s_3=1-s_1$ | $1-c\in(\delta,c)$ | YES |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_3)^2$ | $s_3=1-s_2$ | $1-c\in(\delta,c)$ | YES |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_3+s_4)^2$ | $s_3=1-s_4$ | $1-\delta>c$ | NO |
| $\widehat\Psi(s_3)$ | $s_3=0$ | $0<\delta$ | NO |

Factors $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_4)^2$ are constants in $s_3$; $H$ and $\mathcal{D}_d$ are holomorphic; the "$-1$" piece is holomorphic in $s_3$.

**Round-1 result.** Two simple poles crossed, at $s_3=1-s_1$ and $s_3=1-s_2$.

**Symmetry factor.** The integrand is $S_3$-symmetric in $(s_1,s_2,s_3)$, hence $S_2$-symmetric in $(s_1,s_2)$ after fixing the role of $s_3$. The transposition $s_1\leftrightarrow s_2$ maps one residue to the other and leaves the outer contour invariant, so $\mathrm{Res}_{s_3=1-s_1}[\cdot]$ and $\mathrm{Res}_{s_3=1-s_2}[\cdot]$ produce equal $(s_1,s_2,s_4)$-integrals after relabeling.

**Identity (Round 1).** $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}=\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,\delta,\delta)}+2\,S(N)$, where
$$
S(N)\;:=\;\frac{1}{(2\pi i)^3}\int_{(c)^2\times(\delta)}\mathrm{Res}_{s_3=1-s_1}I(\vec s)\,ds_1\,ds_2\,ds_4
$$
and $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,\delta,\delta)}$ is the 4-fold integral on the contour $(c,c,\delta,\delta)$.

**Residue at $s_3=1-s_1$.** By b.3.A Theorem 9 (with $s_4\to s_3$, $k=1$):
$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_3=1-s_1}I \;=\; \kappa\cdot N\cdot N^{s_2+s_4}\,\widehat\Psi(s_1)\widehat\Psi(s_2)\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\widehat\Psi(s_4)\,G^{(\mathrm{I})}(s_1,s_2,s_4)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(\mathrm{I})}(s_1,s_2,s_4)
$$
where
$$
G^{(\mathrm{I})}(s_1,s_2,s_4)\;:=\;\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_4)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2\,H(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_4),
$$
$$
\mathcal{D}_d^{(\mathrm{I})}(s_1,s_2,s_4)\;:=\;\mathcal{D}_d(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_4)\;=\;\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}\bigl[1+p^{-s_1}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-(1-s_1)}+p^{-s_4}\bigr].
$$

The two **new diagonal hyperplanes** introduced by the substitution $s_3=1-s_1$ are:
- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$: pole at $s_2=s_1$;
- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2$: pole at $s_1=s_4$.

(The original-type pole $s_1+s_2=1$ is still present via the unchanged factor $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2$; similarly $s_1+s_4=1$ and $s_2+s_4=1$.)

## 3. Round 2: shift $s_2:(c)\to(\delta)$ inside the Round-1 residue $S(N)$

Hold $s_1$ on $(c)$, $s_4$ on $(\delta)$. Strip $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)\in[\delta,c]$.

**Pole inventory of the Round-1 residue integrand in $s_2$.**

| factor | pole in $s_2$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)$ at pole | type | crossed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2$ | $s_2=1-s_1$ | $1-c\in(\delta,c)$ | original | YES |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ | $s_2=s_1$ | $c$ | diagonal (Round-1) | boundary — see §3.1 |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_4)^2$ | $s_2=1-s_4$ | $1-\delta>c$ | original | NO |
| $\widehat\Psi(s_2)$ | $s_2=0$ | $0<\delta$ | — | NO |

The diagonal pole at $s_2=s_1$ sits exactly on the initial contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=c$. Per b.3.A §10 the cleanest resolution is to perturb $s_1$ onto a slightly smaller contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c-\eta$ for fixed small $\eta>0$ (chosen so $c-\eta>1-c+\eta$, i.e., $\eta<\epsilon_0$, so the two crossed poles $s_2=1-s_1$ and $s_2=s_1$ are separated by $2(c-\eta)-1>0$ in real part inside the new strip $[\delta,c-\eta]$ which still contains $1-c+\eta=1-(c-\eta)$). With this $\eta$-perturbation:

| factor | pole in $s_2$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)$ at pole | type | crossed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2$ | $s_2=1-s_1$ | $1-(c-\eta)=1-c+\eta\in(\delta,c-\eta)$ | original | YES |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ | $s_2=s_1$ | $c-\eta$ | diagonal | YES — endpoint, pole just inside strip on the right |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_4)^2$ | $s_2=1-s_4$ | $1-\delta>c-\eta$ | original | NO |

Two poles crossed in $s_2$: one original-type ($s_2=1-s_1$) and one diagonal-type ($s_2=s_1$). **Crucially, these two poles can COALESCE** when $s_1=1/2$, since $1-s_1=s_1$ exactly there. The contour at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c-\eta>1/2$ keeps them separated by $|s_1-(1-s_1)|=|2s_1-1|\ge 2(c-\eta-1/2)=2(\epsilon_0-\eta)>0$ in real part — but their imaginary parts coincide along the codimension-1 subvariety $\mathrm{Im}(s_1)=0$.

Real-part separation rules out a coalescence at the residue extraction; imaginary-part coincidence does NOT cause a coalescence because the pole locations $s_2=1-s_1$ and $s_2=s_1$ differ by $2s_1-1\ne 0$ in real part along the contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c-\eta$. The two residues are evaluated independently.

(*Subtlety to track.* The coalescence threat shifts to Round 3, where the iterated residue at $(s_2=1-s_1, s_3=1-s_1)$ produces $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2$, exactly the $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ factor that drove the $s_1\to 1/2$ pole in b.3.C. The $N^2(\log N)^2$ tier in b.3.D came from a triple pole at $s_2=1/2$ of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^6$; the analogous mechanism here will be a triple pole at $s_1=1/2$ in the Round-3 integrand.)

### 3.1. Two Round-2 branches

**Branch A (original-type, $s_2=1-s_1$).** Substitute $s_2\to 1-s_1$ in the Round-1 residue. The new integrand carries:

- Factor $\kappa$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2$ residue.
- $N$-power $N\cdot N^{s_2+s_4}|_{s_2=1-s_1}=N\cdot N^{1-s_1+s_4}=N^{2-s_1+s_4}$.
- Pole transformations:
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2$: pole at $s_1=1/2$, simple.
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_4)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2$: pole at $s_1=s_4$ (already present).
  - $\widehat\Psi(s_2)\to\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)$.
- Remaining holomorphic factors: $H, \mathcal{D}_d, \widehat\Psi(s_1), \widehat\Psi(1-s_1)^2, \widehat\Psi(s_4)$, and original-type $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2$, $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2$ (the latter is the substituted $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_3+s_4)^2$ from Round 1, unchanged by Round 2).

**Branch B (diagonal, $s_2=s_1$).** Substitute $s_2\to s_1$ in the Round-1 residue. New integrand carries:

- Factor $\kappa$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ residue. (The Jacobian $d(s_2+1-s_1)/ds_2=1$, so residue equals $\kappa$.)
- $N$-power $N\cdot N^{s_2+s_4}|_{s_2=s_1}=N\cdot N^{s_1+s_4}=N^{1+s_1+s_4}$.
- Pole transformations:
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$: pole at $s_1=1/2$, simple.
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_4)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2$, but $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2$ is already a factor — so we now have $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^4$ (squared!).
  - $\widehat\Psi(s_2)\to\widehat\Psi(s_1)$, so $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2$ now.

The $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^4$ has a double pole at $s_1+s_4=1$. Since $\mathrm{Re}(s_4)=\delta$, this pole in $s_1$ is at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=1-\delta>c-\eta$, OUTSIDE the strip. Not crossed in Round 3.

But it WILL be crossed when we shift $s_4$ later (if at all — currently $s_4$ is on $(\delta)$ and stays there).

### 3.2. Round-2 identity

$$
S(N)\;=\;S_{(c,\delta,\delta,\delta)}(N) \;+\;S_A(N)\;+\;S_B(N),
$$
where $S_{(c,\delta,\delta,\delta)}$ is the residual 3-fold integral on the partially-shifted contour, $S_A$ is Branch A's residue integral, $S_B$ is Branch B's residue integral.

## 4. Round 3: shift $s_1:(c-\eta)\to(\delta)$ in each branch

For each of Branches A, B, examine pole structure in $s_1$.

### 4.1. Branch A pole inventory in $s_1$

After Branch A substitution, the integrand contains factors (in $s_1$):
$\widehat\Psi(s_1)\,\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)^2\,\widehat\Psi(s_4)\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2\,H(s_1,1-s_1,1-s_1,s_4)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(A)}$.

| factor | pole in $s_1$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)$ at pole | type | crossed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\widehat\Psi(s_1)$ | $s_1=0$ | $0<\delta$ | — | NO |
| $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)^2$ | $s_1=1$ | $1>c-\eta$ | — | NO |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2$ | $s_1=1/2$ | $1/2\in(\delta,c-\eta)$ | **diagonal-pair** | YES |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2$ | $s_1=1-s_4$ | $1-\delta>c-\eta$ | original | NO |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2$ | $s_1=s_4$ | $\delta$ | diagonal (Round-1) | boundary — endpoint of strip on the LEFT |

The pole $s_1=s_4$ sits exactly at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=\delta$. To avoid it lying on the new contour, perturb $s_4$ to $(\delta-\eta')$ for some $\eta'>0$ (still in the allowed range $\eta'<1-c-\delta$, so $\mathrm{Re}(s_4)=\delta-\eta'\in(1/3,\delta)$). Then $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=\delta-\eta'$ at the pole, which lies LEFT of the new contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=\delta$. **Not crossed.**

(Alternative: leave $s_4$ on $(\delta)$ and shift $s_1$ from $(c-\eta)$ to $(\delta+\eta'')$ for $\eta''$ small; then the pole at $s_1=s_4$ has $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=\delta<\delta+\eta''$, outside the strip. Same conclusion.)

**Endpoint-pole deformation lemma (used for Round 2 Branch B and analogously for Round 3 boundaries).** When a pole sits at the right (resp. left) endpoint of the shift strip $[\delta,c-\eta]$ in some variable, we replace the rectangular shift contour by an indented rectangle that goes around the pole with a small semicircle on the LEFT (resp. RIGHT) of the pole. This indented contour encloses the pole (resp. excludes it) by standard $\epsilon\to 0$ residue-theorem argument. For Round 2 Branch B (pole at $s_2=s_1$, $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c-\eta$): indenting the new $\delta$-contour to the LEFT around the pole gives a contour deformation $[\delta,c-\eta]$ that encloses the pole — equivalent to perturbing $s_1$ to $(c-\eta-\eta''')$ for arbitrarily small $\eta'''>0$ and taking $\eta'''\to 0^+$. The residue is recovered with sign $+$. *No additional contour technicality once this standard indentation is invoked.*

**Round 3 (Branch A): one pole crossed, at $s_1=1/2$, from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2$.**

This pole is a simple pole. Its residue carries:
- Factor $\kappa\cdot d(2-2s_1)/ds_1|_{s_1=1/2}/|-2|=\kappa/2$ — wait, more carefully: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2$ near $s_1=1/2$: $w:=2-2s_1$, $w-1=-2(s_1-1/2)=-2u_1$. So $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_1)^2=\kappa/(-2u_1)+\gamma_0+\ldots=-\kappa/(2u_1)+\gamma_0+\ldots$ The residue in $s_1$ at $s_1=1/2$ is $-\kappa/2$.

After substituting $s_1=1/2$:
- $N$-power $N^{2-s_1+s_4}|_{s_1=1/2}=N^{3/2+s_4}$.
- $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)^2\to\widehat\Psi(1/2)^2$; $\widehat\Psi(s_1)\to\widehat\Psi(1/2)$.
- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^2$.
- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^2$ — coalesces with the previous factor!

So we get $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^4$, a **double pole in $s_4$ at $s_4=1/2$** (not quadruple — $(\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2)^2$ has order-2 pole). This is part of the residual $s_4$-integrand.

**Branch A leading $N$-power on $(\delta)$.** $N^{3/2}\cdot N^{s_4}$ on $\mathrm{Re}(s_4)=\delta$, so $N^{3/2+\delta}\le N^{11/6+}$ pointwise — $o(N^2)$ on the current contour.

**[Updated, addressing skeptic C1]: Branch A's $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4$ at $s_4=1/2$ produces a $\Lambda^1$ piece at $N^2$ under a rightward $s_4$-shift.** Under a rightward $s_4$-shift from $(\delta)$ to $(\delta_R)$ with $\delta_R>1/2$, the integrand picks up $(-1)\times\mathrm{Res}_{s_4=1/2}$ of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^4 G(s_4)$ where $G(s_4):=N^{s_4}\widehat\Psi(s_4)H(1/2,1/2,1/2,s_4)\mathcal{D}_d^{(A)}(s_4)$ holomorphic at $s_4=1/2$. With $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^4=\kappa^2/u_4^2+2\kappa\gamma_0/u_4+O(1)$:
$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_4=1/2}\bigl[\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^4 G(s_4)\bigr]=\kappa^2 G'(1/2)+2\kappa\gamma_0 G(1/2),
$$
with $G'(1/2)=N^{1/2}(\Lambda K_0^{(A)}+K_1^{(A)})$ and $K_0^{(A)}=\widehat\Psi(1/2)H(\vec{1/2})\prod_p[1+4p^{-1/2}]$ (matching b.3.D's K_0). After combining with Branch A's full prefactor (Round 1 $S_2$-mult 2, Round 1 residue $\kappa$, Round 2 Branch A residue $\kappa$, Round 3 residue $-\kappa/2$, $\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3$, $N$ from Round 1, $N^{1/2}$ from $s_1=1/2$ evaluation, $N^{1/2}$ from $s_4=1/2$ residue: total $N\cdot N^{1/2}\cdot N^{1/2}=N^2$). Numerical-coefficient $(-1)\cdot 2\cdot(-\kappa/2)\cdot\kappa\cdot\kappa^2=+\kappa^4$.

$$
\boxed{\;\text{Branch A's rightward $\Lambda^1$-at-$N^2$ piece} \;=\; +\kappa^4\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^4\,H(\vec{1/2})\,\prod_p[1+4p^{-1/2}]\cdot N^2\Lambda.\,}
$$

**This is a $\Lambda^1$-tier contribution, not $\Lambda^2$.** Lives at $N^2\Lambda$ sub-leading tier; relevant for $N^2\log N$ cancellation with b.3.D subleading + $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$ pieces, but does not address $N^2\Lambda^2$. **Branch A's rightward $\Lambda^2$-at-$N^2$ contribution is zero** (only $\Lambda^1$ via $G'(1/2)$).

So **the leading $N^2(\log N)^2$ cancellation must still come from Branch B** (the only $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^6$-triple-pole branch).

### 4.2. Branch B pole inventory in $s_1$

After Branch B substitution, integrand contains (in $s_1$):
$\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\,\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\,\widehat\Psi(s_4)\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^4\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2\,H(s_1,s_1,1-s_1,s_4)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(B)}$.

| factor | pole in $s_1$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)$ | type | crossed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2$ | $s_1=0$ | $0$ | — | NO |
| $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)$ | $s_1=1$ | $1$ | — | NO |

| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2$ | $s_1=s_4$ | $\delta$ | diagonal | NO (with $\eta'$-perturbation) |

**Round 3 (Branch B): one pole crossed, at $s_1=1/2$, simple, from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$.**

Residue at $s_1=1/2$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$ near $s_1=1/2$: $w=2s_1$, $w-1=2u_1$, residue in $s_1$ is $\kappa/2$.

After $s_1=1/2$: $N^{1+s_1+s_4}|_{s_1=1/2}=N^{3/2+s_4}$; $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\to\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3$; $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_4)^4\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^4$; $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1-s_1+s_4)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^2$. Total $\zeta$-power in $s_4$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^6$.

**This is exactly the structure of $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$ in b.3.D!** Triple pole at $s_4=1/2$.

Branch B leading $N$-power: $N^{3/2}\cdot N^{s_4}$, with $s_4=1/2$ pole at $\mathrm{Re}=1/2>\delta$, to the RIGHT of $(\delta)$ contour. **Not crossed by leftward shift.** Branch B residue is the 1-fold integral
$$
S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}(N)\;=\;C_B\cdot N^{3/2}\,\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\delta)}N^{s_4}\,\widehat\Psi(s_4)\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^6\,H(1/2,1/2,1/2,s_4)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(B,1)}(s_4)\,ds_4
$$
where $\mathcal{D}_d^{(B,1)}(s_4)=\prod_p[1+3p^{-1/2}+p^{-s_4}]$. $C_B=(\kappa/2)\cdot\kappa\cdot\kappa\cdot\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\cdot 2$.

**Comparison to b.3.D.** Same form as $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$, but $s_4$-contour on $(\delta)<1/2$ instead of $(c_2)>1/2$.

**Critical observation.** Triple pole sits RIGHT of the $(\delta)$ contour. To extract its $\Lambda^2$ contribution, shift $s_4$ RIGHTWARD past $s_4=1/2$, with sign $-$.

## 5. Triple-pole extraction: Branch B's $\Lambda^2$ leading coefficient

To extract the $\Lambda^2$-tier from $S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}$, shift the $s_4$-contour RIGHTWARD past $s_4=1/2$. Triple-pole residue at $s_4=1/2$ via b.3.D Theorem 21 mechanism (apply $A\cdot G''(1/2)/2$ with $A=\kappa^3$ leading Laurent coefficient of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^6$):

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_4=1/2}\,[\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(1/2+s_4)^6 G(s_4)] \;\sim\; \frac{\kappa^3 K_0^{(B)}}{2}\cdot N^{1/2}\Lambda^2,
$$
