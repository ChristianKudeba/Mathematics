## 4. The bookkeeping error — piece A vs piece B

The b.3.E.1 §5 calculation treats "$-\kappa^6/2 K^* N^2 \Lambda^2$" as if it were $\Lambda^2[\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}]$. We show this is wrong.

### 4.1 Absolute size bound on $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}$

Direct from the integral definition:
$$
|\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}(N)|\;\le\;\sup_{(c)^3\times(\delta)}|N^{s_1+s_2+s_3+s_4}|\cdot\int|\text{integrand without }N\text{-power}|\;\le\;N^{3c+\delta}\cdot C\cdot 5^{\omega(N^2+1)}.
$$
With $c=1/2+\epsilon_0$, $\delta=1/3+\epsilon_0$ (boundary values), $3c+\delta = 11/6 + 4\epsilon_0$. **Strictly $<2$ for $\epsilon_0 < 1/24$.** So $|\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}| \le C N^{11/6 + 4\epsilon_0 + o(1)}$, which is $o(N^{2-\eta'})$ for some $\eta'>0$ (depending on $\epsilon_0$).

**Therefore $\Lambda^2[\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}] = 0$ at the $N^2(\log N)^2$ tier**, by direct size bound. This is the "$<N^2$ tier" classification correctly applied in b.3.D §10's table.

### 4.2 Why b.3.E.1's "$-\kappa^6/2 K^*$" is not $\Lambda^2[\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}]$

The b.3.E.1 derivation extracted the residue from the rightward $s_4$-shift in Branch B's residual integrand $S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}(N)$. By Cauchy:
$$
S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}(\delta)\;=\;\underbrace{S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}(\delta_R)}_{\text{piece B}}\;+\;\underbrace{(-\mathrm{Res}_{s_4=1/2})}_{\text{piece A}=-\kappa^6/2\,K^* N^2\Lambda^2 + \cdots}.
$$

LHS is the $S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}$ originally defined on $(\delta)$, with absolute size $|N^{3/2+s_4}|_{(\delta)} = N^{3/2+\delta} \le N^{11/6+\epsilon}$ — **strictly sub-$N^2$.** Hence LHS at $\Lambda^2 N^2$ tier is **zero**.

Therefore: **piece A + piece B = 0** at $\Lambda^2 N^2$ tier. So **piece B = $+\kappa^6/2 K^* N^2 \Lambda^2$.**

The b.3.E.1 derivation implicitly DROPPED piece B (claiming the rightward-shifted integral on $(\delta_R)$ is "an absolute integral" without computing its $\Lambda^2$ contribution). But piece B has a non-zero $\Lambda^2$-tier coefficient — equal in magnitude and OPPOSITE in sign to piece A.

### 4.3 What number to put in the b.3.E.3 §5.3 tally

The b.3.E.3 §5.3 row "$\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}: -\kappa^6/2\,K^*$" is incorrect. ## 8. Caveats

(i) The corrected tally still relies on b.3.E.3 Theorem 25's claim $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)}] = 0$. The b.3.E.3 §4.3 proof argues this via "no residue extracted, $N$-power $N^{1+2(c+\eta)}$ has no $\log N$ factor, so $N^2(\log N)^2$ tier is empty." But the size bound $N^{1+2(c+\eta)} > N^2$ — strictly above $N^2$, since $c=1/2+\epsilon_0$ and $\eta>0$ are positive. The "$\Lambda^2 = 0$" conclusion uses the SAME class of heuristic the audit just rejected for $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}$. **If applied consistently, the $-\kappa^6 K^*$ figure for $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta]$ is itself unproven**, and so the corrected $-3\kappa^6 K^*/2$ tally is no more rigorous than the b.3.E.4 figure $-2\kappa^6 K^*$ it replaces. The qualitative conclusion (working hypothesis $\Lambda^2[\mathcal{B}^\sharp]=0$ fails, escalate to Anton) survives only because BOTH numbers are non-zero — but neither is rigorously established. **A complete audit would re-derive $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta]$ by the same standard applied to $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}$.**

(ii) The "$\Lambda^k$ as coefficient of $N^2(\log N)^k$" framework is genuinely fragile when applied across pieces of different $N$-power tiers. The b.3.E.1 / b.3.E.3 confusion arose from comparing $\Lambda^k$-coefficients across pieces that don't all live at the $N^2$ tier. A clean reformulation would express each piece as $C \cdot N^{a} (\log N)^k + \text{lower}$ and only combine pieces with the same $a$.

(iii) **Conflation of two distinct objects in §4.** The §4.1 size bound $N^{3c+\delta} < N^{2-\eta'}$ applies to $\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}$ on contour $(c)^3 \times (\delta)$. The §4.2 piece-A/piece-B Cauchy identity applies to $S_B^{(\mathrm{res})}$, a 1-fold integral on $(\delta)$ in $s_4$ obtained AFTER Round 3's $s_1=1/2$ residue extraction (i.e., $s_1$ on perturbed contour $(c-\eta)$, then leftward shift). Both objects are $o(N^2)$, but for different reasons. The argument logic is: §4.1 establishes $\Lambda^2[\mathcal{B}^\sharp_{(c,c,c,\delta)}] = 0$ directly; §4.2 explains why b.3.E.1's "$-\kappa^6/2 K^*$" extraction is consistent with this (piece A + piece B = 0 at $N^2 \Lambda^2$ tier).
