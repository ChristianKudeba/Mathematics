## 2. Theorem 26: $\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}] = 0$

**Theorem 26.** $\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}] = 0$.

**Proof.** We bound $R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N)$ in absolute value uniformly in $N$ and conclude that it is $o(N^2)$, which forces all $N^2$-tier asymptotic coefficients (including $\Lambda^k$ for $k=0,1,2$) to vanish.

**Step 1: Absolute convergence + size bound on contour.**

Fix any valid pair $(\delta', c_2)$ with $\delta' \in (1/3, 1-c_2)$ and $c_2 \in (1/2, 2/3)$. On the contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1) = \delta'$, $\mathrm{Re}(s_2) = c_2$:

- $|N^{s_1+s_2}| = N^{\delta'+c_2}$.
- $\widehat\Psi(s_1), \widehat\Psi(s_2), \widehat\Psi(1-s_1)$: each entire of polynomial growth, decaying faster than any polynomial in $|\mathrm{Im}(s_i)|$ on vertical lines (b.3.A §2; standard property of Mellin transforms of compactly supported smooth functions).
- $\Gamma^{(3,1)}$: holomorphic on the contour. The candidate poles are
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4$ at $s_1+s_2 = 1$, i.e., $\mathrm{Re}(s_1+s_2) = 1$. On contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1+s_2) = \delta'+c_2 < 1$ (strict, since $\delta' < 1-c_2$). Pole is to the RIGHT of contour, off-contour. ✓
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$ at $s_1 = 1/2$. On contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1) = \delta' < 1-c_2 < 1/2$. Pole is to the RIGHT of contour. ✓
  - $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ at $s_1 = s_2$, i.e., $\mathrm{Re}(s_1) = c_2$. On contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_1) = \delta' < c_2$. Pole is to the RIGHT of contour. ✓

  $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ has at-most-polynomial growth on vertical lines (Iwaniec–Kowalski Thm 5.23 for $\zeta(s)$ on $\mathrm{Re}(s) > 0$ in any zero-free region; applies to $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ pulled back from $\mathrm{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}(i)$).
- $H$: holomorphic when $\min\{\mathrm{Re}(s_1), \mathrm{Re}(s_2), \mathrm{Re}(1-s_1)\} > 1/3$. On contour: $\delta' > 1/3$ ✓, $c_2 > 1/2 > 1/3$ ✓, $1-\delta' > 1-(1-c_2) = c_2 > 1/2 > 1/3$ ✓. So $H$ is bounded on the contour.
- $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2)$: each local factor satisfies
$$
|1 + 2p^{-s_1} + p^{-s_2} + p^{-(1-s_1)}| \;\le\; 1 + 2p^{-\delta'} + p^{-c_2} + p^{-(1-\delta')} \;\le\; 5,
$$
since each of $p^{-\delta'}, p^{-c_2}, p^{-(1-\delta')} \le 1$ for $p \ge 2$ and the relevant exponents positive. Hence
$$
|\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2)| \;\le\; 5^{\omega(N^2+1)} \;=\; N^{o(1)}
$$
by the Erdős–Kac / Hardy–Ramanujan bound $\omega(M) = O(\log M / \log\log M)$.

Combining:

$$
|R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N)| \;\le\; \kappa^2\,N\cdot\frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}\,N^{\delta'+c_2}\,5^{\omega(N^2+1)}\,\int\!\!\int |\widehat\Psi(\delta'+it_1)|^2|\widehat\Psi(c_2+it_2)|\,|\widehat\Psi(1-\delta'-it_1)|\,|\Gamma^{(3,1)}_{\mathrm{nondiv}}|\,dt_1\,dt_2,
$$

where $\Gamma^{(3,1)}_{\mathrm{nondiv}}$ denotes the holomorphic-on-contour part of $\Gamma^{(3,1)}$ (excluding the constant $H$-factor we already bounded, included in the $C_0$ below). The $dt_1 dt_2$ integral converges by rapid $\widehat\Psi$-decay against polynomial $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$-growth, giving a finite constant $C_0 = C_0(\delta', c_2)$.

Thus

$$
\boxed{\;|R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N)| \;\le\; C_0\,\kappa^2\,N^{1+\delta'+c_2}\cdot 5^{\omega(N^2+1)} \;=\; O\!\bigl(N^{1+\delta'+c_2+o(1)}\bigr).\,}
$$

**Step 2: Sub-$N^2$ growth.**

Since $\delta' \in (1/3, 1-c_2)$ and $c_2 \in (1/2, 2/3)$, we have $\delta'+c_2 \in (1/3+1/2, 1) = (5/6, 1)$. In particular, $1+\delta'+c_2 < 2$ STRICTLY. Set $\tau := 1 - \delta' - c_2 > 0$. Then

$$
|R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N)| \;=\; O(N^{2-\tau+o(1)}) \;=\; o(N^2),
$$

uniformly in $N$. Concretely, taking $\delta' = 1/3 + \epsilon$ and $c_2 = 1/2 + \epsilon$ for any sufficiently small $\epsilon > 0$ gives $\delta'+c_2 = 5/6 + 2\epsilon$, hence $|R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N)| = O(N^{11/6 + 2\epsilon + o(1)})$, sub-$N^2$ for $\epsilon < 1/12$.

**Step 3: Asymptotic-coefficient extraction.**

By definition (the canonical convention used throughout b.3.D / b.3.E for the operator $\Lambda^k$),

$$
\Lambda^k[F] \;:=\; \text{coefficient of }N^2(\log N)^k\text{ in the asymptotic expansion of }F(N)\text{ as }N\to\infty.
$$

If $F(N) = O(N^{2-\tau})$ for some $\tau > 0$ uniformly in $N$, then for every $k \ge 0$,

$$
\frac{F(N)}{N^2(\log N)^k} \;=\; O\!\bigl(N^{-\tau}\,(\log N)^{-k}\bigr) \;\to\; 0 \quad\text{as }N\to\infty.
$$

Thus $\Lambda^k[F] = 0$ for all $k \ge 0$. Applying with $F = R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}$ and $\tau$ from Step 2: $\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}] = 0$. $\Box$

**Remark (independence of contour parameters).** $R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N)$, viewed as a function of $N$ alone, is independent of the specific choice of $\delta' \in (1/3, 1-c_2)$ and $c_2 \in (1/2, 2/3)$ within their valid ranges (Cauchy: the integral is constant under contour deformations within the holomorphic region — we verified above that no poles are crossed). The bound $O(N^{1+\delta'+c_2+o(1)})$ holds for EACH fixed valid choice, with a $C_0$ depending on the choice. Choosing the parameters to minimize $\delta'+c_2$ gives the sharpest power; the sharpest available bound is approached by taking $\delta' \to 1/3^+$, $c_2 \to 1/2^+$, giving $|R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}| = O(N^{11/6+o(1)})$. (One cannot achieve this minimum exactly without crossing poles, but one can come arbitrarily close.)

## 9. Caveats and unknowns

(i) The $K^* > 0$ assumption uses $H(\vec{1/2}) > 0$. b.3.A §3 derived $H = \mathcal{D}_F^{\mathcal{F}} / \prod_{i<j} \zeta_\mathcal{Q}(s_i+s_j)^2$ from a positive Euler product, but $H(\vec{1/2})$ specifically requires the Euler product to evaluate positively at the diagonal slice — verified in b.3.D §5 implicitly (the leading-coefficient corollary states "strictly positive"). ✓

(ii) **$\Lambda^k$ extraction with $N$-dependent $K^*$ (skeptic C4 fix).** The "constant" $K^* = \widehat\Psi(1/2)^4 H(\vec{1/2}) \prod_{p\mid N^2+1}[1+4p^{-1/2}]$ contains the divisor product $\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}[1+4p^{-1/2}]$, which depends weakly on $N$ (via $\omega(N^2+1) = O(\log N / \log\log N)$). The strict reading of "$\Lambda^2[\mathcal{B}^\sharp] = -2\kappa^6 K^*(N)$" is therefore: the $N^2 (\log N)^2$ coefficient is itself an $N$-dependent factor in $[c_-, c_+ \cdot N^{o(1)}]$, where $c_\pm > 0$ are absolute. Two precise statements survive this refinement:

   (ii.a) **Lower bound (uniform in $N$).** $\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}[1+4p^{-1/2}] \ge 1$ since each local factor $\ge 1$. Hence $K^*(N) \ge K^*_- := \widehat\Psi(1/2)^4 H(\vec{1/2}) > 0$ uniformly in $N$, and
$$
\mathcal{B}^\sharp(N) \;\le\; -2\kappa^6 K^*_-\cdot N^2(\log N)^2\,(1+o(1))\quad\text{as }N\to\infty
$$
(the inequality reverses sign because $\mathcal{B}^\sharp$'s leading term is negative). Hence $|\mathcal{B}^\sharp(N)| \gg N^2 (\log N)^2$ for all sufficiently large $N$, exceeding the target $K(N)^2 (\log N)^B = N^{o(1)} (\log N)^B$ by at least a factor $N^{2}/(\log N)^{B-2}$.
