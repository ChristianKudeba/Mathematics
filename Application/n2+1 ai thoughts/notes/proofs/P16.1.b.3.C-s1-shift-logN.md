# P16.1.b.3.C. $s_1$-contour shift of $R^{(4,*)}(N)$: emergence of the first $\log N$

## Status

Third sub-chunk of P16.1.b.3 (continuation of `P16.1.b.3.B-s3-shift.md`). The deliverable is the explicit $s_1$-contour shift of the 2-fold residual integral $R^{(4,*)}(N)$ defined in Theorem 12 of b.3.B. The two poles crossed are:

1. The **simple pole** at $s_1=1/2$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$ ‚Äî contributes an $N^{1/2}$-residue term.
2. The **double pole** at $s_1=1-s_2$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4$ ‚Äî contributes an $N\cdot(\log N + O(1))$-residue term.

The double-pole residue is *the first appearance of $\log N$* in the entire $\mathcal{B}^{\sharp}$ analysis. Its source is the derivative $\partial_{s_1}N^{s_1+s_2}|_{s_1=1-s_2}=N\log N$ that arises from the double-pole residue formula $\mathrm{Res}_{z=z_0}[\widetilde I(z)/(z-z_0)^2]=\widetilde I'(z_0)$.

## 1. Setup recap from b.3.B

From `P16.1.b.3.B-s3-shift.md` Theorem 12, boxed formula:

$$
R^{(4,*)}(N) \;=\; \kappa^2\,N\cdot\frac{1}{(2\pi i)^2}\int_{(c+\eta)^2}\!\!\!\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)\,d^2 s,
$$

with integrand

$$
\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2) \;:=\; N^{s_1+s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\,\Gamma^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2),
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Gamma^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2) \;&=\; \zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4\cdot\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2\cdot\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2\cdot H(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_1),\\
\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2) \;&=\; \prod_{p\mid N^2+1}\!\bigl[\,1 + 2\,p^{-s_1}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-(1-s_1)}\bigr].
\end{aligned}
$$

Constants: $\kappa=\pi/(8\,\zeta^{(3,4)}(2))$ from b.3.A Lemma 14, $c=1/2+\epsilon_0$, $\epsilon_0\in(0,1/6)$, $\eta\in(0,\eta_0)$ with $\eta_0=\min(1/6-\epsilon_0,1-c-\delta)$.

## 2. Re-stratification of $R^{(4,*)}$ (Lemma 17)

**Issue.** The factor $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ in $\Gamma^{(3,1)}$ has a pole at the anti-diagonal $s_2=s_1$, i.e., at $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c+\eta$. On the contour $(c+\eta)^2$ this pole sits ON the contour (codim-1 in the integration domain), so the integral in the boxed formula above is at best conditionally convergent. We resolve this exactly as in Lemma 15 of b.3.B: lift one variable's contour BEFORE extracting the residue.

**Lemma 17 (Re-stratification of $R^{(4,*)}$).** Let $\eta_2:=\min(\eta_0,\,(1/6-\epsilon_0-\eta)/2)>0$, and let $\eta'\in(0,\eta_2)$. Then $R^{(4,*)}(N)$ admits the equivalent representation

$$
R^{(4,*)}(N) \;=\; \kappa^2\,N\cdot\frac{1}{(2\pi i)^2}\int_{(c+\eta)\times(c+\eta+\eta')}\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)\,d^2 s,
$$

absolutely convergent, with the pole $s_2=s_1$ now at $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=c+\eta<c+\eta+\eta'$ ‚Äî i.e., strictly off-contour.

*Proof.* Mirror the "lift in 3-fold form, then extract residue" derivation of b.3.B Lemma 15.

1. Start from $R^{(4)}_{\mathcal{C}_\eta}(N)=(2\pi i)^{-3}\int_{(c+\eta)^2\times(c)}\mathrm{Res}_{s_4=1-s_3}I(\vec s)\,d^3 s$ on the b.3.B stratified contour $\mathcal{C}_\eta$.

2. The 3-fold integrand has only the pole hyperplanes listed in b.3.B Lemma 15 table. In $s_2$, on the strip $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)\in[c+\eta,\,c+\eta+\eta']$ with $s_1$ on $(c+\eta)$ and $s_3$ on $(c)$ fixed: the candidate poles are at $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=1-\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=1-c-\eta\,(<c+\eta)$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^2$ (outside left), at $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=1-\mathrm{Re}(s_3)=1-c$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+s_3)^2$ (outside left for $c>1/2$), and at $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=\mathrm{Re}(s_3)=c$ from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_3)^2$ (outside left). All candidate poles in $s_2$ are strictly LEFT of the lift strip; the lift $s_2:(c+\eta)\to(c+\eta+\eta')$ is unobstructed by Cauchy.

3. After lifting in the 3-fold form, extract $\mathrm{Res}_{s_3=1-s_1}$ as in b.3.B ¬ß4. The residue evaluation is identical (same algebraic computation); the resulting 2-fold integrand is the same $\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)$, but on the new contour $(c+\eta)\times(c+\eta+\eta')$.

4. Anti-diagonal pole now off-contour: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ has pole at $s_2=s_1$, at $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=c+\eta\ne c+\eta+\eta'$. Integrand holomorphic on a tubular neighborhood of the new contour; absolute convergence follows from rapid decay of $\widehat\Psi$ and polynomial growth of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ and $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4$ factors on vertical lines (b.3.A ¬ß8 convergence argument carries over).

The two representations of $R^{(4,*)}$ ‚Äî on $(c+\eta)^2$ and on $(c+\eta)\times(c+\eta+\eta')$ ‚Äî agree by analytic continuation of the integral in the parameter $\eta'$ from $\eta'=0^+$. $\Box$

**Convention.** From here on, "$R^{(4,*)}$" means the absolutely convergent integral on $(c+\eta)\times(c+\eta+\eta')$ of Lemma 17. We shorthand $c_1:=c+\eta$, $c_2:=c+\eta+\eta'$, so $1/2<c_1<c_2<1/2+1/6=2/3$ for the parameter ranges we use.

## 3. Pole structure of $\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)$ in $s_1$

Hold $s_2$ on the line $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=c_2$, generic imaginary part. View $\mathcal{I}$ as a meromorphic function of $s_1$. Factors carrying $s_1$:

| factor | pole in $s_1$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)$ at pole | order |
|---|---|---|---|
| $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2$ | $s_1=0$ | $0$ | double |
| $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)$ | $s_1=1$ | $1$ | simple |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4$ | $s_1=1-s_2$ | $1-c_2$ | **double** |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$ | $s_1=1/2$ | $1/2$ | simple |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ | $s_1=s_2$ | $c_2$ | simple |
| $H(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_1)$ | none in $1/3<\mathrm{Re}(s_1)<2/3$ | ‚Äî | ‚Äî |
| $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2)$ | none in $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)>0$ | ‚Äî | ‚Äî |

(For $H$: holomorphic when $\min\{\mathrm{Re}(s_1),\mathrm{Re}(s_2),\mathrm{Re}(1-s_1),\mathrm{Re}(s_1)\}>1/3$ i.e., $1/3<\mathrm{Re}(s_1)<2/3$ and $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)>1/3$. For $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}$: each local factor $1+2p^{-s_1}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-(1-s_1)}$ is an entire function of $s_1$, real-positive for real $s_1\in(0,1)$ and never vanishing in our strip.)

**Strip selection.** We shift $s_1$ from $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c_1=c+\eta$ down to $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=\delta'$, where $\delta'\in(1/3,\,1-c_2)=(1/3,\,1/2-\epsilon_0-\eta-\eta')$. Inside the strip $[\delta',c_1]$:

- $s_1=1-s_2$ at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=1-c_2=1/2-\epsilon_0-\eta-\eta'$ ‚úì (inside, since $\delta'<1-c_2$ by definition).
- $s_1=1/2$ at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=1/2$ ‚úì (inside, since $\delta'<1/2$ ‚Äî automatic from $\delta'<1-c_2<1/2$).
- $s_1=s_2$ at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=c_2=c+\eta+\eta'>c_1$ ‚úó (outside, on right).
- $s_1=0$ at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=0<\delta'$ ‚úó (outside, on left).
- $s_1=1$ at $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=1>c_1$ ‚úó (outside, on right).

**The stratification has worked**: only the two poles relevant to the analysis (double at $s_1=1-s_2$, simple at $s_1=1/2$) are inside the strip; the anti-diagonal $s_1=s_2$ is pushed strictly out the right side by the lift of b.3.B + Lemma 17.

**Order of the two poles along $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)$.** $1-c_2=1/2-(\epsilon_0+\eta+\eta')<1/2$, so the double pole is at a STRICTLY SMALLER real part than the simple pole. As $s_1$ shifts from $c_1$ leftward, it crosses $s_1=1/2$ FIRST, then $s_1=1-s_2$.

## 4. Residue at the simple pole $s_1=1/2$ (Theorem 13)

**Theorem 13 (Simple-pole residue at $s_1=1/2$).** With $s_2$ on $(c_2)$ generic,

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1/2}\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2) \;=\; \frac{\kappa}{2}\,N^{1/2}\cdot N^{s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,\Gamma^{(2,1)}(s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2),
$$

where

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Gamma^{(2,1)}(s_2) \;&:=\; \zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6\cdot H(1/2,\,s_2,\,1/2,\,1/2),\\
\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2) \;&:=\; \prod_{p\mid N^2+1}\!\Bigl[\,1+3\,p^{-1/2}+p^{-s_2}\,\Bigr].
\end{aligned}
$$

*Proof.* $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2=\kappa/(2s_1-1)+\mathcal{R}(2s_1)=(\kappa/2)/(s_1-1/2)+\mathcal{R}(2s_1)$ by Lemma 14, so the residue in $s_1$ of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$ at $s_1=1/2$ equals $\kappa/2$ (Jacobian $\partial(2s_1)/\partial s_1=2$). All other factors of $\mathcal{I}$ are holomorphic at $s_1=1/2$ for generic $s_2$ on $(c_2)$ (none of $1/2+s_2$, $1-1/2=1/2$, $s_2+1-1/2=s_2+1/2$ produce a $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$-pole when $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=c_2>1/2$; the auxiliary factor $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)=\widehat\Psi(1/2)$ is finite). Evaluating each factor at $s_1=1/2$:

- $N^{s_1+s_2}\to N^{1/2+s_2}$.
- $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\to\widehat\Psi(1/2)^2$; $\widehat\Psi(s_2)\to\widehat\Psi(s_2)$; $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\to\widehat\Psi(1/2)$. Combined: $\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)$.
- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^4$; $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2\to\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^2$. Combined: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$.
- $H(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_1)\to H(1/2,s_2,1/2,1/2)$.
- $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2)\to\prod_p[1+2p^{-1/2}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-1/2}]=\prod_p[1+3p^{-1/2}+p^{-s_2}]$.

The residue is $(\kappa/2)$ times the product of these. $\Box$

**Observation.** The residue at $s_1=1/2$ carries the explicit $N^{1/2}$ factor and a clean $s_2$-only integrand. It will be one of the components of the $s_1$-shift identity (Theorem 15 below). When integrated over $s_2$ on $(c_2)$, the result is a "**$N^{1/2}$-tier**" contribution to the overall bound.

## 5. Residue at the double pole $s_1=1-s_2$ (Theorem 14): the $\log N$ extraction

**Setup.** Write $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4$ via its Laurent expansion at $s_1+s_2=1$. From Lemma 14 of b.3.A:

$$
\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2 \;=\; \frac{\kappa}{w-1} + \gamma_0 + \gamma_1(w-1) + O((w-1)^2),
$$

where $\gamma_0:=\mathcal{R}(1)=\lim_{w\to 1}[\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2-\kappa/(w-1)]$ is the "constant term" of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ at $1$, and $\gamma_1:=\mathcal{R}'(1)$. Squaring:

$$
\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^4 \;=\; \frac{\kappa^2}{(w-1)^2} + \frac{2\kappa\gamma_0}{w-1} + (2\kappa\gamma_1+\gamma_0^2) + O(w-1).
$$

So $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4$ has a double pole at $w=1$, with principal part $\kappa^2/(w-1)^2+2\kappa\gamma_0/(w-1)$.

Factor

$$
\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2) \;=\; \zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4\cdot \widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(s_1,s_2),\quad \widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(s_1,s_2) := \mathcal{I}/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4 \;=\; N^{s_1+s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\,\widetilde\Gamma(s_1,s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2),
$$

with $\widetilde\Gamma(s_1,s_2):=\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2\,H(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_1)$. For generic $s_2$ on $(c_2)$, $\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}$ is holomorphic at $s_1=1-s_2$ (the candidates would be $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$ at $s_1=1/2$ ‚Äî only an issue when $1-s_2=1/2$, i.e., $s_2=1/2$, which is off our contour $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)=c_2>1/2$ for generic imaginary part ‚Äî and $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ at $s_1=s_2$ ‚Äî only at the diagonal slice $\mathrm{Im}(s_1)=\mathrm{Im}(s_2)$, again not at the point $s_1=1-s_2$ with $\mathrm{Re}(s_1)=1-c_2$).

**Theorem 14 (Double-pole residue at $s_1=1-s_2$, with $\log N$ extraction).** With $s_2$ on $(c_2)$ generic,

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1-s_2}\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2) \;=\; \kappa^2 \cdot\frac{\partial \widetilde{\mathcal{I}}}{\partial s_1}\bigg|_{s_1=1-s_2} \;+\; 2\kappa\gamma_0\cdot\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(1-s_2,s_2),
$$

and explicitly,

$$
\boxed{\;\mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1-s_2}\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2) \;=\; \kappa^2\,N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\,\bigl[\log N + \Xi(s_2)\bigr] \;+\; 2\kappa\gamma_0\,N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\;}
$$

with

$$
\begin{aligned}
J_{\log}(s_2) \;&:=\; \widehat\Psi(1-s_2)^2\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2\,H(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(1-s_2,s_2),\\[1mm]
\Xi(s_2) \;&:=\; 2\,\frac{\widehat\Psi'(1-s_2)}{\widehat\Psi(1-s_2)} \;-\; \frac{\widehat\Psi'(s_2)}{\widehat\Psi(s_2)} \;+\; 4\,\frac{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2-2s_2)}{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)} \;-\; 2\,\frac{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2s_2)}{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)} \\
&\quad+\; \bigl[\partial_1 H - \partial_3 H + \partial_4 H\bigr]/H\,\bigg|_{(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)} \;+\; \frac{\partial_{s_1}\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}}{\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}}\bigg|_{s_1=1-s_2},
\end{aligned}
$$

and $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(1-s_2,s_2)=\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}\bigl[1+2\,p^{-(1-s_2)}+2\,p^{-s_2}\bigr]$.

*Proof.* The residue at a double pole: for $f(z)=\widetilde I(z)/(z-z_0)^2+h(z)/(z-z_0)+\text{holo}$, $\mathrm{Res}_{z=z_0}f=\widetilde I'(z_0)+h(z_0)$. Apply with $z=s_1$, $z_0=1-s_2$, $\widetilde I(s_1)=\kappa^2\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(s_1,s_2)$, $h(s_1)=2\kappa\gamma_0\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(s_1,s_2)$ (since $(s_1+s_2-1)$ is the local parameter; $(w-1)$ where $w=s_1+s_2$, Jacobian $\partial w/\partial s_1=1$). So

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1-s_2}\mathcal{I} \;=\; \kappa^2\,\partial_{s_1}\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(1-s_2,s_2) + 2\kappa\gamma_0\,\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(1-s_2,s_2).
$$

Evaluate $\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(1-s_2,s_2)$: at $s_1=1-s_2$, $N^{s_1+s_2}=N$, $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2=\widehat\Psi(1-s_2)^2$, $\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)=\widehat\Psi(s_2)$, $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2=\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2$, $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2=\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2$, $H\to H(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)$, $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}\to \prod_p[1+2p^{-(1-s_2)}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-s_2}]=\prod_p[1+2p^{-(1-s_2)}+2p^{-s_2}]$. Hence

$$
\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(1-s_2,s_2) \;=\; N\cdot J_{\log}(s_2).
$$

For the derivative: use the logarithmic-derivative identity $\partial_{s_1}\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}=\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}\cdot\partial_{s_1}\log\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}$. From the explicit factorization of $\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}$:

$$
\partial_{s_1}\log\widetilde{\mathcal{I}} \;=\; \log N \;+\; 2\frac{\widehat\Psi'(s_1)}{\widehat\Psi(s_1)} \;-\; \frac{\widehat\Psi'(1-s_1)}{\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)} \;+\; 4\frac{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2s_1)}{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)} \;-\; 2\frac{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(s_2+1-s_1)}{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)} \;+\; \bigl[\partial_1 H - \partial_3 H + \partial_4 H\bigr]/H \;+\; \frac{\partial_{s_1}\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}}{\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}}.
$$

(The factor $4=2\cdot 2$ comes from $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2$: chain rule gives $2\cdot\partial_{s_1}\log\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)=2\cdot 2\cdot\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2s_1)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)$. The factor $-2$ for $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$: chain rule gives $2\cdot(-1)\cdot\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(s_2+1-s_1)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)$. The signs on $H$-derivatives: $H$'s arguments are $(s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_1)$ so $\partial_{s_1}\log H=(\partial_1+\partial_4)H/H + (-1)\partial_3 H/H$.) Setting $s_1=1-s_2$ gives $\Xi(s_2)$.

Multiplying together: $\partial_{s_1}\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}|_{s_1=1-s_2}=N\cdot J_{\log}(s_2)\cdot[\log N+\Xi(s_2)]$. Combining with the constant-term contribution $2\kappa\gamma_0\cdot\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}(1-s_2,s_2)=2\kappa\gamma_0\cdot N\cdot J_{\log}(s_2)$:

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1-s_2}\mathcal{I} \;=\; \kappa^2\,N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\,[\log N + \Xi(s_2)] + 2\kappa\gamma_0\,N\,J_{\log}(s_2),
$$

which is the boxed formula. $\Box$

**Structural observation.** The $\log N$ factor has emerged from a single source: differentiating $N^{s_1+s_2}$ with respect to $s_1$ and evaluating at $s_1=1-s_2$ gives $(\log N)N^{(1-s_2)+s_2}=N\log N$. Every other term in $\partial_{s_1}\log\widetilde{\mathcal{I}}$ (the $\widehat\Psi'/\widehat\Psi$, $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}$, $\partial_i H/H$, $\partial_{s_1}\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}/\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}$ pieces) is bounded uniformly in $s_2$ on $(c_2)$ for generic imaginary parts. So $\Xi(s_2)$ is a bounded "Stieltjes-like" function on the integration line, and the $\log N$ term genuinely dominates as $N\to\infty$.

## 6. Contour-shift identity in $s_1$ (Theorem 15)

**Theorem 15 ($s_1$-contour shift of $R^{(4,*)}$).** For $1/3<\delta'<1-c_2=1/2-\epsilon_0-\eta-\eta'$,

$$
R^{(4,*)}(N) \;=\; R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N) \;+\; R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}(N) \;+\; R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N),
$$

where:

$$
R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N) \;:=\; \kappa^2\,N\cdot\frac{1}{(2\pi i)^2}\int_{(\delta')\times(c_2)}\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)\,d^2 s,
$$

$$
\boxed{\,R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}(N) \;=\; \frac{\kappa^3}{2}\,N^{3/2}\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\cdot\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_2)}\!\! N^{s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,\Gamma^{(2,1)}(s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2)\,ds_2\,}
$$

(the "**$N^{3/2}$-tier residue**", combining $\kappa^2 N$ from the prefactor of $R^{(4,*)}$ with $(\kappa/2)\cdot N^{1/2}$ from Theorem 13), and

$$
\boxed{\,R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N) \;=\; \kappa^2\,N\cdot\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_2)}\!\!\Bigl\{\kappa^2\,N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\,[\log N + \Xi(s_2)] + 2\kappa\gamma_0\,N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\Bigr\}\,ds_2\;=\; \kappa^4\,N^2\,(\log N)\cdot \mathcal{J}_0 + \kappa^4\,N^2\,\mathcal{J}_1 + 2\kappa^3\gamma_0\,N^2\,\mathcal{J}_0\,}
$$

(the "**$N^2(\log N)$-tier residue**"), where

$$
\mathcal{J}_0 \;:=\; \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_2)}\!\! J_{\log}(s_2)\,ds_2,\qquad \mathcal{J}_1 \;:=\; \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_2)}\!\! J_{\log}(s_2)\,\Xi(s_2)\,ds_2.
$$

*Proof.* Fix $s_2$ on $(c_2)$ generic. Apply Cauchy on the rectangle $\delta'\le\mathrm{Re}(s_1)\le c_1$, $|\mathrm{Im}(s_1)|\le T$, and let $T\to\infty$. The top and bottom edges contribute zero in the limit by the rapid decay of $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)\ll(1+|\mathrm{Im}(s_1)|)^{-A}$ against polynomial growth of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^4,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ on vertical lines (b.3.A ¬ß8 / Iwaniec‚ÄìKowalski Thm 5.23). By the residue theorem:

$$
\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_1)}\!\!\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)\,ds_1 \;=\; \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\delta')}\!\!\mathcal{I}(s_1,s_2)\,ds_1 \;+\; \mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1/2}\mathcal{I} \;+\; \mathrm{Res}_{s_1=1-s_2}\mathcal{I}.
$$

Integrate over $s_2$ on $(c_2)$, multiply by the prefactor $\kappa^2 N/(2\pi i)$. The simple-pole contribution gives Theorem 13 ‚Üí $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$, the double-pole contribution gives Theorem 14 ‚Üí $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$, and the $(\delta')$-integral gives $R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}$. Fubini is justified by absolute convergence on each contour combination ($\min\mathrm{Re}>1/3$ throughout, $H$ bounded, $\widehat\Psi$ rapid-decay). $\Box$

## 7. Combined 4-term decomposition of $\mathcal{B}^{\sharp}(N)$

Combining b.3.A Theorem 10, b.3.B Theorem 12, and Theorem 15 of this chunk (with $R^{(4)}$ interpreted on the b.3.B stratified contour, and $R^{(4,*)}$ on the b.3.C stratified contour):

$$
