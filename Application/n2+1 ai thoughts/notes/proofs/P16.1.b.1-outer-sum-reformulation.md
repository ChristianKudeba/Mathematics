# P16.1.b.1. Outer-sum reformulation of $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N)$

## Status

First sub-chunk of chunk **P16.1.b** (from `P16.1.a-S3hard-unique-prime-refinement.md` §8). Builds on Corollaries 7–8 of P16.1.a. This file does not prove the full $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \le K(N)^2 \log^B N$ bound; rather, it produces the clean ordered-4-tuple reformulation $\mathcal{B}(N)$ to which the bound reduces, identifies the multiplicative-over-primes structure of the weight, and sets up the prime-by-prime decoupling for chunk P16.1.b.2.

**This file proves:**

1. **Lemma 9 (Structure $\leftrightarrow$ tuple bijection).** Shared-prime structures $\sigma$ with $\mathfrak{P}(\sigma) \ne \emptyset$ are in canonical bijection with non-trivial 4-tuples $(F_i)_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{F}^*$, where $\mathcal{F}$ is the set of ordered 4-tuples of squarefree positive integers all of whose prime divisors are $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$, and every prime in $\bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)$ divides at least two of the $F_i$.

2. **Theorem 6 (Outer-sum reformulation).** $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \le \mathcal{B}(N)$, where
   $$
   \mathcal{B}(N) \;:=\; \sum_{(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}^* \cap [1,N]^4} W(F) \cdot \tilde\Phi(N; F),
   $$
   $W(F) := \prod_{p \in \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)} 2^{\#\{i : p \mid F_i\} - 1}$, and $\tilde\Phi(N; F) := \#\{(d_i)_{i=1}^4 : d_i \mid N^2+1,\,(d_i)\text{ pwc},\, d_i \le N/F_i\}$.

3. **Lemma 10 (Multiplicative-over-primes structure of $W$ + box envelope).** $W$ is multiplicative over the prime factorization of $\prod_i F_i$. The box constraint encoded in $\tilde\Phi$ enforces $\prod_p p^2 \le \prod_i F_i \le N^4 / \prod_i d_i \le N^4$, i.e., the shared-prime mass $\prod_{p \in \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)} p \le N^2$.

4. **Sanity check.** The $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1$ contribution to $\mathcal{B}(N)$ recovers the $36 \cdot \tau(N^2+1)^4 \cdot \pi(N) = N^{1+o(1)}$ warm-up bound of P16.1.a §6.

## 1. Notation recap

From `P16.1.a-S3hard-unique-prime-refinement.md` §1–3, every $(D3)$-tuple $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{T}(N)^4$ in $S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}$ (i.e., with $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}((e_i)) \ne \emptyset$) decomposes uniquely as
$$
e_i \;=\; d_i \cdot F_i, \qquad d_i := e_i^{(1)},\ F_i := e_i^{(\ge 2)},
$$
with the following structure:

- $(d_i)_{i=1}^4$ is an ordered pairwise-coprime 4-tuple of squarefree divisors of $N^2+1$.
- $\mathrm{supp}(d_i) \cap \mathrm{supp}(F_j) = \emptyset$ for all $i, j$ (the unique-prime support and the shared-prime support are disjoint).
- $\sigma := (\mathcal{P}_S)_{|S| \ge 2}$ — the shared-prime structure — recovers $F_i = \prod_{S \ni i} \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_S} p$.
- The residues are determined by $a_i^{(1)} \equiv -N \pmod{d_i}$ (forced by Theorem 4) together with $(\eta_{p,i})_{p \in \mathfrak{P}(\sigma),\, i \in S_p}$ satisfying $(\diamond_p)$ for each shared prime $p$, with the count of valid $\eta$-tuples bounded by $\prod_p 2^{|S_p|-1}$ (Theorem 5 + Corollary 8).

Define $\mathfrak{P}(\sigma) := \bigsqcup_{|S| \ge 2} \mathcal{P}_S = \bigcup_p \{p : p \mid F_i \text{ for some } i\}$.

Box constraint on $\mathcal{T}(N)$: $e_i = d_i F_i \le N$, i.e., $d_i \le N/F_i$.

Set $K(N) := \tau(N^2 + 1)$ and write $\mathcal{Q}(N) := \{p \text{ prime} : p \equiv 1 \pmod 4,\, p \le N\}$.

## 2. Bijection $\sigma \leftrightarrow (F_i)$ (Lemma 9)

**Lemma 9.** The map $\sigma \mapsto (F_i(\sigma))_{i=1}^4$, where $F_i(\sigma) := \prod_{S \ni i} \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_S} p$, is a bijection between:
- Shared-prime structures $\sigma = (\mathcal{P}_S)_{|S| \ge 2}$ (disjoint sets of primes $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$), and
- Ordered 4-tuples $(F_i)_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{F}$, where
  $$
  \mathcal{F} \;:=\; \bigl\{(F_i)_{i=1}^4 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 1}^4 \;:\; \text{each } F_i \text{ squarefree, primes } \equiv 1 \pmod 4,\; \#\{i : p \mid F_i\} \in \{0\} \cup \{2,3,4\} \;\forall p\bigr\}.
  $$

The bijection sends $\mathfrak{P}(\sigma) = \emptyset$ to $(F_i) = (1,1,1,1)$; we write $\mathcal{F}^* := \mathcal{F} \setminus \{(1,1,1,1)\}$ for the non-trivial 4-tuples.

*Proof.* **Forward.** Given $\sigma$, set $F_i := \prod_{S \ni i} \prod_{p \in \mathcal{P}_S} p$. Each $F_i$ is squarefree (factors are distinct primes by disjointness of the $\mathcal{P}_S$). All prime factors are $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$ (sf-good condition). For each prime $p \mid \prod_i F_i$, $p$ lies in exactly one $\mathcal{P}_S$, and $\{i : p \mid F_i\} = S$ has $|S| \ge 2$. Hence $(F_i(\sigma)) \in \mathcal{F}$.

**Backward.** Given $(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}$, for each prime $p \mid \prod_i F_i$ define $S(p) := \{i : p \mid F_i\}$; by the constraint, $|S(p)| \ge 2$. Set $\mathcal{P}_S := \{p : S(p) = S\}$. The $\mathcal{P}_S$ are disjoint (each prime has a unique $S(p)$). This recovers $\sigma$.

**Inversion.** Both maps are inverses on the nose: $\sigma \mapsto (F_i) \mapsto \sigma'$ gives $\mathcal{P}'_S = \{p : S(p) = S\} = \mathcal{P}_S$. Similarly in the other direction. $\square$

**Corollary.** Under this bijection, the shared-prime weight transforms as
$$
w(\sigma) \;=\; \prod_{p \in \mathfrak{P}(\sigma)} 2^{|S_p| - 1} \;=\; \prod_{p \in \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)} 2^{\#\{i : p \mid F_i\} - 1} \;=:\; W(F).
$$

## 3. Theorem 6: outer-sum reformulation

**Theorem 6.** $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \;\le\; \mathcal{B}(N)$, where
$$
\mathcal{B}(N) \;:=\; \sum_{(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}^*,\, F_i \le N\,\forall i} W(F) \cdot \tilde\Phi(N; F),
$$
$$
\tilde\Phi(N; F) \;:=\; \#\bigl\{(d_i)_{i=1}^4 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 1}^4 \;:\; d_i \mid N^2+1,\; (d_i)\text{ pairwise coprime},\; d_i \le N/F_i\bigr\}.
$$

*Proof.* By Corollary 7, every $(D3)$-tuple in $S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}$ is determined by the data $(d_i, \sigma, (\eta_{p,i}))$ subject to:
(i) $(d_i)$ pwc, $d_i \mid N^2+1$.
(ii) $\sigma$ a shared-prime structure with $\mathfrak{P}(\sigma) \ne \emptyset$.
(iii) $\mathrm{supp}(d_i) \cap \mathfrak{P}(\sigma) = \emptyset$.
(iv) $d_i \cdot F_i(\sigma) \le N$ for each $i$.
(v) The signs $(\eta_{p,i})_{p \in \mathfrak{P}(\sigma),\,i \in S_p}$ satisfy $(\diamond_p)$ for each $p$; by Corollary 8 the number of such $\eta$-tuples is at most $w(\sigma)$.

So
$$
|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \;\le\; \sum_{\sigma:\,\mathfrak{P}(\sigma) \ne \emptyset} w(\sigma) \cdot N_\sigma,
$$
where $N_\sigma$ is the count of $(d_i)$ satisfying (i)–(iv). Apply the bijection of Lemma 9 to replace the sum over $\sigma$ by a sum over $(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}^*$, replace $w(\sigma)$ by $W(F)$, and drop constraint (iii) (since dropping it only enlarges $N_\sigma$, an upper bound is preserved). Then $N_\sigma \le \tilde\Phi(N; F(\sigma))$. The constraint $F_i \le N$ is automatic: if $F_i > N$, then $d_i \le N/F_i < 1$ forces no valid $d_i \ge 1$, so $\tilde\Phi(N; F) = 0$. $\square$

**Remark.** Dropping constraint (iii) loses at most a factor of $N^{o(1)}$ in the bound (since the number of ways to additionally assign primes of $N^2+1$ to shared classes is at most $4^{\omega(N^2+1)} = N^{o(1)}$). This is absorbed in the $\log^B N$ slack of the final $K^2 \log^B N$ target; the explicit absorption is deferred to chunk b.4.

## 4. Lemma 10: multiplicative structure and box envelope

The next chunk b.2 will exploit the following observations.

**Lemma 10 (Multiplicativity of $W$).** For $(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}$, the weight $W$ factorizes over primes:
$$
W(F) \;=\; \prod_{p \in \mathcal{Q}(N)} w_p(F),
$$
where $w_p(F) := 2^{|S(p)| - 1}$ if $p \mid \prod_i F_i$ (with $S(p) := \{i : p \mid F_i\}$, $|S(p)| \ge 2$ by $(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}$), and $w_p(F) := 1$ otherwise.

*Proof.* Direct from the definition. $\square$

**Lemma 10' (Box envelope).** For any $(F_i)$ appearing with non-zero contribution in $\mathcal{B}(N)$, the shared-prime mass satisfies
$$
\prod_{p \in \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)} p \;\le\; \biggl(\prod_i F_i\biggr)^{1/2} \;\le\; N^2.
$$

*Proof.* Each prime $p \in \bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)$ appears in at least 2 of the $F_i$'s, so $p^2 \mid \prod_i F_i$, hence $\prod_p p^2 \le \prod_i F_i$ (using squarefreeness of the $F_i$, so the $p^2$ contribution to $\prod_i F_i$ is exact). The upper bound $\prod_i F_i \le \prod_i N = N^4$ uses $F_i \le N$ (Theorem 6). $\square$

**Implication.** The shared-prime support has total mass $\le N^2$, even though individual primes may be as large as $N$ and the count of shared primes may be as large as $O(\log N / \log\log N)$. This is the crucial "small total mass" constraint that lets chunk b.3 produce a $K^2 \log^B N$ bound rather than a divergent one.

**Why dropping the box constraint would fail.** Without the box constraint, the multiplicative structure of $W$ would give
$$
\sum_{(F_i) \in \mathcal{F}} W(F) \;=\; \prod_{p \in \mathcal{Q}(N)} \biggl(1 + \sum_{S \subseteq \{1,...,4\},\,|S| \ge 2} 2^{|S|-1}\biggr) \;=\; \prod_{p \in \mathcal{Q}(N)} 37,
$$
which is $\exp(\Theta(N/\log N))$, hopelessly divergent. The box constraint is essential.

## 5. Sanity check: $|\mathfrak{P}(\sigma)| = 1$ regime recovers P16.1.a §6

The contribution to $\mathcal{B}(N)$ from $(F_i)$ with $|\bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)| = 1$ — i.e., exactly one shared prime $p_0$ — is computed as follows. Fix $S \subseteq \{1,2,3,4\}$ with $|S| \ge 2$ and a prime $p_0 \in \mathcal{Q}(N)$. Set $F_i := p_0$ for $i \in S$, $F_i := 1$ for $i \notin S$. Then $W(F) = 2^{|S|-1}$.

$\tilde\Phi(N; F) \le \tilde\Phi(N; (1,1,1,1)) = \#\{(d_i)\text{ pwc divisors of } N^2+1,\, d_i \le N\} \le \tau(N^2+1)^4$ (loose bound; the actual count is $\le 5^{\omega(N^2+1)} \le \tau(N^2+1)^{\log_2 5}$, but $\tau^4$ suffices for the sanity check).

Summing (using $|\mathcal{Q}(N)| \le \pi(N)$):
$$
\mathcal{B}(N)\big|_{|\mathfrak{P}|=1} \;\le\; \sum_{S : |S| \ge 2} 2^{|S|-1} \cdot |\mathcal{Q}(N)| \cdot \tau(N^2+1)^4 \;\le\; 36 \cdot \pi(N) \cdot \tau(N^2+1)^4 \;=\; N^{1+o(1)},
$$
recovering the warm-up bound of P16.1.a §6. $\checkmark$

## 6. Setup for chunk P16.1.b.2 — prime-by-prime decoupling

The goal of b.2 is to dyadically decouple $\mathcal{B}(N)$ over primes. Two approaches identified:

**Approach A — Dyadic Mellin envelope.** Replace the box constraint $d_i \le N/F_i$ by a smooth dyadic envelope $\mathbf{1}[d_i F_i \le N] \approx \int_{(\sigma)} (N/d_i F_i)^s \frac{ds}{2\pi i s}$. The resulting Dirichlet sum factors over primes, giving a 4-variable Dirichlet generating function $D(s_1, s_2, s_3, s_4)$. The leading-order behavior at $s_i \to 0$ should yield $K(N)^2 \log^B N$ for $B \le 6$ (counting the four box-shifts and the multiplicativity of $W$).

**Approach B — Direct dyadic decomposition.** Decompose $\mathcal{B}(N) = \sum_{\Delta} \mathcal{B}_{\Delta}(N)$ over dyadic ranges $\Delta = (\Delta_1, \Delta_2, \Delta_3, \Delta_4)$ with $\Delta_i / 2 < F_i \le \Delta_i$. For each fixed $\Delta$, sum $\tilde\Phi(N; F)$ over $F$ in the $\Delta$-box using divisor sums, then sum $W(F)$ via the multiplicative structure (Lemma 10) over primes contributing to each $\Delta_i$-slice. Bound by $\tau_4$-style identities.

Approach A is cleaner if a 4-variable Dirichlet series with the right pole structure can be written down; Approach B is more explicit and dovetails with Hooley/Heath-Brown $\tau_k$-moment machinery.

**Chunk b.2 deliverable.** Choose one approach and write down the decoupled-over-primes formula. Define the 4-variable Dirichlet kernel or the 4-dyadic-box decomposition.

**Chunk b.3 deliverable.** Apply $\tau_k$-moment / Hooley bound to obtain $\mathcal{B}(N) \le C \cdot K(N)^2 \log^B N$ for explicit $B$.

**Chunk b.4 deliverable.** Verify the relaxation of constraint (iii) (disjointness of $\mathrm{supp}(d_i)$ from $\bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(F_i)$) costs at most a constant factor (or absorb into $K^c$).
