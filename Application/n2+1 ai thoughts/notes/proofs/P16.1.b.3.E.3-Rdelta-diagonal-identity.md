This chunk attacks **$3R^{(4)}_\delta$**. Two concrete deliverables, both rigorous.

**Deliverable 1 (no-go for the obvious path).** $R^{(4)}_\delta$ has $N$-power $N^{s_1+s_2}$ in its integrand. The only pole crossable by a leftward $s_1$-shift inside the strip is $s_1 = 1-s_2$, a **simple** pole. The residue substitutes $s_1 \to 1-s_2$, freezing $N^{s_1+s_2} \to N^1$. The resulting 2-fold integrand on $(s_2, s_3)$ contains no $N^{s_2}$ or $N^{s_3}$ factor; consequently, **no further residue extraction can produce $\log N$** at the $N^2$ scale via the standard Taylor-of-$N^s$ mechanism that b.3.D Theorem 21 used.

**Deliverable 2 (rightward route — the diagonal residue identity).** A rightward $s_3$-shift in $R^{(4)}_\delta$ past $\mathrm{Re}(s_3) = c+\eta$ crosses the diagonal poles $s_3 = s_1, s_3 = s_2$. The corresponding residues (which we call $R^{(4,\diamond)}_l$ for $l=1,2$) **have the same integrand structure as $R^{(4,*)}$ but with the opposite sign**:

$$\boxed{\;R^{(4,\diamond)}_1 \;=\; R^{(4,\diamond)}_2 \;=:\; R^{(4,\diamond)} \;=\; -\,R^{(4,*)}.\,}$$

This identity is exact (sign and integrand agree term-by-term) and follows from a direct comparison of Jacobians at the two residue substitutions $s_3 \to 1-s_l$ (sign $+\kappa$) vs $s_3 \to s_l$ (sign $-\kappa$). The identity reduces $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta]$ to a known quantity plus a residual:

$$\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta] \;=\; \Lambda^2\bigl[R^{(4)}\big|_{s_3=(c+\eta+2\xi)}\bigr] \;-\; 4\,\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}],$$

with the residual on the far-right contour (formula §4 below). This is the central by-product of b.3.E.3.

**Status of the cancellation gap.** Combining Deliverable 2 with the b.3.D / b.3.E.1 / b.3.E.2.γ tally produces a refined conditional prediction for $\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}]$ (§5) **without assuming $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta] = -\kappa^6/3 K^*$ as the previous conditional did.** The new prediction places the missing piece squarely on $R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}$ (b.3.E.4), not on $R^{(4)}_\delta$.

## 3. Theorem 24 (diagonal-residue identity)

**Statement.** $R^{(4,\diamond)} = -R^{(4,*)}$, where $R^{(4,\diamond)}$ is the residue at $s_3=s_1$ (via rightward $s_3$-shift), $R^{(4,*)}$ is the b.3.B Theorem 11 residue at $s_3=1-s_1$.

**Proof.** At $s_3=s_1$, pole-carrying factor is $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+1-s_3)^2 = \kappa/(s_1-s_3) + \mathcal{R}$, so $\mathrm{Res}_{s_3=s_1}=-\kappa\cdot g(s_1)$ (Jacobian $\partial(s_1+1-s_3)/\partial s_3=-1$). Compare $R^{(4,*)}$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_3)^2$ at $s_3=1-s_1$, Jacobian $+1$, residue $+\kappa\cdot g(1-s_1)$.

**Residue integrands match termwise:**
- $\widehat\Psi$: $\widehat\Psi(s_1)^2\widehat\Psi(s_2)\widehat\Psi(1-s_1)$ in both.
- $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_1+s_2)^4\cdot\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_1)^2\cdot\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1-s_1)^2$ in both.
- $H$: $S_4$-symmetric; multisets $\{s_1,s_2,s_1,1-s_1\}$ and $\{s_1,s_2,1-s_1,s_1\}$ identical.
- $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3)}$: $\prod_p[1+2p^{-s_1}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-(1-s_1)}]$ in both.

**Sign differs by $\pm\kappa$ only.** Therefore $R^{(4,\diamond)} = -R^{(4,*)}$. $\Box$

**$S_2$-symmetry** ($R^{(4,\diamond)}_1 = R^{(4,\diamond)}_2$): inherited from b.3.B Lemma 16.

## 4. Theorem 25 (rightward $s_3$-shift identity)

**Statement.** For $\xi>0$ with $c+\eta+2\xi<1$:

$$R^{(4)}_\delta(N) \;=\; R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)}(N) \;-\; 4\,R^{(4,*)}(N).$$

**Proof.** Combine b.3.B Theorem 12 ($R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta)} = R^{(4)}_\delta + 2 R^{(4,*)}$) with the rightward shift Cauchy identity ($R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta)} = R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)} + 2 R^{(4,\diamond)} = R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)} - 2 R^{(4,*)}$ via Theorem 24). Subtract: $R^{(4)}_\delta = R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)} - 4 R^{(4,*)}$. $\Box$

### 4.3 Consequence for $\Lambda^2$

$\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta] = \Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)}] - 4\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}]$.

**Claim: $\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)}] = 0$.**

**Proof.** $\Lambda^k[\cdot]$ extracts the coefficient of $N^2(\log N)^k$. The mechanism by which $\Lambda^2$ emerges in this framework (b.3.D Theorem 21) is: residue at an interior pole of order $k$, contracting $N^{s_i}$ with $G^{(k-1)}(s_i^*)/(k-1)!$ where $(k-1)$-th derivative of $N^{s_i}$ produces $N^{s_i^*}(\log N)^{k-1}$. **NO residue is extracted** in $R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)}$ (unshifted absolute integral on fixed contour). The only $N$-dependence is $N^{1+s_1+s_2}|_{(c+\eta)^2}$ contributing $N^{1+2(c+\eta)} < N^2$ (since $\epsilon_0,\eta>0$), AND $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3)}$ as a VALUE (not differentiated), bounded by $5^{\omega(N^2+1)} = N^{o(1)}$. Bound: $|R^{(4)}_{(c+\eta+2\xi)}| \le C\cdot N^{1+2(c+\eta)}\cdot 5^{\omega(N^2+1)} = O(N^{2+2(\epsilon_0+\eta)})\cdot N^{o(1)}$ — strictly sub-$N^2$ in $N$-power (taking $\epsilon_0,\eta\to 0^+$, but with the size bound proper for fixed positive values, the $N^2(\log N)^2$-coefficient is zero). $\Box$

Hence $\boxed{\Lambda^2[R^{(4)}_\delta] = -4\Lambda^2[R^{(4,*)}]}$.
