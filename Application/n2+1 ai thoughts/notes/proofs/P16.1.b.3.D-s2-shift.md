# P16.1.b.3.D. $s_2$-contour shift of $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$ and $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$: emergence of the second $\log N$

## Status

Fourth sub-chunk of P16.1.b.3 (continuation of `P16.1.b.3.C-s1-shift-logN.md`). The deliverable is the explicit $s_2$-contour shift of the two principal residue terms produced in b.3.C:

1. $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}(N)$ — the $N^{3/2}$-tier 1-fold integral whose integrand contains $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$ (triple pole at $s_2=1/2$).
2. $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N) = \kappa^4 N^2\Lambda\,\mathcal{J}_0 + \kappa^4 N^2\,\mathcal{J}_1 + 2\kappa^3\gamma_0 N^2\,\mathcal{J}_0$ — the $N^2\Lambda$-tier piece, with $\mathcal{J}_0, \mathcal{J}_1$ 1-fold integrals on $(c_2)$.

The pole crossed in each case is at $s_2=1/2$ (the simultaneous zero of $2s_2-1$ and $2-2s_2-1$, where $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ has its only pole on the strip).

**Headline result (Theorem 23 below).** The second $\log N$ of the iteration appears at the $N^2(\log N)^2$ scale in $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\mathrm{Res}}$ — emerging from the triple pole of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$ at $s_2=1/2$ combined with the Taylor expansion of $N^{s_2}=N^{1/2}\exp(u\log N)$ at $u=s_2-1/2=0$. **This corrects the source-of-second-$\log N$ prediction in b.3.C §8**, which attributed it to $R^{(4,*)}_{\log,\mathrm{Res}}$; we show below that $R^{(4,*)}_{\log,\mathrm{Res}}$ contributes only one factor of $\log N$ (at the $N^2\Lambda$ scale), because its integrand contains no $N^{s_2}$ factor — the $N$-power has been fully absorbed into the prefactor $\kappa^2 N$ in the b.3.C double-pole residue extraction.

We use $\Lambda := \log N$ and $u := s_2-1/2$ throughout.

## 1. Setup recap from b.3.C

From Theorem 15 of b.3.C:

$$
R^{(4,*)}(N) \;=\; R^{(4,*)}_{\delta'}(N) \;+\; R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}(N) \;+\; R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N),
$$

with

$$
R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}(N) \;=\; \frac{\kappa^3}{2}\,N^{3/2}\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\cdot\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_2)} N^{s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6\,H(1/2,s_2,1/2,1/2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2)\,ds_2,
$$

$$
R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N) \;=\; \kappa^2\,N\cdot\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(c_2)}\!\Bigl\{\kappa^2 N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\,[\Lambda+\Xi(s_2)] + 2\kappa\gamma_0 N\,J_{\log}(s_2)\Bigr\}\,ds_2,
$$

where $J_{\log}(s_2)=\widehat\Psi(1-s_2)^2\widehat\Psi(s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2\,H(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(1-s_2,s_2)$, and $\Xi(s_2)$ is the explicit bounded combination of logarithmic derivatives given in b.3.C Theorem 14. Constants: $\kappa=\mathrm{Res}_{w=1}\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2>0$, $\gamma_0=\mathcal{R}(1)$ from b.3.A Lemma 14. Contour: $c_2=c+\eta+\eta'\in(1/2,2/3)$.

Strip selection. We shift $s_2:(c_2)\to(\delta'')$ with $\delta''\in(1/3,\,1-c_2)$. Inside $[\delta'',c_2]$ the only candidate poles of the integrands are at $s_2=1/2$ (see §2-3 below).

**Notation convention.** Set $u:=s_2-1/2$. Write $f=O(u)$ to mean a function holomorphic at $u=0$ vanishing at $u=0$.

## 2. Pole structure of the $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$ integrand in $s_2$

Integrand: $\mathcal{F}_{1/2}(s_2):= N^{s_2}\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6\,H(1/2,s_2,1/2,1/2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2)$.

| factor | pole in $s_2$ | $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)$ at pole | order |
|---|---|---|---|
| $N^{s_2}$ | none | — | — |
| $\widehat\Psi(s_2)$ | $s_2=0$ | $0$ | simple |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$ | $s_2=1/2$ | $1/2$ | **triple** |
| $H(1/2,s_2,1/2,1/2)$ | none in $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)>1/3$ | — | — |
| $\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2)$ | none | — | — |

Inside the strip $\mathrm{Re}(s_2)\in[\delta'',c_2]\subset(1/3,2/3)$ the only pole is the triple pole at $s_2=1/2$.

*Order accounting for $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^6$.* $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2$ has a simple pole at $w=1$ with residue $\kappa$ (Lemma 14 of b.3.A). Hence $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^6=(\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2)^3$ has a pole of order $3$ at $w=1$, not order $6$. The substitution $w=s_2+1/2$ has unit Jacobian, so $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$ has a triple pole at $s_2=1/2$.

(b.3.C §8's offhand description "$\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$ has a 6th-order pole" was incorrect; the correct order is 3. The structural prediction it draws downstream — that a second $\log N$ appears at the $N^2$-tier — turns out correct, by the order-3 mechanism worked out below.)

## 3. Pole structure of the $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$ integrand in $s_2$

Integrand: $\mathcal{F}_{\log}(s_2):= J_{\log}(s_2)\,[\Lambda + \Xi(s_2)] + (2\gamma_0/\kappa)\,J_{\log}(s_2)\cdot\kappa$. (The full integrand of the $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$ formula is $\kappa^2 N J_{\log}[\Lambda+\Xi]+2\kappa\gamma_0 N J_{\log}$; factor out $\kappa^2 N$ and rearrange.) Equivalently $\mathcal{F}_{\log}(s_2)= J_{\log}(s_2)[\Lambda+\Xi(s_2)+2\gamma_0/\kappa]$, since $2\kappa\gamma_0 J_{\log}=(2\gamma_0/\kappa)\cdot\kappa^2 J_{\log}$ and the outer prefactor is $\kappa^2 N$.

**Note: $\mathcal{F}_{\log}$ contains no $N^{s_2}$ factor.** The $N^1$-prefactor in b.3.C Theorem 14 absorbed all $N$-dependence into $N^{s_1+s_2}|_{s_1=1-s_2}=N$.

Pole inventory of $J_{\log}(s_2)$ inside $[\delta'',c_2]$ (already given in b.3.C §8):

| factor | pole in $s_2$ | order |
|---|---|---|
| $\widehat\Psi(s_2)^2$ | $s_2=0$ (outside left) | — |
| $\widehat\Psi(1-s_2)^2$ | $s_2=1$ (outside right) | — |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2$ | $s_2=1/2$ | simple |
| $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2$ | $s_2=1/2$ | simple |
| $H(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)$ | none | — |
| $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(1-s_2,s_2)$ | none | — |

Inside the strip, $J_{\log}$ has a single double pole at $s_2=1/2$ (product of two coalescing simple poles).

Pole inventory of $\Xi(s_2)$ inside the strip (forward inventory in b.3.C §8, completed in §6 below): the only singular contribution is at $s_2=1/2$, from the two zeta-log-derivative pieces $4\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2-2s_2)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)$ and $-2\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2s_2)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)$. Simple pole; explicit residue computed in §6.

## 4. Laurent expansions at $s_2=1/2$

### 4.1 Laurent of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2$ at $w=1$ (recap)

From b.3.A Lemma 14:
$$
\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2 \;=\; \frac{\kappa}{w-1} + \gamma_0 + \gamma_1(w-1) + \gamma_2(w-1)^2 + O((w-1)^3),
$$
where $\gamma_0 := \mathcal{R}(1)$, $\gamma_k := \mathcal{R}^{(k)}(1)/k!$. We will use the substitution $w-1\to(w-1)$ with various Jacobians below.

### 4.2 Laurent of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6$ at $s_2=1/2$

Substitute $w=s_2+1/2$, $w-1=u$. Then $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^2=\kappa/u+\gamma_0+\gamma_1 u+O(u^2)$. Cubing:

$$
\boxed{\;\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6 \;=\; \frac{\kappa^3}{u^3} \;+\; \frac{3\kappa^2\gamma_0}{u^2} \;+\; \frac{3\kappa^2\gamma_1+3\kappa\gamma_0^2}{u} \;+\; A_0 \;+\; O(u)\,}
$$

where $A_0 := 3\kappa^2\gamma_2+6\kappa\gamma_0\gamma_1+\gamma_0^3$.

*Proof.* Let $f(u)=\kappa u^{-1}+g(u)$ with $g(u)=\gamma_0+\gamma_1 u+\gamma_2 u^2+\ldots$ Then $f^2 = \kappa^2 u^{-2}+2\kappa g(u)u^{-1}+g(u)^2$, $f^3 = f^2\cdot f$. Collecting powers of $u$ up to $u^0$ gives the stated coefficients (routine; the $u^{-2}$ coefficient $3\kappa^2\gamma_0$ comes from $\kappa^2 u^{-2}\cdot \gamma_0 + 2\kappa\gamma_0 u^{-1}\cdot\kappa u^{-1}$, etc.). $\Box$

### 4.3 Laurent of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2$ at $s_2=1/2$

Substitute $w=2s_2$, $w-1=2u$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2=\kappa/(2u)+\gamma_0+2\gamma_1 u+4\gamma_2 u^2+O(u^3)$.

Substitute $w=2-2s_2$, $w-1=-2u$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2=-\kappa/(2u)+\gamma_0-2\gamma_1 u+4\gamma_2 u^2+O(u^3)$.

Product (multiply the two Laurent series): coefficient of $u^n$ is $2^n\sum_{k+l=n}c_k c_l(-1)^l$ where $c_k$ are the Laurent coefficients of $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}^2$ at $w=1$. Explicit values:

| $n$ | computation | result |
|---|---|---|
| $-2$ | $c_{-1}^2(-1)\cdot 2^{-2}$ | $-\kappa^2/4$ |
| $-1$ | $c_{-1}c_0\cdot 2^{-1}+c_0 c_{-1}(-1)\cdot 2^{-1}$ | $0$ |
| $0$ | $c_{-1}c_1(-1)+c_0^2+c_1 c_{-1}(-1)$ | $\gamma_0^2-2\kappa\gamma_1$ |

So
$$
\boxed{\;\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2 \;=\; \frac{-\kappa^2/4}{u^2} \;+\; 0\cdot u^{-1} \;+\; (\gamma_0^2-2\kappa\gamma_1) \;+\; O(u)\,}
$$

— a **double pole with vanishing simple-pole residue**. The vanishing of the $u^{-1}$ coefficient is forced by the $u\to-u$ antisymmetry of $w=2s_2-1$ vs $w=1-2s_2$ in the two factors; physically the same as the standard observation that $\partial_s[(s)(s-1)]$ is symmetric under $s\to 1-s$.

### 4.4 Symmetry of the $J_{\log}$-prefactor $Q(s_2)$

Write $J_{\log}(s_2) = \zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)^2\,\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)^2\cdot Q(s_2)$, where

$$
Q(s_2) \;:=\; \widehat\Psi(1-s_2)^2\,\widehat\Psi(s_2)^2\,H(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(1-s_2,s_2)
$$

is holomorphic at $s_2=1/2$ (each factor is). Crucially $Q$ satisfies

**Lemma 19 ($u\to-u$ symmetry of $Q$).** $Q(1/2+u)=Q(1/2-u)$, hence $Q'(1/2)=0$ and more generally all odd derivatives of $Q$ at $1/2$ vanish.

*Proof.* Under $s_2\to 1-s_2$:
- $\widehat\Psi(1-s_2)^2\cdot\widehat\Psi(s_2)^2$ is invariant (swaps the two factors).
- $H$ is fully symmetric in its four arguments (b.3.A §3 derives $H=\mathcal{D}_F^{\mathcal{F}}/\prod_{i<j}\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_i+s_j)^2$ from the $S_4$-symmetric Euler product $\mathcal{D}_F^{\mathcal{F}}$ of b.2 Lemma 12; both numerator and denominator are individually $S_4$-symmetric, so $H$ is $S_4$-symmetric). The multiset $\{1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2\}$ is invariant under $s_2\to 1-s_2$, so $H(1-s_2,s_2,s_2,1-s_2)$ is unchanged.
- $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(1-s_2,s_2)=\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}[1+2p^{-(1-s_2)}+2p^{-s_2}]$ is symmetric in $s_2\leftrightarrow 1-s_2$.

All three pieces invariant, hence $Q(s_2)=Q(1-s_2)$, i.e., $Q$ is even in $u=s_2-1/2$. $\Box$

**Corollary.** $J_{\log}(s_2)$ has the Laurent expansion at $s_2=1/2$
$$
J_{\log}(s_2) \;=\; \frac{-\kappa^2 Q_0/4}{u^2} \;+\; \frac{0}{u} \;+\; \Bigl[(\gamma_0^2-2\kappa\gamma_1)Q_0 - \tfrac{\kappa^2 Q_2}{8}\Bigr] \;+\; O(u),
$$
where $Q_k:= Q^{(k)}(1/2)$. The $u^{-1}$ coefficient is zero: it would come from $-\kappa^2 Q_1/(4u)$, and $Q_1=0$ by Lemma 19.

So $J_{\log}$ has a clean double pole with $\boxed{\mathrm{Res}_{s_2=1/2} J_{\log}(s_2)=0}$.

### 4.5 Laurent of $\Xi(s_2)$ at $s_2=1/2$ (Theorem 20)

**Theorem 20 (Sign-and-multiplicity of $\Xi$ at $s_2=1/2$).** $\Xi(s_2)$ has a simple pole at $s_2=1/2$ with residue $3/2$:

$$
\Xi(s_2) \;=\; \frac{3/2}{u} \;+\; \xi_0 \;+\; \xi_1\,u \;+\; O(u^2),
$$

where $\xi_0$ is the explicit finite constant given below (sum of the regular parts of the six logarithmic-derivative pieces). The $u^1$ coefficient $\xi_1$ is also a finite constant, equal to the sum of the second-order Taylor coefficients of each log-derivative piece at $u=0$; its explicit closed form is deferred to b.3.E (Remark: $\xi_1$ enters Theorem 22 below only in the sub-leading $N^2$-tier residue, not in the leading $N^2(\log N)^2$ tier, so its explicit form is not needed for the headline result of this chunk).

*Proof.* $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2$ has a simple pole at $w=1$ with residue $\kappa$, so $\log\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2 = -\log(w-1)+\log\kappa+(\gamma_0/\kappa)(w-1)+O((w-1)^2)$, whence

$$
\frac{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(w)^2}{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2} \;=\; -\frac{1}{w-1} + \frac{\gamma_0}{\kappa} + O(w-1).
$$

But $\partial_w\log\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)^2=2\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(w)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)$, so

$$
\frac{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(w)}{\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(w)} \;=\; -\frac{1}{2(w-1)} + \frac{\gamma_0}{2\kappa} + O(w-1).
$$

Substituting:
- $w=2s_2$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2s_2)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)=-1/(2(2s_2-1))+\gamma_0/(2\kappa)+O(u)=-1/(4u)+\gamma_0/(2\kappa)+O(u)$.
- $w=2-2s_2$: $\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2-2s_2)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)=-1/(2(1-2s_2))+\gamma_0/(2\kappa)+O(u)=1/(4u)+\gamma_0/(2\kappa)+O(u)$.

Singular parts in $\Xi$:
- From $+4\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2-2s_2)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2-2s_2)$: $4\cdot 1/(4u) = 1/u$.
- From $-2\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}'(2s_2)/\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(2s_2)$: $-2\cdot(-1/(4u)) = 1/(2u)$.

Total singular part: $1/u + 1/(2u) = (3/2)/u$. ✓

The constant $\xi_0$ is then the sum of:
- $4\cdot\gamma_0/(2\kappa) - 2\cdot\gamma_0/(2\kappa) = 2\gamma_0/\kappa - \gamma_0/\kappa = \gamma_0/\kappa$ from the two zeta-log-derivatives.
- $2\widehat\Psi'(1/2)/\widehat\Psi(1/2) - \widehat\Psi'(1/2)/\widehat\Psi(1/2) = \widehat\Psi'(1/2)/\widehat\Psi(1/2)$ from the $\widehat\Psi$-log-derivatives evaluated at $s_2=1/2$ (where both $\widehat\Psi(1-s_2)$ and $\widehat\Psi(s_2)$ collapse to $\widehat\Psi(1/2)$).
- $(\partial_1 H - \partial_3 H + \partial_4 H)/H$ at $(1/2,1/2,1/2,1/2)$. Since $H$ is fully symmetric in its four arguments, all four partial derivatives $\partial_i H$ at the diagonal point $\vec{1/2}$ are equal. Hence $(\partial_1-\partial_3+\partial_4)H/H\big|_{\vec{1/2}} = \partial_1 H/H\big|_{\vec{1/2}}$.
- $\partial_{s_1}\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}/\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}|_{(1-s_2,s_2)}$ at $s_2=1/2$: from $\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)}(s_1,s_2)=\prod_p[1+2p^{-s_1}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-(1-s_1)}]$, $\partial_{s_1}\log\mathcal{D}_d^{(3,1)} = \sum_p\frac{-2(\log p)p^{-s_1}+(\log p)p^{-(1-s_1)}}{1+2p^{-s_1}+p^{-s_2}+p^{-(1-s_1)}}$. At $(s_1,s_2)=(1/2,1/2)$: numerator $=(-2+1)(\log p)p^{-1/2}=-(\log p)p^{-1/2}$, denominator $=1+4p^{-1/2}$. So this contribution is $-\sum_{p\mid N^2+1}(\log p)p^{-1/2}/(1+4p^{-1/2})$.

So
$$
\xi_0 \;=\; \frac{\gamma_0}{\kappa} \;+\; \frac{\widehat\Psi'(1/2)}{\widehat\Psi(1/2)} \;+\; \frac{\partial_1 H}{H}\bigg|_{\vec{1/2}} \;-\; \sum_{p\mid N^2+1}\frac{(\log p)\,p^{-1/2}}{1+4p^{-1/2}}.
$$

(The $p$-sum depends mildly on $N$ via $\omega(N^2+1)$; it is $O(\log N)$ in the worst case, but this enters $\xi_0$ at the SAME order as $\Lambda=\log N$, hence is comparable, not dominant, with respect to the leading-$\Lambda$ count.) $\Box$

**Sanity.** The residue $3/2$ is independent of $H, \widehat\Psi, \mathcal{D}_d$. It comes purely from the zeta-piece chain rule with multiplicities $4$ and $-2$, weighted by the universal log-derivative residue $\mp 1/2$.

## 5. Triple-pole residue for $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$ (Theorem 21): the second $\log N$

**Theorem 21 (Triple-pole residue at $s_2=1/2$ of the $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$ integrand).**

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_2=1/2}\,\mathcal{F}_{1/2}(s_2) \;=\; \frac{\kappa^3 K_0}{2}\,N^{1/2}\Lambda^2 \;+\; \bigl[\kappa^3 K_1 + 3\kappa^2\gamma_0 K_0\bigr]\,N^{1/2}\Lambda \;+\; \bigl[\tfrac{\kappa^3 K_2}{2} + 3\kappa^2\gamma_0 K_1 + (3\kappa^2\gamma_1+3\kappa\gamma_0^2)K_0\bigr]\,N^{1/2},
$$

where $K_k := K^{(k)}(1/2)$ with $K(s_2):=\widehat\Psi(s_2)\,H(1/2,s_2,1/2,1/2)\,\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(s_2)$.

*Proof.* Write $\mathcal{F}_{1/2}(s_2)=\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6\cdot G(s_2)$, with $G(s_2):=N^{s_2}K(s_2)$ holomorphic at $s_2=1/2$. By §4.2 the first factor has Laurent
$$
\zeta_{\mathcal{Q}}(s_2+1/2)^6 \;=\; \frac{A}{u^3} + \frac{B}{u^2} + \frac{C}{u} + O(1), \quad A=\kappa^3,\;B=3\kappa^2\gamma_0,\;C=3\kappa^2\gamma_1+3\kappa\gamma_0^2.
$$
For a function $\sum_k a_k u^k$ multiplied by a holomorphic $G(s_2)=\sum_j G^{(j)}(1/2)u^j/j!$, the residue at $u=0$ (the $u^{-1}$ coefficient) is $A\cdot G''(1/2)/2 + B\cdot G'(1/2) + C\cdot G(1/2)$ (standard).

Compute $G^{(j)}(1/2)$: $G(s_2)=N^{s_2}K(s_2)$, $G^{(j)}(s_2) = \sum_{k=0}^j\binom{j}{k}\Lambda^k N^{s_2}K^{(j-k)}(s_2)$. At $s_2=1/2$:
$$
G^{(j)}(1/2) \;=\; N^{1/2}\sum_{k=0}^j\binom{j}{k}\Lambda^k\,K_{j-k}.
$$
So $G(1/2)=N^{1/2}K_0$, $G'(1/2)=N^{1/2}(\Lambda K_0+K_1)$, $G''(1/2)=N^{1/2}(\Lambda^2 K_0+2\Lambda K_1+K_2)$.

Substitute: $A\cdot G''(1/2)/2 + B\cdot G'(1/2) + C\cdot G(1/2)$
$= \kappa^3 N^{1/2}(\Lambda^2 K_0+2\Lambda K_1+K_2)/2 + 3\kappa^2\gamma_0 N^{1/2}(\Lambda K_0+K_1) + (3\kappa^2\gamma_1+3\kappa\gamma_0^2)N^{1/2}K_0$
$= N^{1/2}\bigl\{\tfrac{\kappa^3 K_0}{2}\Lambda^2 + (\kappa^3 K_1+3\kappa^2\gamma_0 K_0)\Lambda + [\tfrac{\kappa^3 K_2}{2}+3\kappa^2\gamma_0 K_1+(3\kappa^2\gamma_1+3\kappa\gamma_0^2)K_0]\bigr\}$. $\Box$

**Definition.** $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\delta''}(N) := \frac{\kappa^3}{2}N^{3/2}\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\cdot\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\delta'')}\mathcal{F}_{1/2}(s_2)\,ds_2$,
$R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\mathrm{Res}}(N) := \frac{\kappa^3}{2}N^{3/2}\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3\cdot\mathrm{Res}_{s_2=1/2}\mathcal{F}_{1/2}(s_2)$.

**Corollary (the second $\log N$).**

$$
\boxed{\;R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\mathrm{Res}}(N) \;=\; \frac{\kappa^6\,K_0\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3}{4}\,N^2\,(\log N)^2 \;+\; C_1(N)\,N^2\log N \;+\; C_0(N)\,N^2\,}
$$

where $C_1(N), C_0(N)$ are bounded by $O(5^{\omega(N^2+1)})$ uniformly (they are products of $\widehat\Psi,H$ derivatives at $\vec{1/2}$ times polynomial expressions in $\kappa,\gamma_0,\gamma_1$ times $K_k$, and each $K_k$ contains the same divisor-product $\mathcal{D}_d^{(2,1)}(1/2)=\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}[1+4p^{-1/2}]\le 5^{\omega(N^2+1)}$).

Explicitly the leading coefficient:
$$
\frac{\kappa^6}{4}\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^4\,H(1/2,1/2,1/2,1/2)\,\prod_{p\mid N^2+1}\bigl[1+4p^{-1/2}\bigr].
$$

This is **strictly positive** (each factor positive), so the leading $(\log N)^2$ contribution has a definite sign — useful for downstream cancellation arguments in b.3.E.

## 6. Residue extractions for $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$ (Theorem 22): only one $\log N$ tier

**Theorem 22 (Residues at $s_2=1/2$ for $\mathcal{J}_0,\mathcal{J}_1$).**

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_2=1/2}\,J_{\log}(s_2) \;=\; 0,
$$

$$
\mathrm{Res}_{s_2=1/2}\,\bigl[J_{\log}(s_2)\,\Xi(s_2)\bigr] \;=\; -\frac{\kappa^2\,Q_0\,\xi_1}{4} \;+\; \frac{3\,J_0}{2}, \quad J_0 := (\gamma_0^2-2\kappa\gamma_1)Q_0 - \tfrac{\kappa^2 Q_2}{8}.
$$

Both are finite constants in $N$.

*Proof.* First, by Lemma 19 + §4.3, $J_{\log}(s_2)$ has Laurent $-\kappa^2 Q_0/(4u^2)+0\cdot u^{-1}+J_0+O(u)$. Its $u^{-1}$ coefficient (the residue) is $0$.

Second, multiply the Laurent of $J_{\log}$ by the Laurent of $\Xi$:
$$
J_{\log}\cdot\Xi \;=\; \Bigl[\frac{-\kappa^2 Q_0/4}{u^2} + 0 + J_0 + O(u)\Bigr]\cdot\Bigl[\frac{3/2}{u} + \xi_0 + \xi_1 u + O(u^2)\Bigr].
$$
The $u^{-1}$ coefficient: $(-\kappa^2 Q_0/4)\cdot\xi_1 + 0\cdot\xi_0 + J_0\cdot(3/2) = -\kappa^2 Q_0\xi_1/4 + 3J_0/2$. $\Box$

**Corollary.** Define
$$
\mathcal{J}_{0,\delta''} \;:=\; \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\delta'')}J_{\log}(s_2)\,ds_2, \qquad \mathcal{J}_{1,\delta''} \;:=\; \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\delta'')}J_{\log}(s_2)\,\Xi(s_2)\,ds_2.
$$
Then by Cauchy + Theorem 22:
$$
\mathcal{J}_0 \;=\; \mathcal{J}_{0,\delta''}, \qquad \mathcal{J}_1 \;=\; \mathcal{J}_{1,\delta''} \;+\; \bigl[-\kappa^2 Q_0\xi_1/4 + 3J_0/2\bigr].
$$

In particular $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N) = \kappa^4 N^2\Lambda\,\mathcal{J}_{0,\delta''} + \kappa^4 N^2\,\bigl\{\mathcal{J}_{1,\delta''}+(-\kappa^2 Q_0\xi_1/4+3J_0/2)\bigr\} + 2\kappa^3\gamma_0 N^2\,\mathcal{J}_{0,\delta''}$.

**No $\Lambda^2$ tier appears in $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$.** The $\Lambda$ factor in front of $\mathcal{J}_0$ is constant in $s_2$, so neither contour shift nor residue extraction generates a second $\Lambda$. The leading tier of $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$ is $N^2\Lambda$, with coefficient $\kappa^4\,\mathcal{J}_{0,\delta''}$.

## 7. Combined $s_2$-shift identity (Theorem 23)

**Theorem 23 ($s_2$-contour shift of $R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}$ and $R^{(4,*)}_{\log}$).** For $\delta''\in(1/3,\,1-c_2)$,

$$
\boxed{\;R^{(4,*)}_{1/2}(N) \;=\; R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\delta''}(N) \;+\; R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\mathrm{Res}}(N)\,},
$$

$$
\boxed{\;R^{(4,*)}_{\log}(N) \;=\; R^{(4,*)}_{\log,\delta''}(N) \;+\; R^{(4,*)}_{\log,\mathrm{Res}}(N)\,},
$$

with the second-summand-tiered structure

$$
\begin{aligned}
R^{(4,*)}_{1/2,\mathrm{Res}}(N) \;&=\; \frac{\kappa^6\,K_0\,\widehat\Psi(1/2)^3}{4}\,N^2(\log N)^2 \;+\; O(N^2\log N)\cdot 5^{\omega(N^2+1)},\\

Residues from Theorems 21, 22. Combine. $\Box$

## 9. Correction to b.3.C §8 prediction (with apology)

The b.3.C §8 forward inventory and the b.3.C "Next-session pickup hint" attributed the second $\log N$ to $R^{(4,*)}_{\log,\mathrm{Res}}$, via the triple pole of $J_{\log}\cdot[\Lambda+\Xi]$ at $s_2=1/2$. **This attribution was wrong** (in two specific ways).

Both b.3.C errors are bookkeeping issues; none invalidate any earlier b.3.A/B/C theorem. The corrected attribution does not require revisiting any boxed formula in those chunks — only the b.3.C §8 forward-inventory paragraph is amended (via this §9).
