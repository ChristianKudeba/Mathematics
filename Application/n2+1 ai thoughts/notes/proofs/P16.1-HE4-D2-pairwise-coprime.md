# P16.1. HE-4: rigorous (D2) bound and pairwise-coprime (D3) bound

## Status

Chunk completion for sub-question P16.1 of `P16-Th-fourth-moment-chaining.md`.

**This file proves:**
1. A clean reformulation of the frequency set: $\beta_{e,\epsilon} = (N + a)/e \pmod 1$ where $a^2 \equiv -1 \pmod e$ (Lemma 1).
2. (D2) bound: the number of ordered pairs $((e_1, a_1), (e_2, a_2)) \in \mathcal{T}(N)^2$ with distinct labels and equal frequency satisfies
$$\#(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) \;\le\; 3^{\omega(N^2+1)} \cdot K(N) \;=\; N^{1+o(1)}$$ (Theorem 2).
3. Pairwise-coprime (D3) bound: the number of 4-tuples in $\mathcal{T}(N)^4$ with $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$, pairwise-coprime $e_i$, and no pairing structure satisfies
$$\#(D3)_{\mathrm{pwc}}(N) \;\le\; \tau(N^2+1)^4 \;=\; N^{o(1)}$$ (Theorem 3).

**This file does not prove:** the full HE-4 bound. The residual hard case — (D3) tuples with at least one prime $p$ shared by two or more of the $e_i$ and no $|S_p|=1$ forcing — is identified as the open chunk P16.1.next.

## 1. Setup recap

From `P16-Th-fourth-moment-chaining.md` §1, the frequency set of $T_h(N)$ as a trigonometric polynomial in $h$ is
$$
\mathcal{F}(N) \;=\; \{\beta_{e,\epsilon} : 2 \le e \le N \text{ sf-good odd}, \, \epsilon \in \{\pm 1\}^{\omega(e)}\},
$$
where
$$
\beta_{e,\epsilon} \;=\; \frac{N}{e} + \alpha_\epsilon(e) \pmod 1, \qquad \alpha_\epsilon(e) \;=\; \sum_{p \mid e} \epsilon_p \cdot \frac{r_p}{p} \cdot (e/p)_p^{-1} \pmod 1,
$$
and "$e$ sf-good" means $e$ squarefree with every prime divisor $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$, $r_p$ is the (chosen) root of $x^2 + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod p$ in $\{1, \dots, p-1\}$, and $(e/p)_p^{-1}$ is the inverse of $e/p$ modulo $p$.

## 2. CRT reformulation of the frequencies

**Lemma 1 (CRT form of $\beta_{e,\epsilon}$).** For each sf-good odd $e \ge 2$ and $\epsilon \in \{\pm 1\}^{\omega(e)}$, define $a_\epsilon(e) \in \{0, 1, \dots, e-1\}$ as the unique residue with
$$
a_\epsilon(e) \equiv \epsilon_p \cdot r_p \pmod p \quad \text{for every } p \mid e.
$$
Then:
(i) $a_\epsilon(e)^2 \equiv -1 \pmod e$.
(ii) The map $\epsilon \mapsto a_\epsilon(e)$ is a bijection from $\{\pm 1\}^{\omega(e)}$ onto the set $\mathcal{R}(e) := \{a \in \{0, \dots, e-1\} : a^2 \equiv -1 \pmod e\}$ of square roots of $-1$ mod $e$.
(iii) $\beta_{e,\epsilon} \equiv (N + a_\epsilon(e))/e \pmod 1$.

*Proof.*
(i) For each $p \mid e$: $a_\epsilon(e)^2 \equiv \epsilon_p^2 r_p^2 \equiv 1 \cdot (-1) \equiv -1 \pmod p$. By CRT, $a_\epsilon(e)^2 \equiv -1 \pmod e$.

(ii) Injective: $a_\epsilon(e) = a_{\epsilon'}(e)$ forces $\epsilon_p r_p \equiv \epsilon'_p r_p \pmod p$ for each $p \mid e$; since $r_p \not\equiv 0 \pmod p$, $\epsilon_p = \epsilon'_p$. Surjective: any $a \in \mathcal{R}(e)$ satisfies $a^2 \equiv -1 \pmod p$ for each $p \mid e$, so $a \equiv \pm r_p \pmod p$, i.e., $a \equiv \epsilon_p r_p \pmod p$ for some $\epsilon_p \in \{\pm 1\}$. The vector $\epsilon$ is uniquely determined by $a$, so $a = a_\epsilon(e)$. The cardinalities match: $|\{\pm 1\}^{\omega(e)}| = 2^{\omega(e)} = |\mathcal{R}(e)|$.

(iii) Multiply $\alpha_\epsilon(e) = \sum_p \epsilon_p (r_p/p) (e/p)_p^{-1}$ by $e$:
$$e \cdot \alpha_\epsilon(e) \;=\; \sum_{p \mid e} \epsilon_p r_p \cdot (e/p) \cdot (e/p)_p^{-1}.$$
For each $p \mid e$, the factor $(e/p) \cdot (e/p)_p^{-1}$ is $\equiv 1 \pmod p$ and $\equiv 0 \pmod q$ for every prime $q \mid e$ with $q \ne p$ (since $q \mid e/p$). Hence
$$e \cdot \alpha_\epsilon(e) \;\equiv\; \epsilon_p r_p \pmod p \text{ for every } p \mid e,$$
so by CRT, $e \cdot \alpha_\epsilon(e) \equiv a_\epsilon(e) \pmod e$, i.e., $\alpha_\epsilon(e) \equiv a_\epsilon(e)/e \pmod 1$. Adding $N/e$ gives (iii). $\square$

**Notation.** Henceforth we work with the index set
$$
\mathcal{T}(N) \;:=\; \{(e, a) : 2 \le e \le N, e \text{ sf-good odd}, a \in \mathcal{R}(e)\},
$$
with $|\mathcal{T}(N)| = K(N) := \sum_{2 \le e \le N, \text{ sf-good odd}} 2^{\omega(e)}$, and frequencies
$$
\beta_{e,a} \;:=\; (N + a)/e \pmod 1, \qquad (e, a) \in \mathcal{T}(N).
$$

(Note: in `P16-Th-fourth-moment-chaining.md`, frequencies were written as $\beta_{e,\epsilon}$; the identification $\epsilon \leftrightarrow a$ via Lemma 1 lets us drop the $\epsilon$-index in favor of the residue $a$.)

## 3. (D2) bound

**Theorem 2 ((D2) self-pair bound).** Let
$$
(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) \;:=\; \#\bigl\{ ((e_1, a_1), (e_2, a_2)) \in \mathcal{T}(N)^2 : (e_1, a_1) \ne (e_2, a_2), \, \beta_{e_1, a_1} \equiv \beta_{e_2, a_2} \pmod 1 \bigr\}.
$$
Then
$$
(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) \;\le\; 3^{\omega(N^2+1)} \cdot K(N).
$$
In particular, by Wigert's bound $\omega(m) = O(\log m / \log\log m)$, we have $3^{\omega(N^2+1)} = N^{o(1)}$, so $(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) = N^{1+o(1)}$.

*Proof.*

**Step 1: $e_1 = e_2$ case is empty.** If $e_1 = e_2 = e$, then $\beta_{e,a_1} \equiv \beta_{e,a_2} \pmod 1$ means $(a_1 - a_2)/e \equiv 0 \pmod 1$, hence $e \mid a_1 - a_2$. Since $0 \le a_1, a_2 < e$, $a_1 = a_2$, contradicting the distinct-label condition. So we may assume $e_1 \ne e_2$.

**Step 2: Decomposition.** Set $d := \gcd(e_1, e_2)$, $u := e_1/d$, $v := e_2/d$. Since $e_1, e_2$ are squarefree, $\gcd(u, v) = 1$ and $\gcd(d, uv) = 1$; all of $u, v, d$ are sf-good odd, and $u \ne v$ (as $e_1 \ne e_2$). The equation $\beta_1 \equiv \beta_2 \pmod 1$ becomes, after multiplying by $\mathrm{lcm}(e_1, e_2) = duv$,
$$
v (N + a_1) - u (N + a_2) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod{duv}. \tag{$\star$}
$$

**Step 3: $u v \mid N^2 + 1$.** Reduce ($\star$) modulo $u$: since $\gcd(u, dv) = 1$, ($\star$) gives $v(N + a_1) \equiv 0 \pmod u$, hence $u \mid N + a_1$. Combined with $a_1^2 \equiv -1 \pmod u$ (from $u \mid e_1$ and $a_1^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{e_1}$): $N^2 \equiv (-a_1)^2 \equiv -1 \pmod u$, i.e., $u \mid N^2 + 1$. Symmetrically $v \mid N^2 + 1$. Since $\gcd(u, v) = 1$, $uv \mid N^2 + 1$.

**Step 4: Per-prime constraint on $d$.** Reduce ($\star$) modulo a prime $p \mid d$ (so $\gcd(p, uv) = 1$, hence $u, v$ are units mod $p$). Using $a_1, a_2 \equiv \pm r_p \pmod p$ (square roots of $-1$ mod $p$), write $a_1 \equiv \eta_1 r_p$, $a_2 \equiv \eta_2 r_p \pmod p$ with $\eta_1, \eta_2 \in \{+1, -1\}$. Then ($\star$) mod $p$ becomes
$$
v(N + \eta_1 r_p) - u(N + \eta_2 r_p) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod p,
$$
i.e.,
$$
r_p (v \eta_1 - u \eta_2) \;\equiv\; (u - v) N \pmod p. \tag{$\diamond_p$}
$$
We claim ($\diamond_p$) admits at most 2 solutions $(\eta_1, \eta_2) \in \{\pm 1\}^2$.

Consider the map $\Phi: \{\pm 1\}^2 \to \mathbb{F}_p$, $(\eta_1, \eta_2) \mapsto v \eta_1 - u \eta_2$. Its image is the multiset $\{v - u, \, v + u, \, -v - u, \, -v + u\}$, i.e., $\{\pm(v - u), \pm(v + u)\}$. We bound the largest fiber size of $\Phi$:

- *Case $p \nmid (u - v)$ and $p \nmid (u + v)$:* the four values $\pm(v-u), \pm(v+u)$ are pairwise distinct mod $p$ (none is $0$, and $\pm(v-u) \ne \pm(v+u)$ since $\pm 2u, \pm 2v \not\equiv 0 \pmod p$ — recall $p > 2$ and $\gcd(p, uv) = 1$). So $\Phi$ is injective; every fiber has size $\le 1$.
- *Case $u \equiv v \pmod p$:* then $v - u \equiv 0$, $u - v \equiv 0$, so $\Phi(1,1) = \Phi(-1,-1) = 0$. The other two values $\Phi(1, -1) = v + u, \Phi(-1, 1) = -(v+u)$ are nonzero (since $p \nmid (u+v)$ here, else $p \mid 2u$ and $p \mid u$, contradiction). Fiber sizes: $\{0 : 2,\ \pm(u+v) : 1\}$. Largest fiber 2.
- *Case $u \equiv -v \pmod p$:* symmetric. $\Phi(1, -1) = v + u \equiv 0$ and $\Phi(-1, 1) = -(v+u) \equiv 0$, so fiber over $0$ has size 2; remaining values $\pm(v - u) = \pm 2v$ each have one preimage. Largest fiber 2.
- *Cases $u \equiv \pm v$ simultaneously* would force $2u \equiv 0 \pmod p$, hence $p \mid u$ — contradiction.

In every case the largest fiber of $\Phi$ has size $\le 2$. Since ($\diamond_p$) is the equation $r_p \cdot \Phi(\eta_1, \eta_2) \equiv (u - v) N \pmod p$, and $r_p \ne 0 \pmod p$, the number of solutions $(\eta_1, \eta_2)$ equals the size of the fiber of $\Phi$ over the specific value $r_p^{-1}(u - v) N \pmod p$, which is $\le 2$.

**Step 5: Counting.** Fix $(u, v, d)$ with $uv \mid N^2 + 1$, $\gcd(d, uv) = 1$, $du \le N$, $dv \le N$, all sf-good odd. The pair $(a_1, a_2) \in \mathcal{R}(du) \times \mathcal{R}(dv)$ is determined by CRT data $(a_1 \bmod u,\ a_1 \bmod d,\ a_2 \bmod v,\ a_2 \bmod d)$ with $a_1 \bmod u \in \mathcal{R}(u)$, $a_1 \bmod d \in \mathcal{R}(d)$, $a_2 \bmod v \in \mathcal{R}(v)$, $a_2 \bmod d \in \mathcal{R}(d)$. The constraint ($\star$) is equivalent (by CRT, using $\gcd(u, v) = \gcd(d, uv) = 1$) to its three reductions:
- ($\star$) mod $u$: forces $a_1 \equiv -N \pmod u$ (Step 3). Since $u \mid N^2 + 1$, this residue lies in $\mathcal{R}(u)$ — exactly 1 of the $2^{\omega(u)}$ choices.
- ($\star$) mod $v$: forces $a_2 \equiv -N \pmod v$. Exactly 1 of $2^{\omega(v)}$.
- ($\star$) mod $d$: by Step 4 applied prime-by-prime and CRT over $p \mid d$, the joint pair $(a_1 \bmod d, a_2 \bmod d) \in \mathcal{R}(d) \times \mathcal{R}(d)$ has at most $2^{\omega(d)}$ valid choices (out of $4^{\omega(d)} = |\mathcal{R}(d)|^2$).

Total count of $(a_1, a_2)$ for fixed $(u, v, d)$: at most $1 \cdot 1 \cdot 2^{\omega(d)} = 2^{\omega(d)}$.

Summing:
$$
(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) \;\le\; \sum_{\substack{(u, v): uv \mid N^2+1 \\ \gcd(u,v)=1, \, uv \text{ sf-good odd}}} \;\sum_{\substack{d \le N/\max(u,v) \\ d \text{ sf-good odd} \\ \gcd(d, uv) = 1}} 2^{\omega(d)}.
$$

**Step 6: Estimate.** The inner sum is bounded by $\sum_{d \le N} 2^{\omega(d)}$ over sf-good odd $d$, which is $\le K(N)$ by definition (extending to all $d \le N$ rather than $d \le N/\max(u,v)$ is an upper bound).

The outer sum counts ordered pairs $(u, v)$ of coprime sf-good odd integers with $uv \mid N^2 + 1$. Each prime $p \mid N^2 + 1$ that is $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$ — which is every odd prime divisor of $N^2 + 1$, since $N^2 + 1$ has no prime divisor $\equiv 3 \pmod 4$ — has three independent placement choices: in $u$, in $v$, or in neither. The prime $p = 2$, if present, is excluded by the sf-good odd condition. Hence the count of valid ordered $(u, v)$ is at most $3^{\omega_{\mathrm{odd}}(N^2+1)} \le 3^{\omega(N^2+1)}$.

Since $(u, v)$ is ordered (via $u = e_1/d, v = e_2/d$), the count $(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}$ is for ordered pairs $((e_1, a_1), (e_2, a_2))$ as desired; no further factor of 2 is needed.

Combining:
$$
(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) \;\le\; 3^{\omega(N^2+1)} \cdot K(N).
$$

By Wigert, $\omega(m) \le (1 + o(1)) \log m / \log\log m$ uniformly, so $3^{\omega(N^2+1)} = \exp(O(\log N / \log\log N)) = N^{o(1)}$. With $K(N) = O(N \log^{O(1)} N)$ (Selberg–Delange on $\sum 2^{\omega(e)}$ over sf-good odd $e$), $(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) = N^{1+o(1)}$. $\square$

**Remark.** The bound is essentially sharp at $N$ for which $N^2 + 1$ has many small prime factors (the smooth-shift case); at "generic" $N$, $(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N)$ is much smaller. The Wigert bound is uniform but not tight; pointwise, $\tau(N^2+1)$ is $(\log N)^{O(1)}$ on average.

## 4. Pairwise-coprime (D3) bound

**Theorem 3 (Pairwise-coprime (D3) bound).** Let
$$
(D3)_{\mathrm{pwc}}(N) \;:=\; \#\Bigl\{ ((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{T}(N)^4 \;:\; e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4 \text{ pairwise coprime}, \, \beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1 \Bigr\}.
$$
Then
$$
(D3)_{\mathrm{pwc}}(N) \;\le\; \tau(N^2+1)^4 \;=\; N^{o(1)}.
$$

*Proof.* Suppose $e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4$ are pairwise coprime, sf-good odd, in $[2, N]$. Set $L := e_1 e_2 e_3 e_4 = \mathrm{lcm}(e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4)$ and $L_i := L/e_i$. The equation $\beta_1 + \beta_2 - \beta_3 - \beta_4 \equiv 0 \pmod 1$ becomes, after multiplying by $L$:
$$
L_1 (N + a_1) + L_2 (N + a_2) - L_3 (N + a_3) - L_4 (N + a_4) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod L. \tag{$\star\star$}
$$
Fix $i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$. For each $j \ne i$, $e_i \mid L_j$ (since pairwise coprime), so the $j$-term contributes $0$ mod $e_i$. Hence ($\star\star$) mod $e_i$ reduces to
$$
\pm L_i (N + a_i) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod{e_i}.
$$
Since $\gcd(L_i, e_i) = 1$, we conclude $e_i \mid N + a_i$. Combined with $a_i^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{e_i}$: $N^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{e_i}$, i.e., $e_i \mid N^2 + 1$. Additionally, $a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i}$ is uniquely determined (one of the $2^{\omega(e_i)}$ square roots of $-1$ mod $e_i$, namely the one congruent to $-N$).

So each pairwise-coprime (D3) tuple is determined by a choice of four (ordered) divisors $e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4$ of $N^2 + 1$ that are pairwise coprime, sf-good odd, in $[2, N]$. The number of such ordered 4-tuples is at most $\tau(N^2 + 1)^4$ (without coprimality and sf-good odd constraints; both are upper-bound preserving).

Conversely, every such ordered 4-tuple satisfies ($\star\star$) automatically: each $a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i}$ gives $L_i (N + a_i) \equiv 0 \pmod L$, since $N + a_i \equiv 0 \pmod{e_i}$ and $L_i (N + a_i) = L \cdot (N + a_i)/e_i \in \mathbb{Z}$. Summing four such terms gives $0$ mod $L$. So ($\star\star$) holds.

Hence $(D3)_{\mathrm{pwc}}(N) \le \tau(N^2+1)^4 = N^{o(1)}$. $\square$

## 5. Consequences

We partition the HE-4 quadruples $((e_i, a_i))_{i=1}^4 \in \mathcal{T}(N)^4$ satisfying $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$ into three disjoint strata:
- $S_1$ (label-pairing): there exists a bijection $\sigma \in \mathrm{Sym}(\{3, 4\})$ with $(e_i, a_i) = (e_{\sigma(i)+2}, a_{\sigma(i)+2})$ for $i = 1, 2$ (i.e., $\{(e_1, a_1), (e_2, a_2)\} = \{(e_3, a_3), (e_4, a_4)\}$ as multi-sets of labels).
- $S_2$ (frequency-pairing without label-pairing): not in $S_1$, but $\{\beta_1, \beta_2\} = \{\beta_3, \beta_4\}$ as multi-sets in $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$.
- $S_3$ (genuine 4-term): $\beta_1 + \beta_2 \equiv \beta_3 + \beta_4 \pmod 1$ but $\{\beta_1, \beta_2\} \ne \{\beta_3, \beta_4\}$ as multi-sets in $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$.

Then $\mathsf{E}_4(N) = |S_1| + |S_2| + |S_3|$.

**Corollary 4 ($|S_1|$ exact, $|S_2|$ bounded).**
(a) $|S_1| = 2 K(N)^2 - K(N)$.
(b) $|S_2| \le 4 \cdot (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) \cdot K(N) + 2 \cdot (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N)^2 = O(N^{2 + o(1)})$.

*Proof.*
(a) $S_1$ consists of $(\beta_1 = \beta_3 \text{-as-labels} \text{ and } \beta_2 = \beta_4 \text{-as-labels})$ plus $(\beta_1 = \beta_4, \beta_2 = \beta_3)$. Each branch gives $K^2$ tuples; the overlap (both branches) is the diagonal $(e_1, a_1) = (e_2, a_2) = (e_3, a_3) = (e_4, a_4)$, contributing $K$ tuples. Inclusion-exclusion: $|S_1| = 2 K^2 - K$.

(b) Define for any value $\beta \in \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$ the count $r(\beta) := \#\{(e, a) \in \mathcal{T}(N) : \beta_{e,a} = \beta\}$. Then $\sum_\beta r(\beta) = K$ and $\sum_\beta r(\beta)^2 = K + (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N)$ (the $K$ accounting for $(e, a) = (e', a')$ identical-label pairs, the $(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}$ for distinct-label same-frequency pairs).

Each $S_2$ quadruple is of one of two types (factor 2 from swapping positions 3, 4):

Type A: $\beta_1 = \beta_3$ as values *and* $\beta_2 = \beta_4$ as values, but the labels do not match $S_1$'s pattern. The total Type A count (including $S_1$ overlap) is $\sum_\beta r(\beta)^2 \cdot \sum_{\beta'} r(\beta')^2 = (K + (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}})^2$. Subtracting the $S_1$-pattern-1 contribution $K^2$ leaves Type A excess $\le (K + (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}})^2 - K^2 \le 2 K (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}} + (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}^2$.

Type B (positions 3-4 swapped): symmetric, same bound.

Total: $|S_2| \le 4 K (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N) + 2 (D2)_{\mathrm{ord}}(N)^2$. With $(D2)_{\mathrm{ord}} = N^{1+o(1)}$ and $K \asymp N \log^{O(1)} N$: $|S_2| = O(N^{2+o(1)})$. $\square$

**Corollary 5 ($S_3$ is supported on non-pairwise-coprime tuples).** Every $S_3$-quadruple has at least one pair $(i, j) \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}^2$ with $i \ne j$ and $\gcd(e_i, e_j) > 1$.

*Proof.* Suppose $((e_i, a_i)) \in S_3$ has pairwise-coprime $e_i$. By Theorem 3's argument, $e_i \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i}$ for every $i$. Then $N + a_i \equiv 0 \pmod{e_i}$, so $\beta_i = (N + a_i)/e_i$ is an integer, i.e., $\beta_i \equiv 0 \pmod 1$ for every $i$. Hence $\{\beta_1, \beta_2\} = \{0, 0\} = \{\beta_3, \beta_4\}$ as multi-sets in $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$, so the tuple lies in $S_1 \cup S_2$, contradicting $S_3$-membership. $\square$

**Corollary 6 (HE-4 reduces to a single named residual).** Let $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) := |S_3|(N)$ (which by Corollary 5 is supported on non-pairwise-coprime tuples). Then:
$$
\mathsf{E}_4(N) \;=\; \bigl(2 K(N)^2 - K(N)\bigr) + |S_2| + |S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}| \;=\; 2 K(N)^2 + O(N^{2+o(1)}) + |S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|.
$$
The HE-4 conjecture $\mathsf{E}_4(N) \le C K(N)^2 \log^B N$ is therefore equivalent to
$$
|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|(N) \;\le\; C' K(N)^2 \log^B N
$$
for some absolute constant $C'$.

*Proof.* By the partition $\mathsf{E}_4 = |S_1| + |S_2| + |S_3|$, and Corollary 4(a), Corollary 4(b), the first two terms are $(2K^2 - K) + O(N^{2+o(1)}) = 2K^2 + O(N^{2+o(1)})$ (using $K \le O(N \log^{O(1)} N)$). So the HE-4 bound is equivalent to the asserted $|S_3^{\mathrm{hard}}|$ bound. $\square$

**Remark.** Theorem 3 still has direct content even though $S_3^{\mathrm{pwc}} = \emptyset$: it bounds the *total* count of pairwise-coprime equation-satisfying tuples (across all of $S_1, S_2, S_3$) by $\tau(N^2+1)^4$. This in particular controls a sub-stratum of $|S_2|$ — the "all-zero-frequency" tuples — independently of the (D2) bound. The structural take-away of Theorem 3 + Corollary 5 is: the genuinely hard part of HE-4 ($S_3$) is supported entirely on tuples with at least one shared prime among the $e_i$.

## 6. The residual hard case — open chunk P16.1.next

**Setup.** Let $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}((e_i)) := \{p : |S_p((e_i))| \ge 2\}$ where $S_p((e_i)) := \{i \in \{1,2,3,4\} : p \mid e_i\}$. The pairwise-coprime case has $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2} = \emptyset$; the hard case is $\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2} \ne \emptyset$ AND not all primes of $\bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(e_i)$ satisfy the "$|S_p| = 1 \Rightarrow p \mid N^2+1$" forcing from Theorem 3.

**Why it is hard.** When $|S_p| \ge 2$ for some prime $p$, the equation ($\star\star$) mod $p$ involves $\ge 2$ free $\eta$-signs in the sub-system
$$
\sum_{i \in S_p} (L/e_i) (N + \eta_i r_p) \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod p,
$$
which generically has solutions $(\eta_i)_{i \in S_p}$ without forcing $p \mid N^2 + 1$. The freedom grows with $|S_p|$, and the joint mod-$p$ constraint over all $p \in \mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}$ couples the tuples.

**Next-chunk plan.**

- **P16.1.a.** Refine Theorem 3 to: in $(D3)_{\mathrm{hard}}$, the "$|S_p| = 1$ part" of each $e_i$ still divides $N^2 + 1$. Specifically, define $e_i^{(1)} := \prod_{p \in \mathrm{supp}(e_i): \,|S_p|=1} p$ (the "unique-to-$i$ primes"). Then $e_i^{(1)} \mid N^2 + 1$ and $a_i \equiv -N \pmod{e_i^{(1)}}$. So $(D3)_{\mathrm{hard}}$ tuples decompose as $(e_i^{(1)}) \times (e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ with the former constrained to $N^2+1$-divisors and the latter being the residual structure.

- **P16.1.b.** Bound the count of $(e_i^{(\ge 2)})$ in $(D3)_{\mathrm{hard}}$ via divisor-sum reasoning on the shared-prime structure. Conjecturally: $\le K^2 \log^B N$. The most natural approach is to fix the partition of $\bigcup_i \mathrm{supp}(e_i)$ by $|S_p|$-class, then apply standard divisor-sum bounds within each class. Heath-Brown / Hooley-style identities (e.g., $\tau_3$ moments) are likely candidates.

- **P16.1.c.** Estimate the contribution of (D3)$_{\mathrm{hard}}$ tuples with $|\mathfrak{P}_{\ge 2}| = 1$ (the "minimally shared" case) separately, since this case admits clean parameterization: one shared prime $p_0$ with $|S_{p_0}| = 2, 3,$ or $4$; the rest of each $e_i$ is unique.
